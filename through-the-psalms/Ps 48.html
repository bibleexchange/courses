 Ps 48:14 For this God is our God for ever and ever: he will be our guide even unto death.

  1. We believe, in God the Father, who made us and all mankind,
    1. Who created all things, and for whose pleasure they are and were created.
    2. God has not left Himself without witness among us.
      1. In volume after volume He has spoken to us.
      2. In voice after voice He has made known His will
      3. by His works which are all around us in the universe wherein we live;
      4. by His word which He inspired into holy men of old;
      5. by that conscience which is the lamp lit by the Spirit in every soul of man;
      6. by history, which is the record of His dealing with nations;
      7. by His experience, which is the pattern woven by His own hand in the web of our lives.
      8. By these we all may know Him.
      9. They teach us that He is perfect, awful, holy; that He is of purer eyes than to behold iniquity.
      10. But when we think of God only as the Creator, there is something in this thought which inevitably amazes us.
  2. We believe in God the Son
    1. God, then, pointing us to Christ,
    2. God reveals to us the mystery of our redemption,
    3.  For us there is no longer a God in the rushing fire, or destroying earthquake, or roaring wind; but the Divine temple of God was the human body of His Son, and even for rebels and for sinners "God is in Christ, reconciling the world unto Himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them."

2Co 5:19 To wit, that God was in Christ, reconciling the world unto himself, not imputing their trespasses unto them; and hath committed unto us the word of reconciliation.

  1. We believe in God the Holy Ghost
    1. The spiritual presence of the Comforter was nearer, more powerful, more blessed, than even the physical presence.
    2. God had been with them, but it was better for them that He should be in them.
      1. The Father, who made,
      2. the Son, who redeemed,
      3. the Holy Ghost, who sanctified and who liveth in the temple of our heart
      4. this God is our God for ever and ever; He shall be our Guide unto death."
    1. Who is this God that is emphatically designated and claimed as our God? (1) He is a known God. We are not left to frame a God for ourselves;
      1. He is revealed to us in the Bible
        1. In the person and work of Jesus Christ, God, not only as our Creator, but as our loving Father and our Saviour and Sanctifier.
      2. Our God is a covenant God.
        1. We live under a new and better covenant.
          1. The two great provisions of this covenant are:
            1.  that God will write His laws in our hearts, and that He will put them within us;
            2. "Your sins and iniquities will I remember no more.
      3. This God, called "our God," is a tried God.
        1. During all the ages of the world's and the Church's history,
        2. He has been put to the test by countless multitudes of those who have trusted in Him,
      4. God is called our Guide.
        1. He is our Guide into the truth.

    Joh 16:13 Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come.

    (2) God is our Guide in making our way clear before our face. Seek His blessing, and He will guide you even unto that hour to which this text refers you—the last. "Thou shalt guide me with Thy counsel and afterward receive me to glory."

     

    One event, which corresponds point for point to the details of this Psalm—

    The crushing destruction of the Assyrian army under Sennacherib.

    We may regard it as the hymn of triumph over the baffled Assyrian and the marvellous deliverance of Israel by the arm of God.

     

    The Psalm falls into three portions.

    1. There is the glory of Zion,
    2. The deliverance of Zion
    3. The consequent grateful praise and glad trust of Zion.
    1. The glory of Zion. The Jew's pride in Jerusalem was a different thing altogether from the Roman's pride in Rome. The one thing that gave it glory was that in it God abode.

The name even of the earthly Zion was "Jehovah-Shammah"—"The Lord is there." We are not spiritualizing or forcing a New Testament meaning into these words when we see in them an eternal truth. Zion is where hearts love, and trust, and follow Christ. The "city of the great King" is a permanent reality in a partial form upon earth, and that partial form is itself a prophecy of the perfection of the heavens.

  1. The deliverance of Zion.
    1. Mark the dramatic deliverance.
    2. Mark the silence
    3. There is no resistance.
      1. An unseen hand smites once; and when the morning dawned, "they were all dead corpses."

The silence magnifies the deliverance.

    1. Mark The deliverance "God will establish it for ever."
  • The grateful praise and glad trust of Zion.
      1. The deliverance deepens the glad meditation on God's favour and defence.
      2. It spreads His fame throughout the world.
      3. It produces in Zion, the mother city, and her daughter villages, a triumph of rapture and gladness. The last verses set forth the height and perfectness of the confidence which the manifold mercies of God ought to produce in men's hearts.
