Be Merciful: have a forgiving spirit and a compassionate heart.

It is being benevolent. It is forgiving those who are wrong, It is empathy;                 it is getting right inside the person and feeling right along with him.                                It is a deliberate effort, an act of the will to understand the person and to meet his need by forgiving and showing mercy.                                                                 It is the opposite of being hard, unforgiving, and unfeeling. God forgives only those who forgive others. A person receives mercy only if he is merciful .

Mt 6:12 And forgive us our debts, as we forgive our debtors.                                      Jas 2:13 For he shall have judgment without mercy, that hath shewed no mercy; and mercy rejoiceth against judgment.

  1. The person who is merciful has a tender heart
    1. A heart that cares for all who have need,
      1. The seen and unseen.
      2. If he sees the needful, he feels for them and reaches out to do all he can.
      3. If he does not see them, he feels and reaches out through prayer and giving as opportunity arises.
    2. The merciful do not hoard or hold back help, no matter the cost.
    3. They have the love of God dwelling in them.

1Jo 3:17 But whoso hath this world's good, and seeth his brother have need, and shutteth up his bowels of compassion from him, how dwelleth the love of God in him?                                                                                                          Jas 2:15 If a brother or sister be naked, and destitute of daily food,            16 And one of you say unto them, Depart in peace, be ye warmed and filled; notwithstanding ye give them not those things which are needful to the body; what doth it profit? 

    1. They know that it is "more blessed to give than to receive."

 

Ac 20:35 I have shewed you all things, how that so labouring ye ought to support the weak, and to remember the words of the Lord Jesus, how he said, It is more blessed to give than to receive.

2.  Every believer can be merciful. Some may not have money or other means to help, but they can be tender and compassionate and demonstrate mercy through kind expressions and prayer. God instructs the believer to be merciful. He charges the believer to do some very practical things:

a.  "Deal...bread to the hungry" (Isaiah 58:7; James 2:15).

b.  "Bring the poor that are cast out to thy house" (Isaiah 58:7).

c.  "Cover him [the naked]" (Isaiah 58:7; James 2:15).

d.  Strengthen and comfort the broken and grieving soul

Job 16:5 But I would strengthen you with my mouth, and the moving of my lips should assuage your grief.

 

e.  Pity the afflicted

Job 6:14 To him that is afflicted pity should be shewed from his friend; but he forsaketh the fear of the Almighty.

Jas 1:27 Pure religion and undefiled before God and the Father is this, To visit the fatherless and widows in their affliction, and to keep himself unspotted from the world.

 

f.  Bear the burdens of others—even to the point of restoring them when they sin. But we reach out to them in a spirit of meekness.

Ga 6:1 Brethren, if a man be overtaken in a fault, ye which are spiritual, restore such an one in the spirit of meekness; considering thyself, lest thou also be tempted.

 2 Bear ye one another's burdens, and so fulfil the law of Christ.

 

g.  Support the weak
