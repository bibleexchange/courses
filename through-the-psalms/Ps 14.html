    Ex 35:30 ¶ And Moses said unto the children of Israel, See, the LORD hath called by name Bezaleel the son of Uri, the son of Hur, of the tribe of Judah; 34 And he hath put in his heart that he may teach, both he, and Aholiab, the son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan.

     

    God called two men, (Bezaleel and Aholiab) who were willing to work the task at hand and not be swayed or corrupted by their own ideas. God called two men that He could use to teach others (the power of training others)

    1.  There was the general superintendent named Bezalel (Exodus 35:30).  Bezalel  had special qualifications, the qualifications needed to build the Tabernacle:

    a.  God filled him with the Holy Spirit (Exodus 35:31).

    b.  God filled him with intelligence, knowledge, and skill in construction

    Ex 35:31 And he hath filled him with the spirit of God, in wisdom, in understanding, and in knowledge, and in all manner of workmanship;

    Bezalel was able to design metal objects

    Ex 35:32 And to devise curious works, to work in gold, and in silver, and in brass,

    Bezalel was able to cut and set precious stones

    Ex 35:33 And in the cutting of stones, to set them, and in carving of wood, to make any manner of cunning work.

     

    Bezalel was gifted by God in woodworking and in all kinds of craftsmanship (Exodus 35:33).

    Bezalel was chosen and set apart by God for the most important building project in Israel's history. God chose a man and gave him the necessary skills to build the Tabernacle exactly as it was designed.

    2.  There was the associate superintendent named Oholiab

    He willingly served as a support to Bezalel.

    Ex 35:34 And he hath put in his heart that he may teach, both he, and Aholiab, the son of Ahisamach, of the tribe of Dan.

     

    Oholiab had no desire to usurp Bezalel nor question his authority.

    Oholiab was committed to God, committed to using his gifts to the fullest of his ability, committed to the building of the Tabernacle.

    a. Bezalel and Oholiab were both equipped by God to teach others.

    b. Both were filled by the Holy Spirit of God, filled with skill to do all kinds of work.

    c.  They were skilled to work as craftsmen, designers, embroiderers, and weavers.

    d.  They were skilled as master craftsmen and designers.

    God had everything and everyone in the right place. He had given Moses the blueprint, the pattern and design. He had called the people to give and they had responded. He had called the two supervisors to come and lead the way. The only thing that remained was the actual construction itself. The Tabernacle was soon to become a reality.
