What a proof is this that the best believers may fall into unbelief, and trust for a while in an arm of flesh to save them.

Asa was for a while relieved from fear by turning to Syria for help, and therefore no doubt he thought himself right, but we greatly err if we estimate the correctness of our actions by their result.  

The Lord was angry with Asa, and as a chastisement his life was a troubled one from that day forward.
