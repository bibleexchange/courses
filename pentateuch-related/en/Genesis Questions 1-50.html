1.       Q: The Firmament was what?                                                                                                              

          A: Heaven (Genesis 1:6-8)

 

2.       Q: How was the garden refreshed before man was placed there to tend to it?

          A: By a mist from the earth (Genesis 2:6)

 

3.       Q: For whose sake was the ground cursed?

          A: For man’s sake (Genesis 3:17)

 

4.       Q: When did men begin to call upon the name of the Lord again?

          A: When Seth bore a son Enos (Genesis 4:26)

 

5.       Q: How old was Adam when he died?

          A: 930 years old (Genesis 5:5)

 

6.       Q: What family was the only family found faithful?

          A: Noah’s family (Genesis 6:8)

 

7.       Q: After it rained forty days and forty nights, how long did it continue to rain?

          A: One hundred and fifty days. (Genesis 7:24)

 

8.       Q: What was Noah’s first act when he came forth from the ark?

          A: He built an alter, and offered a sacrifice (Genesis 8:20)

 

9.       Q: Noah put a curse on one of his sons because one of them had seen his nakedness.

          A: Which son was it? It was his grandson Canaan, Son of Ham (Genesis 9:18-26)

 

10.     Q: Nimrod was a mighty hunter before who?

          A: The Lord (Genesis 10:9)

 

11.     Q: What was the name of Abram’s father?

          A: Terah (Genesis 11:26)

 

12.     Q: What relation was Lot to Abram?

          A: His nephew (Genesis 12:4-5)

 

13.     Q: Was Abram a rich or a poor man?

          A: Rich (Genesis 13:2)

 

14.     Q: Where did Lot choose to pitch his tent?

          A: Towards Sodom (Genesis 14:8-12)

 

15.     Q: To what did God compare the number of his seed?

          A: As the number of the stars (Genesis 15:5)

 

 

16.     Q: Ishmael was the son of who?

          A: Hager (Genesis 16:3-4)

 

17.     Q: Abram showed his love to Ishmael how?

          A: He prayed for him the he might live before God, or be favored by him

                                                                                                    (Genesis 17:18)

 

18.     Q: How did Sarah displease the Lord?

          A: She laughed at his message (Genesis 18:12)

 

19.     Q: Moab was Lot’s?

          A: Son and Grandson (Genesis 19:36-37)

 

20.     Q: Was Sarah related to Abraham?

          A: Yes, she was in fact Abraham’s half-sister. (Genesis 20:12)

22.     Q: Who spoke to Abraham, telling him to do no harm to his son?

          A: (Genesis 22:11) …and the angel of the Lord called unto him out of

                    heaven…

                    It is sometimes a common conception that God spoke to him not to do his son

                    any harm. But according to the scriptures an angel of the Lord spoke to

                    Abraham.

 

23.     Q: When Abraham had to obtain possession of that burying place from Ephron the

               Hittite it showed what?

          A: That Abraham had no land of his own (Genesis 23:4)

 

 

 

 

24      Q: What did Eliezar give to his young master’s intended bride?

          A: Golden earrings, bracelets, jewels of silver and gold, and raiment, according to

               the custom of those lands and times. (Genesis 24:22, 30, 53)

 

25.     Q: Whom did Abraham marry after Sarah’s death?

          A: Keturah (Genesis 25:1)

 

26.     Q: Whom did Esau choose as his wives?

          A: Judith and Bashemath, Hittites (Genesis 26:34)

 

27.     Q: Who was the second born son of Issac?

          A: Jacob (Genesis 27:1-4)

 

28.     Q: What remarkable thing occurred to Jacob on his way to Syria?

          A: He had a vision by night, and renewal of God’s promise and blessing.

                                                                                           (Genesis 28:10-15)

 

29.     Q: Whom did Jacob first see when he got to Syria?

          A: The servants of Laban, and then Rachel his daughter. (Genesis 29:3-5, 9)

 

30.     Q: How many years did Jacob serve Laban before he allowed him to have any cattle

               for his wages?

          A: Fourteen years (Genesis 30:26-30)

 

31.     Q: What wicked act was Rachel guilty of?

          A: Of stealing her father’s images. (Genesis 31:19)

 

32.     Q: What name did Jacob give to the place where this occurred?

          A: Peniel, or “the face of God” (Genesis 32:30)

 

33.     Q: How made was Esau when he finally saw Jacob?

          A: He was not made at all, but rather ran to him and wept and kissed him.

                                                                                                   (Genesis 33:4)

 

34.     Q: Jacob’s sons took vengeance on the Hivites, for the rape of their sister Dinah by?

          A: slaughtering them, they toke them by surprise while they were sore from      

               circumcision (Genesis 34:13-26)

 

35.     Q: Jacob’s firstborn was?

          A: Reuben (Genesis 35:23)

 

36.     Q: Why did Jacob and Esau depart?

          A: For their riches were more than that they might dwell together; and the land

               wherein they were strangers could not bear them because of their cattle

                                                                                               (Genesis 36:7)

37.     Q: The two brothers of Joseph that persuaded the others not to kill him were son’s of

               which of Jacob’s wives.

          A: His first wife, Leah (Genesis 37:21-22, 26-27, 29:32, 35)

 

38.     Q: What were the names of the twins that Tamar bore?

          A:  Pharez and Zarah (Genesis 38:29, 30)

 

39.     Q: To whom did the Ishmaelites sell Joseph?

          A: To Potiphar, captain of Pharaoh’s guard. (Genesis 39:1)

 

40.     Q: What two officers was Pharaoh made with?

          A: The chief butler and the chief baker ( Genesis 40:1, 2)

 

41.     Q: How many son’s did Joseph bear?

          A: two (Genesis 41:45, 51, 52)

 

42.     Q: How did Joseph behave to his brethren when he was with them?

          A: He made himself strange to them and spoke roughly to them, but not from a spirit

               of revenge. (Genesis 42:7)

 

43.     Q: How did Joseph feel when he saw Benjamin stand among his brethren?

          A: His love was greatly excited, and he retired and wept (Genesis 43:30, 31)

 

44.     Q: What brother offered himself in exchange for Benjamin?

          A: Judah (Genesis 44:18, 33)

 

45.     Q: How did all of Joseph’s brothers feel when they knew this mighty prince was

               their own brother?

          A: They could not answer him for fear (Genesis 45:3)

 

46.     Q: Was it the will of God for Israel (Jacob) to go down into Egypt?

          A: Yes it was, God had spoken to him for him to go (Genesis 46:3)

 

47.     Q: What was Israel’s request at his time of death?

          A: That he be not buried in the land of Egypt but with his fathers, in their burying  

               place (Genesis 47:30, 31)

 

48:     Q: What peculiar blessing did Jacob give to the sons of Joseph?

          A: He placed his right hand on the younger instead of the elder and blessed as the

               greater (Genesis 48:17-19)

 

49.     Q: What was the special blessing of Judah?

          A: That out of his family the Messiah should come (Genesis 49:10)

 

50.     Q: Where was Jacob buried?

          A: In the cave of Machpelsh (Genesis 50:13)
