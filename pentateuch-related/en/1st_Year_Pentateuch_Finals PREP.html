 Section 5 – Deuteronomy

  1. Explain the title of this book.

The name of this book reveals its purpose: “deuter” meaning “second” and “nomos” meaning “law.” It is a repetition of the laws of Sinai. (17:18) The title of this book is rendered “copy” in 17:18, and means “duplicate.”

  1. Write the key verse to this book and its reference.

“I call heaven and earth to record this day against you, that I have set before you life and death, blessing and cursing: therefore choose life, that both thou and thy seed may live:” (30:19)

  1. Why was this book of ‘reviews’, ‘remembrance’, and ‘repetition’ even necessary?

The old generation that had left Egypt died by the way, except for Joshua and Caleb (1:36; Num. 32:12), and a new generation must have the old-time laws rehearsed in their hearing.

  1. What is repeatedly given has a motive for Israel’s obedience?

The remembrance of deliverance from Egypt is constantly urged as a motive to obedience. (5:15; 10:12,13)1

  1. Moses admonishes in Deuteronomy to ‘remember especially’ something. What was it? Give reference.
