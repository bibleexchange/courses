I Corinthians

 

I. Writer  

 

“Paul, called to be an Apostle of Jesus Christ through the will of God.”

 

I Cor. 1:1

 

II. The Purpose of Writing

 

1. “To the Church of God which is at Corinth”

1:2

 

“To them that are sanctified”

1:2

 

“Called to be saints”

1:2

 

“All that in every place call upon the name of Jesus Christ our Lord”

1:2

 

Paul intended for the letter to circulate beyond this church.

 

2. In Paul’s day, Corinth was the leading commercial center

of Greece.

 

One of the most important cities in the Roman Empire.

 

No other city held such a commanding position in Ancient Greece.

 

It’s location on the Isthmus of Corinth, the narrow strip of land that was considered as a commercial bridge between the North and the South allows it to control all travelling merchants.

 

There was a harbor on the west side and the east side giving it the advantage not only commercially but also a leading naval power of the world.

 

 

 

3. Corinth was a Grecian city noted as a sports center.

I Cor. 9:24-27

 

Populous (500,000 – 700,000) to go with its wealth.

 

The first athletes were attracted to the Isthmian games celebrated near the city.

 

Paul addressed athletes many times.

 

4. A cosmopolitan place, intellectually alert, materially prosperous, but morally corrupt.

 

The ideal of the Corinthian was the reckless development of the individual.

 

The man of pleasure and leisure, proud of physical strength.

 

The Corinthians desire was his law.

 

5. A land of idolatry and prostitution.

 

The Greek goddess – Aphrodite (love and beauty)

 

6. Corinth was a prestigious center from which the Gospel could spread out to the surrounding districts.

 

Anything preached in Corinth was sure to spread.

 

Paul looked for strategic places to preach the Gospel.

 

Matt. 28:18-20 Acts 18:1-11

 

7. To answer questions the Corinthians had concerning problems in the Church.

 

7:1 8:1 12:1 16:1

 

These problems are common today in one form or another.

 

8. To reprove contentious arguments.

I Cor. 1:11-13

 

9. To defend his apostleship and ministry.

I Cor. 9:1-27

 

10. The spirit of the city of Corinth was getting into the Church.

 

The church today faces the same problems.

 

Carnality and immaturity instead of growth under the Lordship of Christ.

 

a. Some of the problems in Corinth

 

(1) Intellectual pride

 

Corinth was the rival of Athens.

The Greeks were proud of their language, literature

and learning.

Paul prepared this epistle to meet the Greek mind.

He begins by showing the foolishness of human

wisdom.

 

I Cor. 1:20,21; 2:14; 3:19

 

Paul renounced human wisdom from his ministry.

I Cor. 1:17; 2:1

 

(2) Addiction to immorality

I Cor. 5:1-11;  6:15-18

 

(3) Drunkenness

I Cor. 11:21

 

(4) Carnality

I Cor. 3:1-3

 

(5) Lack of Brotherly Love

I Cor. 6:1-8

 

(6) The church existing in the atmosphere of luxury and licentiousness

 

(7) Torn by Contention and Divisions

I Cor. 3:4; 16:12

 

(8) Lax in it’s Discipline

 

 

 

III. Theme

 

Cleansing the church from false conceptions.

Christian life and conduct are top priority with Paul.
