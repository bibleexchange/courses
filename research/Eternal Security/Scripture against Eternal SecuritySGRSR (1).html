Scripture against Eternal Security

 

 

Scripture references that denounce the doctrine of “Eternal Security”, or “Once Saved, Always Saved”, which states once a person is saved they cannot lose their salvation.

 

 

 

Exodus 32:33

And the Lord said unto Moses, whosoever hath sinned against me, him will

I blot out of my book.

 

 

Psalm 69:28

Let them be blotted out of the book of the living, and not be written with the righteous.

 

 

Jesus speaks to the seven churches of Asia in Rev. Chapters 2 & 3

At the end of every message he makes wonderful promises to those within each church

That are overcomers (prevails over the world, devil , and the flesh).

 

 

Rev. 3:5

To the church in Sardis; He that overcometh, I will not blot his name our of the book of life.

 

 

Rev. 22:19

If any man shall take away from the words of the book of this prophecy, God shall take away his part out of the book of life.

 

 

II Peter 1:10 & 11

Brethren give diligence to make your calling and election sure; for if ye do these things, ye shall never fall.

 

 

Matt. 22

It looks like this man had always assumed he was saved.  He was carelessly presumptuous.  It would indicate in that he was speechless that the words of the king was totally unexpected by him.

 

 

I Cor. 9:27

Paul said he had to keep his body under subjection to God’ lest after preaching to others, he should be a castaway.

 

 

Matt. 7:21

Jesus said; not all that say Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven, but he that doeth!!!  The will of the father.

 

 

I John 2:4

He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a liar, and the truth is not in him.  Verse 15  Love not the world, neither the things that are in the world, if any man love the world, the love of the father is not in him.

 

 

Jude verse 3

Beloved, when I gave diligence to write unto you of the common salvation, it was needful for me to write unto you, and exhort you that ye should earnestly contend!  For the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.

 

Verse 5; I will therefore put you in remembrance, though ye once knew this, how that the Lord, having saved the people out of the land of Egypt, afterward destroyed them that believed not.

 

 

Heb. 2:1-3

How shall we escape, if we neglect so great salvation?

 

 

Heb. 3:6

But Christ as a son over his own house; whose house are we, if we hold fast the confidence and the rejoicing of the hope firm unto the end!!

 

 

Heb. 3:14

For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our confidence steadfast unto the end!!

 

 

Matt. 10:22

Jesus said; He that endureth to the end shall be saved.

 

 

 

Heb. 3:15-19

God speaks of the people he delivered out of Egypt, and how they died in the wilderness and could not enter into his rest because of their sins.  And then God says; so we see that they could not enter because of unbelief.

Do you really think after they had seen the miracles in Egypt and the crossing of the Red Sea, the cloud by day, the pillar of fire by night to lead them, they actually stopped believing there was a God?  No, but they stopped trusting Him, they were no longer committed to Him.  They went their own way.  God calls that unbelief!

 

 

Heb. 4:1

Let us therefore fear, lest, a promise being left us of entering into his rest, any of you should seem to come short of it.

 

Verse 11

Let us labor therefore to enter into that rest, lest any man fall after the same example of unbelief.

 

Verse 14

Let us hold fast our profession

 

 

Heb. 5:9

And being made perfect, he became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him!

 

 

Acts 13:43

Paul is persuading people to continue in the Grace of God.

 

 

Luke 12:42-48

Blessed are those servants whom the Lord when he cometh shall find watching and doing.  But and if that servant is not living right or contending for the faith when the Lord comes, he will appoint him his portion with unbelievers.

 

Notice the believer is cast away with the unbeliever.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

James 2

Faith without works is dead, the devil believes in God and knew who Jesus was.  The devil has knowledge of the plan of Salvation, but he is still damned to hell.

As the body without the spirit is dead, so faith without works in dead also.  Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he offered up Isaac his son on the altar?  Seest thou how faith wrought with his works, and be works faith was made perfect.

Real faith produces obedience to God.  The fruit of our faith is not self-righteousness but the righteousness of Christ in us, which is wrought by faith.

 

 

The Bible says Noah found grace in the eyes of God

 

God’s grace made a way for Noah to be saved.  Noah’s faith produced obedience to God’s word and in the end he was saved.

Noah was saved by Grace through Faith.  If noah began to doubt after all those years of faithfully working on the ark, and never finished it, would he have been saved?  No; but he continued or kept the faith all the way to the end.

 

 

Gal. 5:19-21

Here we find a list of the works of the flesh.  Verse 21 says; they which do such things shall not inherit the kingdom of God.

 

 

Eph. 5:5 & 6

No unclean person hath any inheritance in the kingdom of Christ.

Let no man deceive you, the wrath of God cometh upon the children of disobedience

 

 

I Cor. 6:9 & 10

The unrighteous shall not inherit the kingdom of God.

 

The doctrine of once saved, always saved is the oldest lie in the world.  

The devil told Eve; go ahead, you can disobey God, you won’t die.

 

Rom. 6:16

If we are controlled by sin, then we are servants of sin.

If we are controlled by God, then we are servants of God.

 

Mt 10:22

And ye shall be hated of all men for my name's sake: but he that endureth to the end shall be saved.

We believe in the perseverance of the saints.
