Marriage, Divorce, Remarriage

 

 

I. God's Will Concerning Marriage -

Matt. 19:4-6 And he answered and said unto them, Have ye not read, that he which made them at the beginning made them male and female, 5 And said, For this cause shall a man leave father and mother, and shall cleave to his wife: and they twain shall be one flesh? 6 Wherefore they are no more twain, but one flesh. What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder.

 

Genesis 2:24 Therefore shall a man leave his father and his mother, and shall cleave unto his wife: and they shall be one flesh.

 

  • In marriage man (cleaves to his wife) – a mental and emotional union.
  • In marriage, Man and Woman become one flesh.—Body, Mind, and Soul
  • This is God's will concerning marriage.
  • It is takes God to join together a spiritual union.

 

1. Man is commanded not to divorce his wife -

The burden is on the man not to put away (divorce) his wife.

 

Luke 16:18  Whosoever putteth away his wife, and marrieth another, committeth adultery:  and whosoever marrieth her that is put away from her husband committeth adultery.

Mark 10:11

I Cor. 7:10

 

2. The one cause for divorce -

Matt. 19:9 "Except it be for fornication"

Jesus uses the two different terms in this passage - "fornication" and "adultery".

Each term has a specific meaning which follows through the scriptures, and appears to be determined by the following rules.

(1) A married person is never said to commit fornication.

(2) An unmarried person is never said to commit adultery.

Adultery - a married person

Fornication - an unmarried person

Deut. 22:22 Adultery Deut. 27:28 Fornication

Lev. 20:10 Hosea 3:13-14 Heb. 13:4

There are different terms used all from the same Hebrew word - "Zanah"

Whore - harlot - fornication

Gen. 34:31; 38:24 Josh. 6:17 I Kings 3:16

Hosea 4:13-14 I Cor. 7:2 Heb. 13:4

Also, the fact that both "fornication" and "adultery" occur together four times in separate lists of sins, shows conclusively that there is a specific distinction between them. Matt. 15:19 Mark 7:21 I Cor. 6:9 Gal. 5:19

If "fornication" is never used in other places of immorality by the married, when their marital status is in view (as it obviously is in the two occurrences of the exception clause); then logically it is not used there.  In which case, the immorality for which Christ allowed divorce would only be premarital, referred to in Deut. 22:13-21.Matt. 19:8-9

3. Jesus' reply to the Pharisees in Matthew 19 covered three main topics -

(1) The rule of permanence of the marriage union - Matt. 19:4-6

(2) The rule of exception, which by its very nature posed a

situation which could only be known after the woman had

actually been intimate as a wife - or shown to be with child.

Deut. 22:13-21

(3) Jesus gives recognition to this possibility, but at the same time making it clear that adultery is not a legitimate cause for divorce in His Kingdom amongst His saints.

Believers should be able to work out their problems.

4. Four reasons Jesus gave no approval of divorce for those on the spiritual level of His kingdom - born- again Believers  -

As Moses did for "the hardness of your hearts” Matt. 19:8 referring to Deut. 24:1

- After conversion -

(1) Responsibility - We have, as Believers, a responsibility to walk in the light of God's Word.

(2) Resources - We have the availability of spiritual means in dealing with marriage problems on the kingdom level, "what therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder."  Matt. 19:6

God made no provision in the beginning for divorce Matt. 19:8  "From the beginning it was not so."

(3) Reconciliation - It is far better to forgive for immorality of any

nature, and seek reconciliation, than to destroy the marriage union.

Mark 11:25-26  I Cor. 7:11

(4) Recognition - The instruction to the Church to "purge out the old leaven of sin" leaves no place among Believers for any divorce provision, so long as they continue in the Church.

Matt. 18:15-17  I Cor. 5:7-11

The Churches authority to judge matters must be recognized by the Believers.  God has given this authority to the Church.  

I Cor. 6:1-3

II. God's Will Concerning Divorce -

Matt. 2:16 "God...hates putting away" (divorce)

Matt. 19:8 "Moses because of the hardness of your hearts suffered you to put away your wives:  but from the beginning it was not so."

The problem was with the heart.

"Suffered" - "Epitrepo" - Jesus used this same word in Luke 8:32

When He suffered the evil spirits to enter into the swine.  

Permitted - gave leave

"Hardness of heart" - "sklerokardia" - destitute of (spiritual) perception -

This word was used by Jesus in Mark 16:14 "Afterward He appeared unto the eleven as they sat at meat, and upbraided them with their unbelief and hardness of heart; because they believed not them which had seen him after He was risen."

Malachi said twice - "take heed to your spirit" Chpt. 2:15-16

 

1. The woman is bound to her husband -

Rom. 7:2,3  I Cor. 7:39  Eph. 5:23

If she leaves the man has no power to stop her.  I Cor. 7:15

The burden is on putting away (divorce).

Not on one that leaves.

I Cor. 7:11 "But and if she depart (divorce), let her remain unmarried, or be reconciled to her husband:  and let not the husband put away (divorce) his wife."

 

2. The woman that leaves her husband -

If she marries again she lives in adultery.

Matt. 5:32  Mark 10:12  Luke 16:18  Rom. 7:3

3. The man that divorces his wife -

Matt. 19:8  "Moses because of the hardness of your hearts suffered you to put away (divorce) your wives:  but from the beginning it was not so."

vs. 9  "And I say unto you, whosoever shall put away his wife, except it be for fornication, and shall marry another, committeth adultery:  and whoso marrieth her which is put away doth commit adultery."

 

4. Instruction to believers -

I Cor. 7:10-11

There is no dissolution of the marriage union, so long as both are

recognized as Believers.  The idea is to be "reconciled".

There should always be prayer and counsel for reconciliation.

 

5. Instruction to one Believer and one willing unbeliever-

I Cor. 7:12-13;16  I Peter 3:12

The believer must do all within his power to live a Christian life,

believing God for the conversion of the one unsaved.

 

6. Instruction to one believer and one unwilling unbeliever - I Cor. 7:15

“If the unbelieving depart, let him depart.”

"Depart “- "Choreo" 5562 --to separate; to put space between

“Put asunder” --to go away Matthew 19:6; Mark 10:9

“A brother or sister is not under bondage in such cases”--not responsible to make them stay at home

Bondage--”Douloo” > not a slave--”not under bondage”

“But God has called us to peace”

Live as to receive them back again.

As a principle, no provision is made for divorce to Believers walking in the light of the truth. I Cor.7:10,11

It is far better, even with the unconverted, to work problems out so that the marriage is saved.

 

III. God's Will Concerning Remarriage -

The Apostle Paul said in I Cor. 7:27  "Art thou bound unto a wife? (bound=married) “seek not to be loosed.” (”Loosed” -”Luo” 3089  alone; break-up; destroy; dissolve; melt; put off) >>>”The vow between us made as long as we both shall live.” <<<

“Art thou loosed from a wife? Remarriage is permitted only after separation by death. Loosed only by death. >>“seek not a wife.” <<  

 

1. Remarriage is permitted for a widower-

V.28 “but and if thou marry thou has not sinned”

“Such shall have trouble in the flesh, but I spare you.”

In the latter part of verse 28 Paul said, Amplified N.T., "Yet those who marry will have physical and earthly troubles, and I would like to spare you that."  

7:29 “But this I say brethren, the time is short; it remaineth, that both they that have wives be as though they had none.

I Cor. 7:8 “abide even as I”

Mark 19:9-11 All men cannot receive this saying, save they to whom it is given.

2. Remarriage is permitted but only to a single woman -

Matt. 5:32  "Whosoever shall marry her that is divorced committeth

adultery."

However this permission is only given to those whose wife has passed away or who were married under a false pretense-- their wife claimed to be a virgin and she was found to be impure--only at marriage

 

3. Remarriage is permitted to a widow -

I Cor. 7:39 "If her husband be dead, she is at liberty to be married."

 

4. Those remarried with two living spouses cannot be in the ministry as a pastor minister or deacon-

(1) Pastors - I Tim. 3:2  "A bishop then must be blameless, the husband of one wife"

(2) Minister - Titus 1:5,6 "Elders...the husband of one wife."

(3) Deacons - I Tim. 3:12  "Deacons be the husbands of one wife."

 

  1. Remarriage is permitted for a widow but only to a single man (includes a widower)-I Cor. 7:19

 

IV. God's Will In Dealing With The Unsaved -

That is those that married and divorced and remarried before salvation.

 

1. The Lord dealt very graciously with those that were married and divorced and remarried before salvation -

John 4:13-19;26

 

2. He also was very gracious with those caught in the act of adultery -

John 8:4-11

 

3. He dealt graciously with the harlots -

Matt. 21:31,32  Luke 7:36-48

But His answer was always the same to those that came to Him and received Him. "Go and sin no more."
