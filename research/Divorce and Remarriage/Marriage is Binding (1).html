THE EXCEPTION OF DIVORCE

 

 

Jesus uses both “fornication” and “adultery” in Matt. 5:32 and in Matt. 19:9. If He wanted you to believe fornication meant adultery He would have said, adultery, He had no problem using the words.

Fornication from the Greek is "Porneia"

Illicit sexual intercourse between the unmarried. Before marriage.

Adultery from the Greek is "Moichos" Unlawful intercourse with the spouse of another. After marriage.

What Jesus was saying - that if a man married a woman and she had been unfaithful before marriage - she was not a virgin - he could put her away at the time of marriage. This is the only exception that Jesus gave was for "fornication" and not for adultery - after marriage. God even told the prophet Hosea to go love his wife that had played the part of a whore - and bring her home and make her the mother of his children. Hosea Chpt. 1 - Chpt. 3.

Not satisfied with what Jesus said, the Pharisees reminded Him of Moses. Matt. 19:7 - "Why did Moses then command to give a writing of divorcement, and to put her away?

Under Moses a man was allowed to divorce his wife, but God never gave that right.

 

Notice the reason that Jesus gives of Moses giving a divorce.

vs. 8 "Moses because of the HARDNESS of your HEARTS suffered you to put away your wives:

Now notice what Jesus said:

"But from the BEGINNING it was not so."

Malachi 2:16 "For the LORD, the God of Israel, saith that He hateth putting away."
