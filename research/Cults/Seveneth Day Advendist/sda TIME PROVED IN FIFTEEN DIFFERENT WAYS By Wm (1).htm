TIME PROVED In FIFTEEN DIFFERENT WAYS
By Wm. Miller

Predicted that Christ would come between  March 21, 1843 and March 21, 1844.  He did not set the October 22, 1844 date but accepted it. After the 1843 and 1844 failure he renounced all of his date setting as false and that the 1844 movement had nothing to do with prophecy in any sense. See SDA Encyclopedia vol. 10, p. 889-891.  

William Miller (1782-1849)                                    

 Miller's Confession

"We expected the personal coming of Christ at that time; and now to contend that we were not mistaken, is dishonest. We should never be ashamed frankly to confess our errors. I have no confidence in any of the new theories that grew out of that movement, namely, that Christ then came as the Bridegroom, that the door of mercy was closed, that there is no salvation for sinners, that the seventh trumpet then sounded, or that it was a fulfillment of prophecy in any sense." History of the Advent Message, p. 410, 412.

Miller's Honesty vs. Ellen G. White's Honesty

Miller confessed his errors and would not hold to non-biblical teachings that grew out of the Advent Movement. He renounced that Christ came as the Bridegroom, the door of mercy was closed to the world, and there were no salvation for sinners, while Ellen White and her husband James White, and Joseph Bates continued to teach these false doctrines after 1844.

Below is a facsimile of the chart used by Millerite preachers to show Miller's calculations for the Second Coming of Christ in 1843 and the end of the world. Ellen G. White put her prophetic stamp of approval on this chart and blamed God for the failure of their cultic calculations.

EGW: I have seen that the 1843 chart was directed by the hand of the Lord, and that the figures were as he wanted then; that his hand was over the figures and hid a mistake in some of the figures, so that none could see it until His hand was removed. Early Writings, p. 74.

Miller's Proof Christ Would Return in 1843, Fifteen Different Ways.

Miller was wrong fifteen times. He twisted Old and New Testament Scripture dealing with time to arrive at the date 1843. Many noted educated pastors of his day tried to show him his errors, but he would not listen to them. Try not to be to hard on Mr. Miller after reading his "Fifteen Different Ways" as he was not educated in theology but he was honest and sincere.

As for 1843 and 1844 you already know it was a non-event! Not only was William Miller mistaken about the identity of the 2,300 evenings and mornings (the earth, in his opinion), but he was pervasively mistaken in all his chronological schemes (his fifteen "proofs"). Later SDA claims that he was merely mistaken because he didn't understand that Daniel 8:14 was speaking of a supposedly heavenly sanctuary, though his chronology was sound only adds insult to injury. The SDA claim that the sanctuary in Dan. 8:14 is heavenly is just as bad as Miller's original identification, and the chronology can't be salvaged in any way. The whole thing is bogus.

 Who will this day overtake as a thief ? Surely not those who are looking for it. I Thess. v. 4; Heb. ix. 28.  Who will be destroyed when it comes?  Those who are overtaken as a thief. I Thess. v. 3; Matt. xxiv. 50; Rev. xvi. 15, You ask, will all who do not look for him, perish in the day of his coming? I answer, it would seem so, by many texts of scripture. Yet I am not their judge. God only knows what will become of them. They that were ready went in with him, and the door was shut.      

                                         WILLIAM MILLER
