EGW's 2,000 Visions Fable

By Robert K. Sanders

The Seventh-day Adventist Church has made every effort to make the claim that their prophetess Ellen G. White had OVER 2,000 visions during her lifetime.

How did this number of "over" 2,000 visions come about?

If the SDA Church lies about this can they be trusted on other claims they make about their prophetess?

We will look at three sources where the SDA Church promotes Ellen G. White as having "over" 2,000 visions.
First: A plaque located at Oak Hill Cemetery; Battle Creek, MI.
Second:  Seventh-day Adventists Believe 28 Fundamental Beliefs.
Third: Charismatic Experiences by Arthur White. EGW's grandson.

First Source

 

 SDA's claim is on a plaque located at Oak Hill Cemetery; Battle Creek, MI where James and Ellen White are buried.

The  plaque tells reads, "Ellen, a prophetess reportedly experienced over two thousand visions." Notice the white line drawn under the statement.

 

 

It is interesting when SDAs are confronted with EGW's failed visions and Bible contradictions they try to get her off the hook by saying she was not a prophetess, even when their church teaches that she is, ON HER PLAQUE

Seventh-day Adventists Believe, p. 224. "From 1844, when she 17, until 1915—the year of her death—she had more than 2,000 visions."

Calculating EGW's Visions

Husband James White gives EGW an estimate of 100-200 visions from 1844-1868.

 

James White wrote, “She has probably had, during the past twenty-three years, between one and two hundred visions.” Life Incidents 1868, p. 272. (1844-1868)

 

Using James' highest estimate of 200 visions between 1844-1866 a 23 years period would be a yearly average 8.6 visions. Keep this average of between 8.6 visions a year in mind.

Arthur White comments on James White's estimate: "that this would mean an average vision once in six weeks." Variation and Frequency of the Ellen G. White Visions, p. 2.

Subtracting 1868 from 1915 the year of EGW's death is 47 years. Using James' highest estimate of visions of 8.6 x 47 years we have 404.2 average visions for the 47 year period.

By adding 402.2 visions to the 200 visions we now have 602.2 average visions from 1844 to 1915. Subtracting this from Arthur Whites' 2,000 visions we have missing 1397.8 visions.

 The White Estate’s own Index to the Writings of Ellen G. White,
