1. Know when not to treat symptoms. Believe it or not, those annoying cold and flu symptoms are part of the natural healing process -- proof your immune system is working.

 

2. Blow your nose often -- and the right way. When you blow too hard, pressure can carry germ-carrying phlegm back into your ear passages, causing earache. So when blowing your nose, press a finger over one nostril and blow gently.

 

3. Treat that stuffy nose with warm salt water.

 

4. Stay warm and rested.

 

5. Gargle. Gargling can moisten a sore throat and bring temporary relief.

 

6. Drink hot liquids. Hot liquids relieve nasal congestion, prevent dehydration and soothe the inflamed membranes that line your nose and throat.

 

7. Take a steamy shower to moisturize your nasal passages. They're relaxing too!

 

8. Use a small dab of mentholated salve under your nose to open breathing passages.

 

9. Apply hot or cold packs around your congested sinuses. Either temperature works.

 

10. Sleep with an extra pillow under your head.

 

11. Don't fly, unless necessary. If you must fly, use a decongestant and carry a nasal spray with you to use before takeoff and landing.

 

12. Eat infection-fighting foods.
