You are blessed with beautiful facilities and student body. From what I have seen, you have a lot of the pieces in place to be successful as a school.

 

I want to encourage Sister Parret to not constantly second guess yourself. As long as you remain humble, learn from other people, and lean on the Lord it doesn’t matter if you ‘don’t do it like everyone else.’ You will grow and learn as an educator every day. The truth of the matter is, that nothing will be done ‘perfect’ the first or second time through and no one reasonable expects that.

 

Your greatest asset will be ASKING QUESTIONS. Utilize all the people that God has blessed you to know and ‘pick their brain’ every chance you get. Asking questions doesn’t make you stupid, but it makes you honest and brings you closer to the answers you need.

 

Looking at Christian schools in general, they are as diverse as people are. No one does it exactly the same way. What works well for one, might not work for you. The challenge is finding what works for you. So, do learn what others are doing and try it out. Also, do not be scared to be creative with your own solutions. ACE gives you the core structure as  a foundation to your school, but the very details that will make your school uniquely effective is your personality, gifts, and anointing.

 

As far as your questions concerning marketing go, you can and should of course do the simple things like a website and other forms of advertisement, but your real ‘draw’ is going to be the SUBSTANCE of your product. Do you create an atmosphere and setting in which students may excel if they set their mind to it? (Are there a lot of distractions? Inconsistencies? Is their absolute fairness and equal treatment? Is the quality of education offered ‘top-notch’? and etc.) Second, capitalize on your uniqueness. If you do what everyone else is doing, what reason would someone have in choosing you? Dare to be different and dynamic while maintaining a consistent core structure. Demand excellence from your students and you will reap excellence.

 

I will try to briefly outline in the next few pages some ideas I hope will inspire even better ideas for you. You are taking up an incredible challenge that is SO needed and rewarding. I believe you two and your church have what it takes to be successful—a vision and sincere burden.

Scheduling

 

What are your main goals for the school day?

PACE work complete

  • Daily complete 20-27 pages.
  • Depends wholly on the age of the student. High school students will need to average more pages per day.
  • All PACE work should be completed during school time in any reasonable schedule.
  • If students are given only 2 hours of class time to complete assignments, it would be unreasonable to expect 30 pages of production.

Spiritual Influence

  • Daily Devotions and opening exercises (15-20 mins.)
  • Weekly chapel services. (Again a reasonable period: 45 min.)
  • Challenge them DAILY.

Real World and relational interaction

  • Field trips.
  • Special trips can be planned on a larger scale for quarter ends, but the average field trip should be of minimal cost and time. Trip to the town hall, local farm, local manufacturer, someone that hand carves wood? Etc.
  • Bring in professionals to the school—policemen, city councilmen, missionary, etc.
  • Students’ own ‘oral reports’ are excellent structured times of learning from each other.
  • Structured learning games as rewards. scrabble, bible trivia, jeopardy, etc.

BREAKS

  • It is important to rest their eyes and stretch about every 45 minutes of sit down study.
  • Breaks should be as consistent and rigid as the Law of Moses! Frequent changing of break times and lengths is counterproductive.
  • A couple 5 minute ‘breaks’ is more beneficial to 1 long break. (with the exception of lunch allowing for time to eat.)
  • Do the same thing every time. i.e., bell rings at 9:30. “Five minute break everybody.” At 9:35, “Break is over, please return to your seat.” Only moments allowed to find a seat and sit down.
  • Leniency with break times are often the greatest source of stress for the teachers. Keep it simple and consistent.
  • Every break should have an OBVIOUS purpose. “The five-minute break is a time to rest the eyes, use the restroom, and get a drink of water. NOT a time to play a game of 21.
  • Lunch breaks could include a time for ‘cool off.’ Five minutes before the 30 minute mark, the bell could be rung so everyone can cool down and get ready to reenter the solemn learning center.

Exciting for Students

  • Variety
  • Give them incentives. “If you complete 20 pages before lunch today, we will leave early for gym.”.
  • Give them options. “Would you like to go on a field trip or finish working toward honor roll status this Friday?
  • How can all this be fit into a school week? A simple but consistent schedule.
  • ULTIMATE GOAL OF A SCHOOL SCHEDULE: CONSISTENCY.

Discipline

 

A lot of disciplinary problems can be avoided if the law is ‘layed down’ (NO exceptions) on core rules. For Example:

  • Maintain Library Atmosphere. Classroom during study times should be as solemn as a funeral. Whispers and minimal noise are only acceptable. Breaks will be provided for time to talk and enjoy each other’s company.
  • PACE work time should be a concentrated time on their work and free of classroom distractions.
  • Visitors should converse quietly or better yet, outside of the classroom.
  • Phone calls should be taken outside of the classroom.
  • The teachers must set the example in the sanctity of the classroom and the students will learn to follow.
  • Certain rule infractions should be considered unacceptable behavior in any measure at all (backtalk, threatening, violence, etc.) and met with immediate disciplinary action.
    • Involve the parents – do not assume the students are communicating with their parents. Send home notices to be signed by parents concerning poor behavior and set detentions. Hold special interviews with parents to discuss their child’s behavior and needs. PTA meetings will allow you to deal with many things corporately so that no one will feel singled out.
    • Involve the pastor – Schedule ‘talks’ with the pastor. Etc.
    • The more you let ‘slide’ the more they will try to get by with.
  • REPEAT rules often. It is a pain but necessary. Remind them of what they should be doing. It comes with the job 

 

SOME IDEAS FOR DISCIPLINARY ACTION:

  • Suspension of Privileges (lose A,C,E status).
  • Isolation from student body during break times.
  • Manual Service (extra cleaning, perform another student’s regular duty)
  • Detention time spent writing essays related offence.
  • Lecture the student body as a whole for the infraction of one or a few students. (Students will work as their own policemen because they don’t want to hear another lecture.) Require hand written notes on the lecture given.
  • Never wait to boiling point, but take some level of action for each offense. (step 1 might be a demerit slip, but a repeat of the same offense might justify detention and an essay.)
  • Expel is obvious the last resort and the least desirable method, but sometimes absolutely necessary. Always consider the well-being of your student body as a whole. 1 rotten apple can spoil a whole barrel.
