The Text

 

What is meant by the text?  The text is the Scriptural foundation/basis/passage upon which the sermon is built.  It is sometimes called the preaching portion, or the preaching unit.  The text is not a springboard from which to jump!

 

 

  1. Its Purpose—The reasons you should use a text.

 

Why do we preach from a Scriptural passage?  Why don’t we just preach?  Why should we use a text?  Is this some pious practice that has developed arbitrarily throughout the course of church history?

 

  1. To follow the example of Jesus (and the apostles), we must “preach the Word.”

F. To preach at all, we must “preach the Word!”

 

In order to preach at all, we must preach from the Bible.

 

 

 

  1. Its Definition—What you should understand by the use of this term.

 

  1. Generally, the text is the Scriptural basis or foundation or passage upon which the sermon is built.  Sometimes, it is called the preaching portion, preaching unit.

 

B. Etymologically, the term is a Latin expression.

1. < L. textus, n.  (Compare, texture.)

  1. The product of weaving.
  2. The web.
  3. The fabric.

 

2. < L. texere, v.

  1. To weave.
  2. To put together, to construct.
  3. To compose.

 

C. Possibly, the text may include more than is read.

 

D. Usually, the text means the words read.

 

  1. As a rule, the theme should cover the entire text.
  2. Although, one theme might not exhaust the text.
  3. If possible, let the text form a complete sentence/thought.

III. Its Selection—How you ought to choose a text.

 

A. You must be careful and prayerful when choosing, because the text must be carefully and prayerfully chosen.  We need the Holy Spirit’s guidance.

 

  1. Your selection must be appropriate.  It will be appropriate if it’s God-ordained.  We need to listen for His direction.

 

  1. You must be purposeful, by seeking to edify or evangelize.  Purpose is the determining factor.  “Purpose is what defines a preaching unit,” (Adams, 26).

 

  1. You will glorify and honor God by building up His church.
  2. The whole purpose of preaching is to edify the flock.
  3. Therefore, your selection should involve the needs of your listener’s.  So, consider the needs of the congregation.
  4. Determine the Holy Spirit’s purpose behind a passage.  (Jay Adams shows his intense feeling about this when he writes that this is “one of the most important obligations in preaching, perhaps the greatest of all….”  He continues, “…failure to do so constitutes an affront to the Holy Spirit.”—Preaching With Purpose, 25.)

 

  1. You might also have to involve one of the following factors.

 

  1. Christian Church calendar.
  2. Current events.
  3. Personal experience.

 

E. You might have difficulty, but it will be worth it!

 

 

 

IV. Its Advantages—The blessings you and your listeners gain by using a text.

 

A. The use of a text conforms the elements of preaching to truth.

 

  1. Awakening interest of audience.
  2. Reinforcing confidence in message.
  3. Giving authority to the preacher.

 

  1. The use of a text promotes attachment to the Bible.

 

  1. Giving greater variety to preaching.
  2. Hedging against un-Scriptural topics and views.
  3. Affording opportunity to explain Scripture

 

  1. The use of a text guards against desultoriness.

 

  1. Holding the message on track.
  2. Keeping the messenger from wandering.
  3. Aiding the listener’s memory.
