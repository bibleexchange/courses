1. THE MISSION OF PREACHING.

 

This tells us why we preach.

 

The sermon is framed with a view to persuasion.

 

  1. The aim which the preacher sets before him.

 

    1. The salvation of souls.

(God chose the foolishness of preaching to

save the lost. – I Cor. 1:21.)

 

    1. Spiritual growth.

 

      1. For the perfecting of the saints.

Eph. 4:11-14.

      1. To feed the sheep.  Jn. 21:15-17.

 

    1. Produce faith. – Rom. 10:17.

 

      1. For healing.
      2. To meet needs.

 

    1. Instruct the church. – II Tim. 4:2 – memorize.

 

    1. To encourage the believers.

 

    1. To bring the congregation to a place of decision.

 

  1. The means which the preacher uses to persuade the

Congregation.

 

    1. Different types of sermons.

 

a. Textual – The text gives the theme, title and

divisions.  The divisions are suggested by the

prominent words of the text.

  1. Topical – Founded on the theme or topic of the

text.

 

 

 

c. Expository – An exposition of the text.

To expose or explain the text.

  1.      Textual Topical – The sermon is treated textually

and expressed topically.

  1.      Textual Inferential – The sermon is drawn from

inferences out of the text.

 

    1. Different types of subjects.

 

      1. Doctrinal – Instructional preaching.  Preaching the

Doctrines of the Word of God. – II Tim. 4:2.

      1. Historical
        1. Setting the historical scene of a Scripture

passage.  (Telling the story.)

        1. Relating of history to inspire faith.

(Great revivals.)

 

      1. Biographical
        1. Scripture characters.
        2. Great men of God.
      2. Exegetical – An explanation of the text.

(Thorough examination)

      1. Prophetical
        1. Show how prophecy has been fulfilled.
        2. Scripturally unfolding God’s plan for

the future.   

 

    1. A good outline. – Know where you are going.

 

      1. Text – Draw the sermon out of it.
      2. Title – The name given to the sermon.
      3. Theme
        1. Point you are going to make.
        2. Drawn from the text.
      4. Introduction – To lead the congregation into the

sermon.

 

 

 

 

      1. Body – The main points (divisions) of the

sermon.

      1. Conclusion – An ending to the sermon.

 

    1. A powerful delivery.
      1. The unction of the Holy Spirit. – I  Jn. 2:20.
      2. Concentrate on speaking clearly. (Diction)
      3. Preach with passion.

(Believe what you preach.)

 

    1. Use plain speech.
      1. Cause the people to understand.

“So they read in the book in the law of God

distinctly, and gave the sense, and caused

them to understand the reading.” –

Neh. 8:8.

    1. Distinctly – The Word was read clearly.
    2. Gave the sense – Explained the Word

to the people.

    1. Caused them to understand the reading –

The people were taught the Word, and

then they understood.

      1. D.L. Moody spoke in common language to  

                                         common men.

  c. Say what you mean, and mean what you say.

“A preacher ought to preach, that when the sermon

is ended, the congregation shall disperse saying,

‘The preacher said this.’ ” – Martin Luther.

 

  1. The objective of the preacher. I Pet. 4:11.

 

    1. The main objective is persuasion.

 

2. To deliver a message from God to man.

      1. The oracles of God.  

(a spokesman of God)

b. The preacher needs a Word from God.

c. The preacher should be led by the Holy

                              Spirit during the delivery of the sermon.

 

                     3. To  glorify God.

                                  a. Flesh must never be exalted. – I  Cor. 1:29.

  b. The preacher must be hidden in Christ. –

Gal. 2:20.

 

   4. To apply the truth to the congregation. –

James 1:22.

   a. What does this mean to me?

                                                                    (To the congregation.)

   b. An illustration or personal experience may

be used to help the congregation apply

the truth to them.

   c. Tell the congregation how to apply the truth.

(For example, don’t only preach you need

to walk in victory, tell them how. – Accept

the victory as a gift. – I  Cor. 15:57.)
