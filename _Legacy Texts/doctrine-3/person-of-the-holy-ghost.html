The Person of the Holy GhostSWAHILI

The study of the Holy Ghost is referred to as pneumatology. "Pneuma" is the Greek word for "spirit" and "-ology" is the study of a particular subject. The English words "spirit" and "ghost" are renderings of the Greek word "pneuma". The King James translators used both of these words to translate the one Greek word "pneuma" which literally means wind or breath. The gifts of the Spirit have already been covered in Doctrine 2 so they will not be discussed again in this course.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost is referred to as third person of the Trinity.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost is not third in power or position.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost is equal with The Father and The Son.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost possesses the attributes of personality.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost is described as having a mind.SWAHILI

Romans 8:27 -- And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to the will of God.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost has a will.SWAHILI

I Corinthians 12:11 -- But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Ghost, dividing to every man severally as he will. SWAHILI

This does not mean that the will of the Holy Ghost is not in perfect harmony with the wills of the Father and the Son. SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost can be grieved.SWAHILI

Ephesians 4:30 -- And grieve not the Holy Ghost of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.SWAHILI

The Holy Ghost has personal activities ascribed to him.SWAHILI
