Objectives of this CourseSWAHILI

PersonalSWAHILI

To aid the student in his personal study,contemplation, and understanding of God with an extended overview.SWAHILI

UnderstandableSWAHILI

To make the material and vocabulary of this subject intelligible to beginning students as well as advanced students. SWAHILI

TrueSWAHILI

Truth is absolute, but our understanding of Truth grows clearer the more we lend our heart and mind to the Holy Ghost and His Word. This course is written knowing that ongoing study with its additions, revisions, enlargements, and modifications is necessary.SWAHILI

UnifyingSWAHILI

It is important that our study be motivated by a passion for the Scriptures and our Lord. Any other motive will cause our learning only to be divisive and destructive. Any truth learned and applied is an awesome blessing and no cause for boasting. Remember that because our knowledge and understanding is fluid we must use much grace when Christian's disagree on doctrinal points. No person or church has a monopoly on God and it is dangerous to study and present the Scriptures as if you are the only one who is right.SWAHILI

Ephesians 4:3,13 -- Endeavouring to keep the unity of the Spirit in the bond of peace. 13 Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God, unto a perfect man, unto the measure of the stature of the fulness of Christ:SWAHILI

Personal Qualifications for this CourseSWAHILI

  1. The ardent love of truth for its own sake.SWAHILI
  2. The supreme and disinterested love of God.SWAHILI
  3. An intense desire to know more of God.SWAHILI
  4. A strong desire to make Him known to others and a willingness to make any personal sacrifice for this end.SWAHILI
  5. A sense of ignorance and dependence upon Divine teaching and such humility as to be willing to expose your ignorance.SWAHILI
  6. A willingness to apply and practice as fast as you learn.SWAHILI
  7. A fixed purpose to know and do the whole truth which is the will of God.SWAHILI
  8. A state of mind that will not be diverted to make provision for the flesh.SWAHILI
  9. Docility of mind. (Teachable; obedient;submissive disposition; no desire to rebel against authority, or control.)SWAHILI
  10. The love of study.SWAHILI
  11. Sound education. (Education is obviously an asset to any student, yet God can work with what you know, if you are willing to apply yourself and build upon what knowledge you do have.)SWAHILI
  12. Industrious habits.SWAHILI
  13. Patience and perseverance in investigation.SWAHILI
  14. A mind so balanced as to be duly influenced by evidence.SWAHILI

Personal Benefits from DoctrineSWAHILI

  1. A constantly increasing sense of your own ignorance.SWAHILI
  2. The highest advantages for growth in personal holiness.SWAHILI
  3. The habit of rapid, correct, and consecutive thought.SWAHILI
  4. System in thinking and communicating thought.SWAHILI
  5. Facility in preparations for the pulpit.SWAHILI
  6. Exactness in the statement of the doctrines of Christianity.SWAHILI
  7. Facility in proving them.SWAHILI
  8. Consistency of views and statements.SWAHILI
  9. A settled state of mind in regard to religious truth.SWAHILI
  10. Ability to teach the doctrines and duties of religion.SWAHILI

SOME THINGS TO BE AVOIDEDSWAHILI

  1. Avoid tempting God, by demanding an impossible or unreasonable kind or degree of evidence.SWAHILI
  2. Avoid a cavilling state of mind.SWAHILI
  3. Avoid defending error for the sake of an argument.SWAHILI
  4. Avoid committing yourself to an opinion.SWAHILI
  5. Avoid calling in question first truths.SWAHILI
  6. Avoid attempting to prove them.SWAHILI
  7. Avoid begging the question.SWAHILI
  8. Avoid impatience at the ignorance or stupidity of your classmates.SWAHILI
  9. Avoid an ambition to excel them in study and argument.SWAHILI
  10. Avoid a disputatious spirit.SWAHILI
  11. Avoid stating one thing and proving another in your skeletons [outlines].SWAHILI
  12. Avoid the use of weak and inconclusive arguments.SWAHILI
  13. Avoid an involved method of stating your propositions.SWAHILI
  14. Avoid stating more than you can prove.SWAHILI
  15. Avoid leaving your propositions, until fully supported by evidence or argument.SWAHILI
  16. Avoid the accumulation of evidence or argument after your proposition is fully established.SWAHILI
  17. Avoid prolixity in the statement of your propositions.SWAHILI
  18. Avoid the great error of supposing that truths that are self-evident to some minds are so to all.SWAHILI
