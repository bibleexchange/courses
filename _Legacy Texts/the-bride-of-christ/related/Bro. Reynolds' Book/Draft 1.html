Preface –

 

I have read that it has been said the book to write is the one that you would like to read and cannot find anywhere.  One of the reasons for this book is the fact that I could not find one that presented these truths in a manner in which truth is not distorted to express men’s opinions, but expresses the truth itself.

 

The primary reason for this book is that I believe God spoke to my spirit by His Spirit, to “write in a book the things, which you have seen and heard.”

 

I present to you in this book the Truth as the Spirit of God revealed it to me through visions, revelation, study and information given to me by spirit-filled, God-fearing people.

 

What is presented in this book is not the popular consensus of fundamental groups but it is the only view that presents the truth in a consistent, logical, honest, intelligent and comprehensible manner.

 

Beginning with the Reformation, God began restoring “the faith which was once delivered unto the saints.”  That is, the Faith that had been lost through tradition, opinions and human ideas.  Remember:  The Bible says, “The natural man receiveth not the things of the Spirit of God:  for they are foolishness unto him:  neither can he know them, because they are spiritually discerned”  (I Cor. 2:14).

 

Again, the Bible says, “As it is written, Eye hath not seen, nor ear hear, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which God hath prepared for them that love him.  But God hath revealed them unto us by His Spirit:  for the Spirit searcheth all things, yea, the deep things of God” (I Cor. 2:9,10).

 

This tenth verse in the Amplified NT reads, “Yet to us God has unveiled and revealed them by and through His Spirit, for the (Holy) Spirit searches diligently, exploring and examining everything, even sounding the profound and bottomless things of God – the divine counsels and things hidden and beyond man’s scrutiny.”

 

 

Jesus said, “If any man will do His will, he shall know of the doctrine” (John 7:17).

 

There has to be obedience to the truth already revealed to receive more truth.

 

Proverbs 4:18 says, “The path of the just is as the shining light, that shineth more and more unto the perfect day.”

 

Several years ago, while I was praying, God gave me a vision.  In the vision, I saw a train moving rapidly through the night:  While I was watching this vision, I was trying to understand what God was saying to me.  There was a night scene, a fast moving train, and a light shinning out into the night.  The Spirit spoke to me and said, “Watch the light.”  I was so taken up with trying to understand what God was saying to me by the vision of the train that I really did not grasp what the Spirit was saying.  I watched the train as it raced through the night and came into a turn.  As it began to make the turn, the trees and grass were lit up along the tracks.  The Sprit said again, “Watch the light.”  The train made the turn and headed down the track.  I could see out into the night.  The track was lit up by the light.  Again, the Spirit said, “Watch the light.”  Suddenly, I saw the train slowing down and coming to a stop.  When it stopped, the Spirit said again, “Watch the light.”  Still I did not understand what God was trying to tell me.  After a moment the train began to move again.  As the big wheels on the engine began to move and the train began to move down the track, the Spirit said again, “Watch the light.”  This time I was made to understand what the Spirit was saying.  As the train moved, the light moved; when the train stopped, the light stopped.

 

We must move in the light that we have for God to give us more.  If we stop moving on in the direction of the light (truth), the light will stop.

 

Hebrews 6:1, “Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection:  not laying again the foundation of repentance from dead works, and of faith toward God.”

 

 

 

 

Ephesians 4:11-14, “He gave some, apostles; and some, prophets; and some, evangelists; and some, pastors and teachers; (or teaching pastors) for the PERFECTING of the SAINTS, for the work of the ministry, for the edifying of the body of Christ:  Till we all come in the unity of the faith, and of the knowledge of the Son of God, UNTO A PERFECT MAN, unto the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ:  That we henceforth be no more children,”

 

As we now approach this 12th chapter, I would admonish you to join your prayers with mine, that God would grant you divine revelation and understanding.

 

Without the inspiration and help of the Holy Spirit, I question that you will understand the real meaning that we are presenting in this book.  There is much prejudice against the study and understanding of this Revelation.  Though it is the greatest  prophetic book of the New Testament addressed to the whole Church Age, from the Ephesus Age – the beginning of the Church, to the Laodicean Age – the Last Church Age.  We now endeavor to present the truths as God has given them to us.  Earnestly praying God’s blessing upon what is written and upon all those who read and desire to understand.

 

Rev. James R. Reynolds, Sr.

The Sun Clothed Woman & The Man-Child

Rev. 12:1-2

 

“And there appeared a great wonder in heaven; a woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and upon her head a crown of twelve stars:  And she being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered.”

 

The Book of Revelation is one of the most important writings in the Word of God.  Though it has withstood attacks in every age, there is not another book in sacred scripture more strongly attested, or more fully authenticated.  Its title, its historical declaration, the nature of its contents, the prophetic statements, are ample evidence of its geniuses, and divine origin.

 

As the Spirit of God reveals truth, you will find that the adversary will attack.  If you preach a diluted (basic) message of salvation (or social gospel), he will not trouble you much, because he knows that the tribulation in imminent, and those that are ignorant of the truth and are not living in the light of truth will go into the tribulation.

 

Hosea 4:6  “My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge:”

 

The devil is not fighting the luke-warm Christian.  He is positioned/stationed wherever the truth is being revealed and is fighting the truth and the revelation of God for this hour.  The scripture shows exactly where the devil is stationed today.

 

Revelation 12:4  “And the dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, (for one purpose) for to devour her Child as soon as it was born.”  This is the woman that was sun-clothed which John had just spoken about – being with child…travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered.  The woman is ready to give birth, the devil is standing before the woman ready to devour her child as soon as it was brought forth.

 

One thing most remembered – there are definite points of division in the Book of Revelation, and here, in this twelfth chapter is one of the dividing points.  In the fulfillment of this part of the prophecy we are going back into the Church Age.  The eleventh chapter ends with the last of the Millennium reign of Christ.  Vs. 15 “And the seventh angel sounded; and there were great voices in heaven, saying, the kingdoms of our Lord, and of His Christ; and He shall reign forever and ever.”

 

In this passage is the picture of a woman, clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet, and on her head a crown of twelve stars, and she is agonizing in pain and travail to be delivered of a Man-Child.

 

The first thing we will deal with is –

 

I. The Importance of This Revelation

 

1. It was a sign –

 

Rev. 12:1  “Wonder”  Greek – “Semeion”

A sign, symbol, miracle, mystery, wonder.

In this passage we have a word which the Apostle uses

for the first time in the Apocalypse, “Semeion” “sign”, which carries with it a great prophetic significance.

 

What is being revealed in this text is a woman being used in a symbolic manner representing something else, which is the true or real subject being considered.

 

The Standard American Revised Version of the Bible says, “And a great sign was seen in heaven.”

 

The Amplified New Testament says, “And a great sign (wonder warning of future events of ominous significance) appeared in heaven,”

 

Dr. T.A. Seiss in his “Lectures of the Apocalypse” says, “I have repeatedly remarked that when the scripture uses figures of symbols or speaks in a way not intended to be taken literally, like all serious writings they always give some intimation of it, in one way or another.  The text is a case in point.  What is described is said to be a “sign”, a representation or picture of something else – a symbol.  And the fact that we are here told that this is a sign, goes far to prove that the Apocalypse in general is to be taken literally, except where indication to the contrary is given.”

 

Here the Apostle says, “And there appeared a great wonder (sign) in heaven;” Something very mysteriously happening.

 

  1. It was an important sign –

 

“A great sign” – Indicating something of great importance and remarkable significance.  “Great” – “Megas” – extraordinary – important – miracle.

 

The whole scene is itself so remarkable, marvelous and extraordinary as to demand the idea of something of the greatest importance.

 

It ought to be important enough to attract attention to consider and examine it carefully.

 

  1. The Location of This Revelation –

 

“A great wonder in heaven”

 

  1. It was in the Spirit –

 

Rev. 1:10  “I was in the Spirit on the Lord’s Day.”

John testifies that he was caught away in the Spirit and transported through time down to the coming of the Lord, “The Lord’s Day.”

 

Rev. 4:1,2 “After this I looked, and, behold, a door was opened in heaven:  and the first voice which I heard was it were of a trumpet talking with me; which said, Come up hither, and I will shew thee things which must be hereafter.  And immediately I was in the Sprit; and behold, a throne was set in heaven, and one sat on the throne.”

 

Not only was John transported through time, but also through space.

 

  1. It was in Heaven –

 

“There appeared a great wonder in Heaven.”

 

It appeared or was revealed to him in the heavenly realm.  Paul declared that he had such an experience himself.  

II Cor. 12:2-4.  “I knew a man in Christ above fourteen years ago, (whether in the body, I cannot tell; or whether out of the body, I cannot tell:  god knoweth;) such an one caught up to the third heaven.  And I knew such a man, (whether in the body, or out of the body, I cannot tell:  God knoweth.)”

 

How that he was caught up into paradise, and heard unspeakable words, it is not lawful for a man to utter.

 

  1. It was spiritual –

 

“A great wonder in Heaven”

 

It was totally and completely apart from the natural realm.  It was of an heavenly order not only given in the Spirit but it was of a spiritual significance.  If you miss this you may miss the real interpretation of this verse.  Eph. 1:3 “Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who hath blessed us with all spiritual blessings in heavenly places in Christ.”

 

 

  1. The Exposition of This Revelation –

 

“A woman clothed with the sun, and the moon under her feet and upon her head a crown of twelve stars:”

 

“A woman” – A mystery woman.

 

Who is this woman?

 

Before trying to explain who this woman is, it is necessary to explain who she is not.

 

  1. Who the woman is not –

 

  1. She is not Mary –

 

Mary was of a very lowly condition, a peasant woman.  Also Mary had passed away long before this prophecy – while this woman continues into the tribulation for three and a half years.  Rev. 12:6  “And the woman fled into the wilderness, (tribulation) where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there a thousand two hundred and threescore days.”  (Three and a half years.)  Furthermore Jesus came down from heaven at His birth, the “Man-child” is caught up to God and to His throne shortly after his birth.  When Christ was anabaino ascended up into heaven, this Revelation had not been written.  Whereas it is said at the time of the writing of this Revelation that it was a book of prophecy and that it referred to things still future.  Chapter 1:1 “Things which must shortly come to pass.” Vs. 3 “Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy.”  Chapter 4:1  “I will shew thee things which must be hereafter.”  Chapter 22:18 “For I testify unto every man that heareth the words of the prophecy of this book.”

 

Moreover Jesus ascended (“anabaino”) up into heaven.  Whereas it is said of the “Man-child” he was “caught up” (harpzo) to snatch or catch away, is said of the act of the Spirit of the Lord in regard to Philip Acts 8:39; of the Apostle Paul being caught up to paradise.  II Cor. 12:2-4.  To seize – pull – catch away – take by force.  Also stemming from a root word – to prefer – choose – to take for oneself.  The verb conveys the idea of force suddenly exercised, as in Matt. 11:12 “take it by force.”

 

Therefore if John, had wanted us to understand that his was Jesus, I am sure he would have said, that the Lord Jesus was caught up (snatch – seized) to the throne.  What advantage of John not to have said so?

 

  1. She is not Jerusalem –

 

Some believe that this woman means the city of Jerusalem.  It has been said there are only two women spoken of in this Book of the Revelation, and the one is Babylon, “that great city which reigneth over the kings of the earth,” so the other is that city which spiritually is called Sodom and Egypt, where also our Lord was crucified.

 

It is strange that He would refer to Jerusalem in the Eleventh Chapter as, “Sodom and Egypt,” and then in the twelfth chapter says she is “clothed with the Sun and the Moon under her feet.”  Also, it is contrary to the nature of things, for a material city to take wings of an eagle and fly into the wilderness for three and a half years, and then after the stay in the wilderness to be caught away.

 

  1. She is not Israel –

 

Another school of thought, and probably the more prominent teaching today, is the belief that this is literal Israel.  Dr. James Gray says, “The woman represents Israel it is believed.”  Believed by who?  By most, it not all Pre-tribulation rapturists, because their teaching about the time of the rapture requires it.  They reason, since the Church is to be raptured before the tribulation, and this woman is seen in the tribulation, and this woman is seen in the tribulation, she cannot represent the Church, so she must represent Israel.

 

Dr. Schofield is of the same opinion – “The woman:  Israel.”

 

Many say this must be Israel because of what the Prophet Isaiah said, “Isa. 66:7 “Before she travailed, she brought forth; before her pain came, she was delivered of a man-child.”  Look very carefully at the two prophecies – the one in Isaiah is different from the one, which is stated in Rev. 12:2, which is “travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered.”

 

The one says a woman is in pain travailing to bring forth a man-child, and the other says before she travailed she brought forth.  Before her pain came she was delivered.  So then, they are not the same thing.  

 

Israel as a Nation was unaware of it producing the Christ “the Man-child” of Isaiah 66:7.  The woman is in pain to be delivered of a “Man-child.”

 

The first thing to understand concerning the nation of Israel is that it was an earthly heritage.  An earthly people, an earthly kingdom, and earthly possessions.

 

The only time anything is mentioned of Israel with the heavens is Abraham’s seed being as the starts of the sky, and then, only of those that are spiritual – believers.  Rom. 9:6-8

 

Also, look at the nation of Israel in her spiritual condition as pictured in the Scripture toward God.  Israel as a nation is pictured in the book of Hosea, as an unfaithful wife and as an harlot on the streets.   As you look at Israel today you can see that this is her condition rather that in a place of honor.

 

Here a woman is pictured “clothed with the sun,” producing a “Man-child,” which was caught up to God, and to His throne.”

 

  1. She is not the world church –

 

There are those that believe this is the world church, or the church universal.  A good look at the condition of the Church universal will reveal that she is not arrayed in this fashion – but is worldly, unholy, ungodly, and devilish.  How can an unholy thing produce such a holy thing that is preferred – chosen and caught up to God and to His throne?

 

The only thing that the world church will produce and support is the anti-Christ.

 

 

  1. Who the woman is –

 

There are some keys in earlier chapters.  

 

Rev. 1:3  says, “Blessed is he that readeth, and they that hear the words of this prophecy.”  This is “prophecy” has to do with prophetic utterance, foretelling something that is to come to pass.  Also, in Rev. 1:1  “Things which must shortly come to pass.”  That is these things haven’t come to pass.  This is not a record of history, this is a Book of Prophecy.  Again in Rev. 4:1  “And I will shew thee things which must be hereafter.

 

I believer that God gave me a key and I believe that this key can help us understand the mystery of this “woman.”  I’ve noticed in my studies of the Book of Revelation, that wherever there is a question in John’s mind of anyone not grasping what is to be understood, then he goes on and declares to us what should be understood.  Take for example the mystery of the seven stars and the seven golden candlesticks.  Rev. 1:20 “The seven stars are the angels (messengers) of the seven churches:  and the seven candlesticks which thou sawest are the seven churches.”  Again, here in the twelfth chapter he speaks of the, “Great Red Dragon,”  with seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads.  He then tells us who the dragon is.  Vs. 9 “Satan.”  “And the great dragon was cast out, that old serpent, called the Devil, and Satan, which decieveth the whole world:  he was cast out into the earth, and his angels were cast out with him.”

 

He speaks of the harlot church who is riding upon the Beast in Rev. 17, then he makes it very plain that this is Mystery Babylon, the harlot religious system that has existed throughout all ages.

 

He speaks of Israel, and every time he speaks concerning these things in a figurative manner, then he goes on and explains to us the meaning.

 

So now when we came here to Revelation 12, if you will follow the key that I am sure the Holy Spirit put here, it will help you to see the truth.

 

Rev. 1:4 says, “John to the Seven Churches.”  Who is John writing to?  Are we left to interpret who he is writing to? Has not the Holy Spirit been very precise in putting these things in there to help us understand who John is writing to?  “John to the Seven Churches,”  that is the Seven Church Ages or the whole of the Church Age.

 

What age of the Church are we in today?  The scripture makes it quite plain that we are in the Laodician Age.  And what is the Laodician Age?  It is the last age of the Church.  Notice if John by the Holy Spirit has in other places made it plain that we are to understand certain things, then where there is an absence of explanation, we should understand that he is writing to the church or about the church. This “sun-clothed woman” then that is referred to in Chapter twelve is the Church, and you will find that in this Book of Revelation, the Church is one of the most prominent subjects outside of Christ, who the Revelation is of.

 

Revelation 12:1, in the first part of the verse you see this, “Great wonder in Heaven.”  What is the scriptural position of the Church today?  Eph. 2:6 Paul says, “Hath raised us up together,” that is, He hath raised us up out of sin, “And made us sit together in Heavenly places in Christ Jesus.”  He hath raised us up together, hath quickened us, hath given us life and brought us out of death, and raised us up into heavenly places in Christ Jesus, and He has made us sit together in heavenly places in Christ Jesus.  This is where John said he saw this “woman” in heaven.

 

This is a Revelation of Christ and the Church.  “A great wonder (mystery) is heaven.”  

 

Eph. 5:32 “This is a great mystery:  but I speak concerning Christ and the Church.”

 

The Church is the Body of Christ, of which Christ is the Head.  Eph. 1:22,23 “And hath put all things under His feet, and gave Him to be the head over all things to the Church.  Which is His body, the fullness of Him that filleth all in all.”

 

Eph. 5:23  “For the husband is the head of the wife, even as Christ is the head of the Church.”

 

  1. The Woman’s apparel and position –

 

  1. “Clothed with the sun”

 

Christ is “The Sun of Righteousness.”  Malachi 4:2

Light is the symbol of Christ’s divine nature.  

In John 8:12, Jesus said, “I Am the light of the world.”  The Church is clothed with the light and the glory of the Son of God.  To be “clothed with the sun” means to be clothed with the imputed righteousness of Christ.

 

Rom. 13:12 “The night is far spent, the day is at hand:  let us therefore cast off the works of darkness, and let us put on the armor of light.”

 

Vs. 14 “Put ye on the Lord Jesus Christ, and make not provision for the flesh, to fulfill the lusts thereof.”

 

Gal. 3:27  “For as many of you as have been baptized into Christ have put on Christ.”

 

II Peter 1:4  “Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises, that by these ye might be partakers of the Divine Nature.”

 

Pay particular attention that is does not say that the “woman” embodied the “Sun.”  It says, “The Woman was clothed with the Sun.”  There is a vast difference in having Christ’s righteousness accredited to your account (imputed to you) and having it imparted in you, that is Christ’s righteousness revealed in you by righteous acts – righteous living.  So then, He is not talking about a perfected Church, He is talking only about the possibility of perfection.

 

John, when he sees the “woman” (the Church) he does not see her in a perfected state.  He sees her in a position where she has the potential of entering into a perfected state, which she will during the first three and a half years of the tribulation.

 

  1. “The moon under her feet”

 

The sun is the light that rules the day, so the moon is the ruling light of the night.  The first thing we liken the moon to is the Law.  The moon is a reflection of the sun’s light.  So, likewise, the Law is a reflection of things to come.  “The Law was a school-master to bring us to Christ.”

 

“Clothed with the Sun and the Moon under her feet.”

 

The Church has been elevated by grace in Christ where God is satisfied and the Law is satisfied.

 

Rom. 8:2-4 “For the Law of the Spirit of life in Christ Jesus hath made me free from the Law of sin and death.”

 

“For what the Law could not do, in that it was weak through the flesh, God sending His own Son in the likeness of sinful flesh, and for sin, condemned sin in the flesh:  that the righteousness of the law might be fulfilled in us, who walk not after the flesh, but after the Spirit.”

 

If Grace has not elevated you so that the Law is satisfied (i.e. under your feet), you are still under the Law.  Grace does not allow you to live in sin.   This is a perverted idea about Grace.  Jude 4 “There are certain men crept in unawares, who were before of old ordained to this condemnation, ungodly men turning the grace of our God into lasciviousness, (without restraint), and denying the only Lord God, and our Lord Jesus Christ.”

 

Grace (as an enablement) works in us through Christ to the satisfying of the Law, and we are elevated to a position where the Law is under the feet.

 

The second thing the light can be is Jesus being the light of the day.  There is also a light of the night.  The Night represents darkness, and darkness represents the powers of evil.

 

There is a guiding light that shines in the day, there is a light in the night.  “Lucifer” means brightness – light bearer.  “Lucifer” fell perverting the light that he was given.  The scripture says, “Satan comes as an angel of light.”  He is not an angel of light, he, “Comes as an angel of light.”  The Moon has no real light of its own.

 

John said he saw this “sun-clothed woman” exalted in the heavenlies into this high position.  “Clothed with the Sun and the Moon under her feet.”  

 

Not only a type of the Law, but indicating the powers of darkness.

 

Satan had the power of death, and the power of death came through the broken Law.  The devil still holds this power over the unsaved.

 

Heb. 2:14,15 “Himself (speaking of Christ) likewise took part of the same; that through death He might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the Devil, and deliver them who through fear of death were all their life time subject to bondage.”

 

Christ has put under the feet of the Church the powers of darkness, and the Law (broken Law) which gives darkness its power.  Heb. 2:8  “Thou hast put all things under His feet.”

 

Satan cannot lord it over you.  Remember, “Greater is He that is in you, than He that is in the world.”  You are in a position of rule, you are the devil’s master, you are no longer his servant.  You can put him in his place under your feet and you can stand in your position over the powers of darkness.

 

Jesus said, “And I will build my Church,” and this is what He is doing today; “Building His Church.”  We have seen it all the way from Pentecost right on down through to this very hour.  The real true Church has been militant, successful and spiritually prosperous.  The Church has been attacked, it has been put through the fire, burned at the stake, yet it lives on.  Paul was beheaded, but the Church went on – they crucified Peter possibly upside down, but the Church went on.  Christ has built His Church on Revelation, and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it.

 

We want the Lord to do great things in us.  We want to see great things for us.”  The Word of God gives the answer.  The Lord has already done great things.

 

Eph. 1:19-23 “And what is the exceeding greatness of His power to us-ward who believe, according to the working of His mighty power, which He wrought in Christ, when He raised Him from the dead, and set Him at His own right hand in the heavenly places, far above all principality, and power, and might, and dominion, and every name that is named, not only in this World, but also in that which is to come:  and hath put all things under His feet, and gave him to be the head over all things to the Church, which is His body, the fullness of Him that filleth all in all.”

 

  1. “Upon her head a crown of twelve stars”

 

Eph. 1:22 Christ, “is the head of the Church.”  We are seeing something of a body composed of Christ the head and the Church His body.  Christ has entered into His reward.  He is in the place of authority and has entered into His reward, He has been crowned.  Twelve has to do with governmental perfection – Christ is the Head of this heavenly government.

 

There are some that believe these stars are the twelve apostles.  Turn to Eph. 2:19,20.  “Now therefore ye are no more strangers and foreigners, but fellow-citizens with the saints, and of the household of God;  And are built upon the foundation of the apostles and prophets, Jesus Christ Himself being the Chief corner stone;”

 

Also, you will find that in the New City, Jerusalem, the stones of the foundations will have the names of the twelve apostles inscribed on them.  Foundation stones are much different from stars on a crown.  When you think about a crown, you think about royalty, somebody has been crowned.  Therefore, there must be a kingdom, and when you have a kingdom you have rule and authority and government.  Also, a crown speaks of reward.  Christ being the head of the Church has both been crowned and entered into His reward.  The, “Crown of twelve stars” then has to do with the office, function and the reward of the Lord Jesus Christ.

 

Heb. 5:8,9  “Though He were a Son, yet learned He obedience by the things which He suffered;”

 “And being made perfect, He became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey Him;”

 

Heb. 2:9,10  “We see Jesus,…crowned with glory and honor,…”

“For it became Him, for whom are all things in bringing many sons unto glory, to make the Captain of their salvation perfect through sufferings.”

 

This is Christ’s earthly life that is referred to, but now Christ has entered into His reward and crowned, King of kings and Lord of lords.”

 

  1. “She being with Child”

 

The woman is producing something.  Here a child; in other places bride.

 

In Vs. 2 “With Child”  “gaster”  “echo”  to conceive.

 

There is a cry and a travail in the Church to bring forth a “Man-child”, a company of overcomers.  A people that will live according to the truth of God out of the Church and out of this Church Age, before the tribulation.

 

As God took a rib out of Adam while he slept, God is taking a Rib out of the Church that is asleep and not appropriating all that has been provided for her.  The “Man-child”, the overcomer, the “Rib” company, will appropriate all that has been provided for it.  An overcomer is one that appropriates by faith all the redemptive rights that Christ has provided, so the “Man-child” will be overcomers out of the Church living in all that Christ has provided.

 

Rev. 3:10  “Because thou hast kept the Word of My patience, I also will keep thee from the hour of temptation, (tribulation) which shall come upon all the world, to try them that dwell upon the earth.”

 

There is a people that is going to come out and “escape all these things, and stand before the Son of Man.”  Luke 21:36.

 

While the Church is in this state of being clothed upon, I believe that there are some within the Church that not only are going to be “clothed with,” but are going to be indwelt by the Son.

 

Rev. 3:20  “Behold, I stand at the door, and knock:  if any man hear My voice and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with Me.”

 

Rev. 19:7,8  “Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honor to Him:  for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and His wife hath made herself ready.  And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white:  for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints.”

 

“Righteous Acts”  This is not an imputed righteousness only, set down to your account (“clothed with”), but an imparted righteousness.  Jesus Christ living, ruling and reigning within.  As Paul said in Gal. 2:20  “Christ liveth in me:  and the life which I now live in the flesh I live by the faith of the Son of God, who loved me, and gave Himself for me.”

 

The whole Church will not listen.  Rev. 3:20  “Behold, I stand at the door and knock:  if any man hear My voice, and open the door, I will come in to him, and will sup with him, and he with Me.”

 

Vs. 21  “To him that overcometh will I grant to sit with Me in My throne, even as I also overcame, and am set down with My Father in His throne.”

 

  1. “Cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered.”

 

When birth pains begin to come, they are signs that birth is near.  When the pains become sharp and regular, birth is getting nearer.

 

The Lord wants us to know that after the first sign of birth pains, we should look for more to come.  As they begin coming more regularly and severely we should know that the time has come.

 

Birth pains are a sign – the Church is in pain.

Jesus told us the signs to look for.

John 16:21 “A woman when she is in travail hath sorrow, because her hour is come.”

 

  1. “And she brought forth a man-child, who was to rule all nations with a rod of iron:  and her child was caught up unto God, and to His throne.”

 

In vs. 2  “She being with child” – “Gaster”  Unborn.

In vs. 4  Giving birth – “Teknon” – New born – developing.

In vs. 5  Maturing – “Huios”  - “Man Child”

 

He will search among the Church and find those who are maturing that will make up the Bride.  All that are a part of the Church the Body of Christ will not be in the Bride.  As the Church, the Body of Christ will be taken from the body of believers, so will the Bride (the Man-Child) be taken from the Body of the Church.

 

  1. “And the woman fled into the wilderness”

 

For 3 ½ years – First part of the Tribulation.

 

Just as there are various levels of anointing, there are different depths of commitments and dedication that saints give to God.  Being baptized in the Holy Ghost and speaking in tongues does not necessarily qualify one to be a part of the “Man Child”, the Bride.  However, it does give them access to become a part.  In order to be a part of the “Man Child”, the Bride, there must be a willingness of dedication to the Lord and sincere submission to the Holy Sprit to sanctify and to cleanse “from all filthiness of the flesh and spirit, perfecting holiness in the fear of God.”

 

God has sent the Holy Spirit to get a Bride and He has given the Holy Sprit the responsibility of perfecting the Bride, and He is doing the work of perfection in the hearts of those who will yield to Him.

 

Furthermore, I believe we have in type in the life of Mary bringing Christ in the world by the Holy Ghost.  So God by the Holy Ghost is going to over shadow the Church in this end-time and He is going to bring forth a holy child – “The Man Child.”

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

The Great Red Dragon

Rev. 12:3 – 4

“And there appeared another wonder in heaven:  and behold a great red dragon, having seven heads and ten horns, and seven crowns upon his heads.

And his tail drew the third part of the stars of heaven, and did cast them to the earth:  and the dragon stood before the woman which was ready to be delivered, for to devour her child as soon as it was born.”

It is the history of a great hostile evil power with which the Church has and must contend with, and which, this evil force, is set to hinder the progress and destroy the hopes of the Church.  It is “another wonder,” “sign,” therefore it is to be interpreted after the same manner as the preceeding “sign.”

 

  1. The Dragon –

 

  1. The image of “sign” is that of “a dragon.”

We are to understand it to be the same terrible creature, delighting to attack, terrify and devour.  Especially the truth of God.  Matt. 13:19  I Peter 5:8  Luke 8:12  “Then cometh the devil, and taketh away the Word our of their heart.”

 

  1. It is “A Great…Dragon” –

While the picture in general is that of a dragon, it is altogether peculiar, and different from the common dragon.  It is “a great…dragon.”  “Megas” – “Great” – “Mighty” of external form, measure, of degree and intensity – mighty of rank, whether of persons or angels – of all those who had position of authority and power.

One in size and bulk exceeding the ordinary idea, and every dragon feature greatly magnified.  Here is a being, who has dared to set himself against the Almighty God, to try to overcome Him, and take over the universe, and take from the Creator the creation that He created.

The Dragon is “Great” in its accomplishment of evil.

 

  1. It Is “A Great Red Dragon.”

It is of a peculiar color – “Purrhos” fiery – bloody.  “Red” the color of blood – murder.

This was the color of the horse whose rider was to take peace out of the earth, and bring about a terrible war and bloodshed.

Rev. 6:4 “And there went out another horse that was red:  and power was given to him that sat thereon to take peace from the earth, and that they should kill one another:  and there was given him a great sword.”

Jesus said the devil was a murderer from the beginning.  John 8:44 “He was a murderer from the beginning, and abode not in the truth, because there is no truth in him.”

He has the power to kill.  Heb. 2:14

“The power of death.”
