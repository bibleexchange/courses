SO GREAT SALVATION

Hebrews 2:3

 

  1. It costs to neglect Salvation.
    1. It cost missing out in the Bride and possibly even going to Heaven.
    2. It cost us the rewards of faithfulness.
    3. It cost in that it effects many people’s lives.

 

Usually it is just the gentle, subtle breeze of worldliness, compromise, and carelessness that causes us to drift away, but drift away we do when we neglect so great salvation. This is a problem every true Christian faces, maybe even on a daily basis. It is a problem that cannot be ignored.

 

2Peter 2:13 And shall receive the reward of unrighteousness, as they that count it pleasure to riot in the day time. Spots they are and blemishes, sporting themselves with their own deceivings while they feast with you;

 

 

  1. It is a sin to neglect Salvation.
    1. Sin is missing the mark
    2. Sin is drifting
    3. Sin is not being anchored.

 

Heb 3:12 Take heed, brethren, lest there be in any of you an evil heart of unbelief, in departing from the living God.

 13 But exhort one another daily, while it is called To day; lest any of you be hardened through the deceitfulness of sin.

 

Romans 14:23 And he that doubteth is damned if he eat, because he eateth not of faith: for whatsoever is not of faith is sin.

 

James 4:17 Therefore to him that knoweth to do good, and doeth it not, to him it is sin.

 

  1. It is not worth it to neglect Salvation.
    1. The soul of a man is worth more than the whole world.
    2. The worth of a soul increases in value as We follow Christ.

Romans 6:23 For the wages of sin is death; but the gift of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.

1John 2:4 He that saith, I know him, and keepeth not his commandments, is a liar, and the truth is not in him.

 

Mat. 16:26 For what is a man profited, if he shall gain the whole world, and lose his own soul? or what shall a man give in exchange for his soul?

Mark 8:36 For what shall it profit a man, if he shall gain the whole world, and lose his own soul?
