After Judgment, Revival---The case of Ananias served several important ends.

I.            An emphatic testimony to truth.

a.  Falsehood in the world is a great barrier in the way of the Church. It is impossible to build without a foundation of truth.

b.  Unless the Church find truth it will not overcome the world; it will sink as in the mire.

                                         i.    Seek the truth

1.  Dig to the rock.

The death of Ananias and Sapphira is the arm of the Lord revealed to deliver the body of the Church in her youth from a consumption which, if not checked, it would bring thew whole church down to an early grave,

We learn here it is the work of God to cast out of the body the poison that would undermine life

His work to shield the Church from the power of her foes.

II.          Great fear came upon the Church.

a.  It is a healthful fear,

b.  a needed discipline.

c.   "Lord, is it I?"

d.  "Let him that thinketh he standeth,"

e.   It was Christ who said, "Remember Lot's wife."

Many centuries after the event, He directed that it should be kept in memory.

These dark monuments have obtained a place in the Word that liveth and abideth for ever, that their warning may be available in all nations and times.

III.        Believers were the more added.

a.  The judgment on false professors hastened conversions instead of hindered conversions.

b.  The terror of the Lord effectually persuaded men to take refuge in His mercy.

1.  Believers were added to the Lord;

a.  "Your life is hid with Christ in God."

The life of the branch depends on being in the vine; and its fairness and fruitfulness depends on its being interlaced in bonds of love with other branches.

2.  Multitudes were added.

a.  This is the common experience still.

A great number came at one time with a rush: a period of conviction, Again there is a revival, and again a time of coldness. Ask yourself, Has the tide risen in my time and carried in many on its wave, and am I left behind? When the heaving of the spiritual tide in our neighbourhood has ceased, the door is not shut. We are as welcome when we come one by one or a crowd.

And by the hands of the apostles were many signs and wonders wrought.—

Contrasts

The wrath of God by the mouth of the apostles had consumed two hypocrites;

but relief by the hands of the apostles happened to a great number of miserable people.

The Lord proved thereby that judgment is His strange work, but that He delights in mercy. And as He shows His zeal against the wicked, so He does not turn His heart away from the wretched.

Isa 28:21 For the LORD shall rise up as in mount Perazim, he shall be wroth as in the valley of Gibeon, that he may do his work, his strange work; and bring to pass his act, his strange act.

 

I. LESSON OF THE PRECEPT is in order that we may be right and do right. God tells us the same thing over and over again. A precept is a warning, a command, which says, "Take care," "Mind," "Keep in the way."

II. LESSON OF THE LINE. That is, in order that we may be right and do right, we must try over and over again. God helps all honest trying.

"Line upon line,"—that is the way we all learn and have learnt all that we know or are able to do. It is so in learning to write. So it is in learning arithmetic. So in learning to draw.

IV.        LESSON OF THE LITTLE. That is, in order that we may be right and do right we must not be discouraged if we do not make great advances; we must remember that it is "here a little and there a little." How slowly most great and valuable things grow!

The harvest does not spring up in a field in a night. A step at a time mounts the tallest ladder at last, but it must be a step at a time. How tall an oak is before it comes to its prime; yet if they could speak they would each say "I am growing on. Here a little and there a little" makes a learned man, a prosperous man, a useful and a good man. "Here a little and there a little" makes the perfect needle woman, and sets the most untidy house to rights at last.

How great some ships are!

What holds the mighty anchor which holds the ship in a storm?

 A cable.

And what is a cable made of?

Strands of metal entwined with strands of metal

Every rope made out of little threads.

So it is with the habits of life, good or bad; "here a little and there a little," so trifling as they seemed at first, they become at last such mighty and unconquerable affairs.
