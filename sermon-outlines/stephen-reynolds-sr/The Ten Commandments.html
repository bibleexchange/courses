The basis of the Ten Commandments has been set in stone, The Rock of Ages, the Chief corner stone, The stone of stumbling, Beginning and the End, the First and the Last.

 

The basis of the Ten Commandments is God Exists eternally and man must read what God gives and judge himself wisely.

 

The Ten Commandments work. God knows exactly what man needs to live a fulfilled and orderly life.

 

The Ten Commandments are based upon God Himself, upon His person and work, upon His knowledge of what man needs, upon His understanding and wisdom.

 

The Great Basis of the Ten Commandments:

  • The Person and Work of God Himself,

 

The Ten Commandments are to be the Basic Moral laws of society, which govern human relationships and the relationship between God and man. And they are to govern man down through history, every generation and every society

 

Ex 20:1 And God spake all these words, saying,

Ex 20:2 I am the LORD thy God, which have brought thee out of the land of Egypt, out of the house of bondage.

 

  1. God Exist Eternally
  2. God speaks plainly
  3. God keeps Covenant.
  4. God is our Savior, Deliverer “brought thee out”

 

God has given us the living Word of God, (the Lord Jesus Christ Himself) His own Son to demonstrate and show us how to live out His Written Word.; But more than an example he came as Savior, to deliver, to Redeem and sent the Holy Ghost to make us like Himself.

 

⇒ Through His written Word, the Holy Scripture

⇒ Through the Living Word, the Lord Jesus Christ, the Son of God Himself.

⇒ Through the Power of the Holy Ghost

 

God is the Lord--Jehovah, self-existent, eternal, and the creator of all being and power; therefore He commands us. He that creates gives law; and He is able to bear us out unto obedience, to reward us, and to punish our disobedience.

 

Deut. 5:1 And Moses called all Israel, and said unto them, Hear, O Israel, the statutes and judgments which I speak in your ears this day, that ye may learn them, and keep, and do them. 2 The LORD our God made a covenant with us in Horeb.3 The LORD made not this covenant with our fathers, but with us, even us, who are all of us here alive this day.

 

God condescends by His Grace in turning the commands into a covenant, that we might be encouraged to obey and inspired by the Exceeding great and precious  Promises.

 

Acts 7:8 And he gave him the covenant of circumcision: and so Abraham begat Isaac, and circumcised him the eighth day; and Isaac begat Jacob; and Jacob begat the twelve patriarchs.

 

God made this covenant of law with Israel, not with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob; God gave the covenant of circumcision (Acts 7:8) or Promise but not that of the Ten Commandments.

 

The light of divine revelation is shone gradually, and the children were given greater detail of God’s will than their father’s but the law was inferior; it cannot do what the Promise does and does not change the Promise at all.

 

Ex 23:7 Keep thee far from a false matter; and the innocent and righteous slay thou not: for I will not justify the wicked.

 

Rom. 4:5 But to him that worketh not, but believeth on him that justifieth the ungodly, his faith is counted for righteousness.

 

Gal. 3:17 And this I say, that the covenant, that was confirmed before of God in Christ, the law, which was four hundred and thirty years after, cannot disannul, that it should make the promise of none effect.

 

Ga l.3:22 But the scripture hath concluded all under sin, that the promise by faith of Jesus Christ might be given to them that believe.

 

Gal. 3:24 Wherefore the law was our schoolmaster to bring us unto Christ, that we might be justified by faith.

 

The Moral Law of God; the standard of God’s Righteousness shows men their sin and danger, and leads them to the Savior. The law condemns and prepares them for Pardon through the Redeemer.

 

The experience of passing from Law to Promise is by faith in Christ Jesus

  1. The Law restrains rebukes and keeps us as the ancient

guardian did for the boys.

 

2. The whole Law was designed to introduce Christ.

Point to Christ; Reveal the Redeemer.

  1. A Faith that believes God for Salvation.
  2. A Faith that receives the Righteousness of Christ.
  3. A Faith that can be rewarded.
