There is One to Help when Trouble is near

TEXT: Psalm 29:13

 

INTRODUCTION

 

Where are we at? We cannot live on yesterday’s manna.

Isaiah 29:13—Wherefore the Lord said, Forasmuch as this people draw near me with their mouth, and with their lips do honour me, but have removed their heart far from me, and their fear toward me is taught by the precept of men:

 

Ps 22:11—Be not far from me; for trouble is near; for there is none to help.

 

There is trouble near. There is more trouble than we realize and les help than even what we realize. He is nearer than our problem and trouble. We need more than lip service but a life that pleases and worships God. But many today fail not only in their life, but in the lip service as well.

 

  1. Trouble when Close to the Lord Always Ends in Victory

 

In Psalm 22, we can catch a vision of the cross. How can we look into the face of Jesus and what HE did on the cross and not be changed!?!

We always need the Lord’s help. Divine help is absolutely necessary                                                                               There are things that only the Lord can do. In the natural there is the nearness of trouble, and the absence of a helper.“for trouble is near; for there is none to help”

 

David cried out these words and Jesus cried out these words.

Psalm 22: 1—My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me? why art thou so far from helping me, and from the words of my roaring? 19—But be not thou far from me, O LORD: O my strength, haste thee to help me.

 

There is a difference between complaining and murmuring and being miserable. And a difference between bearing your own burden and bearing a burden that is not your own.

 

We should think upon the sufferings and resurrection of Christ, till we feel in our souls the power of his resurrection, and the fellowship of his sufferings. Christ suffered to teach us and allows our suffering to teach us. (“The fellowship of His sufferings”) There is a place of training and discipline and suffering. There some things we can only learn from the Lord.

 

Philippians 3:10—That I may know him, and the power of his resurrection, and the fellowship of his sufferings, being made conformable unto his death;

 

The crucified Son of David, Son of God, son of man continues to pour out his grievance and prayer.  We need much grace that while reading we may have fellowship with his sufferings.  May the blessed Holy Ghost lead us into a most clear and affecting sight of our Redeemer's woes and crucifixion.

Suffering with a close relationship with the LORD is always in victory. If you are close to God, then you have revelation and understanding that God is speaking to your heart.

 

2 Timothy 3:1; 4:1 Perilous times.

 

We don’t have to wonder at any tiem if God can still deliver from trouble. It won’t be without battles, but battles with a victory.

 

I Cor. 15:57—But thanks be to God, which giveth us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ.

 

Thanks be unto God. Give thanks! Don’t murmur and complain. Give no place to the devil. We can be thankful in the midst of our trouble. We can thankful for the Grace of God.

 

Learn how to be thankful to God without thanking Him for the problem. If we thank Him for the problem we will get use to having one!  God will heal you! He has made the provision and it is up to us to receive it.

 

Ephesians 1:8—Wherein he hath abounded toward us in all wisdom and prudence;

 

God can say the right thing at the right time to produce the right results.

 

  1. Trouble When Not Close to the Lord Always Ends in Defeat

 

Paul in Acts 28 told them it was DANGEROUS to sail. DO NOT SAIL today this way! God was saying DO NOT SAIL THIS WAY.

If all we have is a belief system, Our belief system will fail us.

 

2 Corinthians 1:4—Who comforteth us in all our tribulation, that we may be able to comfort them which are in any trouble, by the comfort wherewith we ourselves are comforted of God.

 

We must have God as our foundation and strong tower.

 

In the natural, trouble is near and in the spiritual, trouble is near. We need to learn to suffer with Joyfulness.

 

Revelation 12:2—And she being with child cried, travailing in birth, and pained to be delivered.

 

We are not going to hop into Heaven undisciplined.
