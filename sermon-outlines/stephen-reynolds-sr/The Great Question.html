The Great Question (Who is the Pearl of Great Price?)

Mt 13:45 Again, the kingdom of heaven is like unto a merchant man, seeking goodly pearls:46 Who, when he had found one pearl of great price, went and sold all that he had, and bought it.

Heb 11:6 But without faith it is impossible to please him: for he that cometh to God must believe that he is, and that he is a rewarder of them that diligently seek him.

Are you willing to give your all that you might partake of His All?

As a sinner He sought us and paid the price in full for our full salvation.                                                                  As a believer we are to search for Him with our whole heart.

Heb 8:6 ¶ But now hath he obtained a more excellent ministry, by how much also he is the mediator of a better covenant, which was established upon better promises.

Having discovered a costly pearl, the merchant had to decide what it was worth, how much he would pay for it. Then he "went and sold all that he had, and bought it." That is how the Lord bought the overcomer; He bought it at the full cost of His life at Calvary.

There is only one Pearl of Great Price in the parable because the Overcomers are unique.

It is a separated People an incomparable people in God's dealings with mankind.

The church is not merely spiritual Israel.                                                                                                                     The church is not merely the spiritual Family.                                                                                                            The church is the Body of Christ.                                                                                                                 The church as a whole will not attain in rewards as the Pearl of Great Price.

The fully matured Sons of God are distinct from all else and so highly esteemed on high that for the sake of this pearl the heavenly merchant paid His all, but all can benefit the same if we are obedient in all things.

The fully matured Sons of God are what Paul called "the purchased possession"

Eph 1:14 Which is the earnest of our inheritance until the redemption of the purchased possession, unto the praise of his glory.

Lu 21:28 And when these things begin to come to pass, then look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh.

Ro 8:19 For the earnest expectation of the creature waiteth for the manifestation of the sons of God.                                                                                                                                                              23 And not only they, but ourselves also, which have the firstfruits of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting for the adoption, to wit, the redemption of our body.

    • A  foreign object pierces the oyster, whereupon the little creature buries the irritation under layer after layer of secretion until at last it has produced a pearl.
      • The Great Pearl cannot be obtained until the oyster is dead.
      • The pearl is found in an oyster's grave.

Col 3:3 For ye are dead, and your life is hid with Christ in God.

This process points us directly to Calvary. There men injured God's Son and put Him, sorely pierced, in His grave. Yet out of that suffering and death God has produced the church. The church is God's answer to Calvary.

A pearl is silky white in color and time cannot rust or tarnish it.

In the days of the Lord, pearls were the special treasure of kings and were destined for the throne. It seems that Christ had all this in mind when he selected a pearl to be a vehicle for conveying mystery truth to His disciples. He had set His heart on a rare, unique, and priceless pearl. He had counted the cost and was about to pay the price. Before long He bought it. And as an oyster's pearl is salvaged from the stormy sea, so the true believer was taken from the restless sea of the Gentile world. Destined for the throne, the overcomer will be exhibited eternally as the prime example of God's sovereign grace .

Eph 2:7 That in the ages to come he might shew the exceeding riches of his grace in his kindness toward us through Christ Jesus.

The heavenly merchant bought the pearl with His all and that is what gives inestimable value to the overcomer. Christ considered it of such worth that He came from afar to seek it and gave His life to possess it.

Eph 5:25 Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it; 32 This is a great mystery: but I speak concerning Christ and the church.

The True church was a mystery, an entity undreamed of at the time the Lord spoke the parable of the costly pearl. The Jewish people knew nothing about the church since it was not revealed in the Old Testament. The truth would soon be revealed to the disciples.

After Pentecost the full truth about the church would be revealed. What is made plain in the New Testament was embodied in mystery in Matthew 13.                                                                                                       Lu 21:28 And when these things begin to come to pass, then look up, and lift up your heads; for your redemption draweth nigh.

When Matthew wrote, the church was well established on earth and the nation of Israel was about to be dissolved by the armies of Rome.

Mt 16:17 And Jesus answered and said unto him, Blessed art thou, Simon Barjona: for flesh and blood hath not revealed it unto thee, but my Father which is in heaven. 18 And I say also unto thee, That thou art Peter, and upon this rock I will build my church; and the gates of hell shall not prevail against it.
