Give me, your attention

  1. LOOK AT THE TEXT- look at it carefully, you will perceive the essential qualities which are necessary to any great success and prevalence in prayer.

 

According to our Savior’s description of prayer,

  • there should always be a definite objective

 

—“what things soever you desire.”

 

  • There should always be an earnest desire,
    • Without an earnest desire it is not prayer—it may be something like prayer, an outward form or the bare skeleton of prayer, but it is not the living word, the all-prevailing, Faith in the Almighty  God True prayer—is a fullness and an overflowing of desires!
    • There should always be Faith (an essential quality of successful prayer—“Have faith in God-believe that you receive them.”

Believe that God really hears and answers you!

 

a realizing expectation with a firm faith—“believe that you receive them.”

Not merely believe that you shall, (but believe that you do have them) receive them—count them as if they were received; reckon them as if you already had them, and act as if you had them—act as if you are sure you have them—“believe that you receive them, and you shall have them.”

 

The value of True prayer is increased with definite objectives

 

1Co 12:31 But covet earnestly the best gifts: and yet shew I unto you a more excellent way.

1Co 14:1 Follow after charity, and desire spiritual gifts, but rather that ye may prophesy.

 

Not rambling in saying prayers, after this, that, and the other, and we get nothing because we do not really desire anything specific. We speak about many subjects, but the soul does not concentrate itself upon any one objective.

Do you fall on your knees without thinking beforehand what you mean to ask God for?

Do you fall on your knees as a matter of habit, without any motion of your heart! You are like a man who goes window shopping, and does not know what to buy; he may, or may not make a purchase when he is there,

 

The Christian in true prayer will attain to real desire to the Glory of God.

 

Having your soul prepared by concentration on the word and self-examination, coming to God with a real request?

 

 “What is your petition, and what is your request, and it shall be done unto you.”

  • Imagine an archer shooting with his bow, and not knowing where the mark is!

Would he be likely to have success?

  • Imagine a ship on a voyage without the captain, having any idea of what he was looking for!

 

Would you expect that he would come back with the goods, with treasures of gold?

 

In everything you have a plan; you do not go to work without knowing that there is something that you planned to make.

 

How is it that you go to God without knowing what you design to have?

Always having an objective, you never find prayer to be a dull and heavy

chore!

 

I am persuaded that you would long for it!

 

You would say, I have something that I need. I will draw near to my God and ask Him for it!

I have a need, I want to have it satisfied; I long till I can get alone, that I may pour out my heart before Him, and ask Him for this great thing after which my soul so earnestly desires.

You will find it more helpful to your prayers, if you have some objectives at which you aim and also, if you have some persons whom you will mention. Do not merely plead with God for sinners in general, but always mention

some in particular. If you are a Sunday school teacher, don’t simply ask that your class may be blessed, but pray for your children definitely by name before the Most High.

Be simple and direct in your pleadings with God. When you pray to Him, tell Him what you need.

If you have not money enough, if you are in poverty, if you are in straits, state the case.

Come at once to the point—speak honestly with Him—

 

Abraham’s words were the best for Abraham, and yours

will be the best for you.

 

You need not study all the texts in Scripture to pray just as Jacob and Elijah did, using their expressions. If you do, you may imitate them but lack the soul that suggested and animated their words.

 

Pray fervently in your own words with Faith in God.

 

Speak plainly to God. Ask for what you need.

Name persons, name things, and make a straight aim at the objective of your supplications, and I am sure you will soon find that the weariness and dullness of your intercessions, will no more fall upon you—

 

You will always, by a little self-examination, see that there is a legitimate objective to pray for.

As you knock at Mercy’s door cry, “Give me, Lord, the desire of my heart!”

 

When you have done for yourself, plead for others!

 

Look on this Sodom, this city is like another Gomorrah lying before us.

 

Carry it constantly in your prayers before God and cry, “Oh that Portland  may live before You; that its sin may be stayed; that its righteousness may be exalted; that God  may get unto Himself much people out of this

city!”

Equally necessary is it with a definite objective for prayer that there should be an earnest desire for its attainment.

“Cold prayers,”

 

True prayer is knowing your faith is in God

How many of our prayers are an abomination unto the Lord!

 

Has Heaven’s greatest gift to man become to us a dry dead duty?

It was said of John Bradford, that he had a peculiar art in prayer, and when asked for his secret, he said, “When I know what I need, I always stay on that prayer until I feel that I have pleaded it with God,

and until God and I have had dealings with each other upon it. I never go on to another petition till I have gone through the first.”

 

Some men begin, “Our Father which are in Heaven, hallowed be Your name;” and before they have realized the greatness of this adoring thought—“hallowed be Your name”—they have begin to repeat the next words—“Your Kingdom come.”

“Do we really desire His Kingdom to come?

And while they are thinking of that, their voice is going on with, “Your will be done on earth as it is in Heaven.” So they jumble up their prayers and run the sentences together!

Oh, stay at each one till you have really prayed it!

Do not try to put two arrows on the string at once—they will both miss!

Let Him increase your accuracy as the arrow finds its mark, don’t just send out arrows you will get tired, discouraged miss led.

 

Look at the Lord’s Prayer itself.

What clear, sharp outlines there are in it.

There are certain definite mercies and they do not run into one another. There it stands, and as you look at the whole, it is a magnificent picture; not

confusion, but beautiful order. Be it so with your prayers!

 

Stay on one till you have prevailed with that, and then go on

to the next.

  • with fervent desires

An essential and Divine quality of firm faith in God.

Believe in prayer?

Believe in the power of prayer?

There are a great many Christians who do not. They think it is a good

thing, and they believe that sometimes it does wonders, but they do not think that prayer, real prayer, True Prayer is always successful. They think that its effect depends upon many other things, but that it has not any essential quality or power

Our objective today is to pray for revival of our own conviction that prayer is the grandest power in the entire universe—that it has a more

Omnipotent force than electricity, worldly attractions, gravitation or any other of those secret forces which men do not understand!

 

True Prayer has a more powerful influence over the entire universe than any of the laws of matter! When a man really prays, it is not a question whether God will hear him or not—He will hear him, He must hear him—not because of the prayer, but because of the promise.

God has promised to hear prayer, and He will keep His promise!

He is the most high and true and living God,

He cannot deny Himself.

Think on this! That you, a man, can stand here and speak to God, and through God move the world!

Yet when your prayer is heard,

  • Creation will not be disturbed;
  • Providence will not be disarranged for a single moment!

Yet your prayer will have effect everything!

 

True Prayer will speak the decrees and purposes of God to be fulfilled daily;

 

The prayers of God’s people are but God’s promises breathed out of living hearts, and those promises are the decrees

 

The decrees become your prayer. Your prayers are the decrees,

Every prayer that is Inspired of the Holy Spirit unto your soul is as Omnipotent and as eternal

as that decree which said, “Let there be light, and there was light.”

Or as that decree which chose His people, and

ordained their redemption by the precious blood of Christ!

You have power in prayer, and you stand today among the

most powerful ministers in the world.

You have power over angels; they will fly at your will.

You have power over fire, wind water and the elements of earth;

you have power to make your voice heard beyond the stars!

Where the thunders die out in silence, your voice shall wake the echoes of eternity. The ears of God, Himself, shall listen,

and the hands of God shall yield to your cry, “Your will be done,” and His will shall be done!

When you can plead His promise, then your will is His will!

Daniel, the 10th Chapter, prayer seems to be laid bare. Daniel is on his

knees in prayer, and Michael the Archangel comes to him. He talks with him, and tells him that as soon as Daniel began to set his heart to understand and to chasten himself before God, his words were heard and the Lord had dispatched the angel. Then he tells him in the most business-like manner in the world, “I should have been here before,

but the Prince of Persia withstood me, nevertheless the prince of your nation helped me, and I am come to comfort and instruct you.” See now, God breathes the desire into our hearts, and as soon as the desire is there, before we call, He begins to answer! Before the words have got half way up to Heaven, while they are yet trembling on our lips—knowing

the words we mean to speak—He begins to answer them—sends the angel! The angel comes, and brings down the needed blessing. Why the thing is a revelation if you could see it with your eyes!

 

I believe there is as much reality in a Christian’s prayer as in a lightning flash and the utility and excellency of the prayer of a Christian may be just as sensibly known as the power of the lightning flash when it tears the tree, breaks off its branches, and splits it to the very root!

Prayer is not a fancy or fiction! It is a real, actual thing compelling the universe, binding the Laws of God themselves in

fetters, and engaging the High and Holy One to listen to the will of His servant!

 

We need a realizing assurance in prayer—to count over the mercies to be sure that we have them! To act as if we had them! When you have asked for your daily bread, no more to be disturbed

with care, but to believe that God has heard you, and will give it to you! When you have taken the case of your sick child before God—to believe that the child will recover, to the Glory of God “I know He has heard me now. I will stand on my watchtower;

I will look for my God, and hear what He will say to my soul.” Were you ever disappointed yet, Christian, when you prayed in faith and expected the answer?
