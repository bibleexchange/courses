Let these Words be Engrained in our Spirit

2Timothy 2:15 Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth.

2Timothy 2:23 But foolish and unlearned questions avoid, knowing that they do gender strifes.

Proverbs 26:4 Answer not a fool according to his folly, lest thou also be like unto him.                                                                             5 Answer a fool according to his folly, lest he be wise in his own conceit.

Pr 9:7 He that reproveth a scorner getteth to himself shame: and he that rebuketh a wicked man getteth himself a blot.                                                                                                                                                                                              8 Reprove not a scorner, lest he hate thee: rebuke a wise man, and he will love thee.

Pr 12:16 ¶ A fool's wrath is presently known: but a prudent man covereth shame.

Pr 13:1 ¶ A wise son heareth his father's instruction: but a scorner heareth not rebuke.

Pr 16:32 ¶ He that is slow to anger is better than the mighty; and he that ruleth his spirit than he that taketh a city.

Pr 19:11 ¶ The discretion of a man deferreth his anger; and it is his glory to pass over a transgression.

1Co 4:14 I write not these things to shame you, but as my beloved sons I warn you.

1Co 15:34 Awake to righteousness, and sin not; for some have not the knowledge of God: I speak this to your shame.

1Ti 5:1 ¶ Rebuke not an elder, but intreat him as a father; and the younger men as brethren;

 Jude 1:9 Yet Michael the archangel, when contending with the devil he disputed about the body of Moses, durst not bring against him a railing accusation, but said, The Lord rebuke thee.

NEVER use the words of the world.

Never use the Word of God lightly from mere memory.
