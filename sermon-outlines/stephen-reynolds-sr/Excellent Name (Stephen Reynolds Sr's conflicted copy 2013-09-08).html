The Unseen Way is fulfilled in Christ

 

Mt 7:12 ¶ Therefore all things whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them: for this is the law and the prophets.

13 Enter ye in at the strait gate: for wide is the gate, and broad is the way, that leadeth to destruction, and many there be which go in thereat:

14 Because strait is the gate, and narrow is the way, which leadeth unto life, and few there be that find it.

 

  1. We must be ruled by Righteousness unto Holiness
    1. We must  have “fruit unto holiness”
      1. Rom. 6:3-6,11-23; Jn.15:2,5-8; Zech.3

        1. The broad wall is likened unto an unchanging Law.  Rom.8:2

    1. Enter ye in at the strait gate (Divine Righteousness)  Heb.4:16;
    2. Turn from the wide gate (self-righteousness),
    3. search for and find and enter the strait gate.

2.  There are two ways – go the narrow way

3.  There are two ends – be led unto life

4.  There are two travellers – few find life

5.  There are two decisions –way of righteousness by faith

 

The wide gate is (way that seems right --self-righteousness),  

The narrow gate is Christ (Divine Righteousness)

Php 3:10,18-21; Ro 8:29; Eph 1:19,20; Heb. 2:9-10; 11:27

The wide gate stands open. so wide-

  1. Not a single hindrance to entering the wide gate.

Nothing keeps a person from entering it.  A person naturally stands before its wide open doors blind to its dangers.

  1. Many people can enter and are entering, plenty of company in entering a wide gate.

3)  It appears to be the only gate to enter

There is so much noise around it.

There is so many people entering it.

 There is so much activity going on.

Most think not of another gate, much less look for one.

4)  The wide gate is wide enough to include all:

All philosophies all beliefs no matter how extreme, all appetites and passions, all liberties  all sin and selfishness. The gate is swung wide open so that any and all can enter.

The narrow gate is so narrow it cannot be seen.

1)  We must turn from the wide gate: turn from following the crowd; turn from the pull of the activity and attractions around and beyond the gate.

2)  We must search for the narrow gate and seek it out diligently by faith in His Word.

3)  We must enter immediately when we find the narrow gate. However, it is hard to enter for it is very narrow.

The narrow gate – is CHRIST (Divine Righteousness, life Everlasting-Eternal Grace/Truth- heaven)

How do you enter this strait gate?   ‘YE’--- ALONE

1)  We must repent: turn from the wide gate, the crowd, the activity and attractions around the gate not just the world but your will and turn to CHRIST

Lu 13:3 “except ye repent, ye shall all likewise perish”               Ac 3:19;17:30;Mt 11:12; Lu 16:16  every man presseth into it

               Mr 10:21; John 16:33; 2Ti 2:12; 2Ti 3:12; Re 3:10          

  1. We must confess we don’t know the strait gate

Enter the strait gate (CHRIST); the location (belief to faith); the activity around it (righteousness, good works-obedience unto holiness and the attraction of it (hope, spiritual qualities, real life, beatitudes, fruit, spices, gifts), and we need help to find it.

3)  We must be born again:

How does a person enter the narrow gate?

  1. Humble yourself---A person must stoop and bow down: become as a little child
  2. A person must strip down: put off the old man
  3. A person must obey God not (struggle: struggle) against the flesh    Beatitude not attitude.

 

The broad way seems to be the easy way, the right way, the only way, but is the way of the flesh, the way of the world.

The narrow way seems to be the hard way but is the way of heaven, the way of faith, hope, and righteousness.

 

The broad way is very broad and ever so easy to travel.

1)  A person can travel along with few hindrances.   The world has many attractions and invitations .

2)  The many attractions stir the mind and the flesh, visual values and sensual desires, cultural interests and pleasurable stimulators.

SOS. 3:2 ; Na 2:4

 

The narrow way is very narrow, it is very difficult to travel.

  1. The way (CHRIST) is the way of Holiness.

The temptation to return to the broad way is strong. Self-denial and the will to obey are needed to overcome the flesh and the fear.

 

  1. The way is unseen and past finding out on your own. It takes not a strong will but a surrendered will not mere determination but an uncommon faith not personal sacrifice to stick to the road but being led by the Holy Ghost

The broad way ends. but the traveller does not pay any attention to its end. The road appears to be so broad and long as he travels day by day that he figures he will know when he comes to the end. The broad road has so many side attractions to draw and embrace the traveller's attention that he runs upon the end without knowing it. He fails to see that the end is immediately ahead; therefore, he runs off the end without having prepared himself to face the destruction.

The narrow way does not end. It leads to a glorious world that is yet unseen, but it opens up to everlasting life to the traveller.

 

Many travellers desire the crowd and the attractions of the broad way so much that they are willing to run the risk of wrecking among so much traffic.

Some seek the strait gate and never enter it.

1)  Some come upon it, but they decide the way is too narrow for them. It requires too much effort, discipline, and self-denial to continue.

2)  Some seek selfishly. They carelessly walk right by it without ever seeing it.

3)  Some seek half-heartedly. They still look back to the wide gate so as not to lose sight of it. They miss the narrow way because of insincerity and lack of discipline.

4)  Some seek and find by grace but try to continue on by their own strength and wisdom.

 

The strait gate/narrow way is hard to find. But it is not shut. It is open to all who seek and find it. The invitation is out to whomsoever will enter it.   Mat.7:12-29
