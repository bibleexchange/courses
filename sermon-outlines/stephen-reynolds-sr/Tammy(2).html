Eph 4:7 But unto every one of us is given grace according to the measure of the gift of Christ.

  1. Jesus Christ gives us the grace to use our gifts.

Everyone of us is gifted by Christ—gifted with a highly specialized gift.

This is significant, for it means that our gift is the gift of Christ.

Living water is alive.

To the Jew, living water was running water that was always flowing and moving along, such as a creek fed by springs or a lake with both an inflow and an outflow. Dead water was stagnant water such as ponds or pools that were always sitting still with no inflow or outflow. However, when Jesus spoke of "living water," He meant much more than living streams and lakes.

  1. Living water is "of God."
    1.  It is of Him who is living, always has and always will be living.
    2. The water that God gives is the most alive water there is. No other water, no matter how alive it may be considered, can compare with the living water that is of God.

For with thee is the fountain of life: in thy light shall we see light" (Psalm 36:9).

  1. Living water is "the gift" of God. The word "gift" means it is freely given, is
    1. not earned and is not deserved.

Ho, every one that thirsteth, come ye to the waters, and he that hath no money; come ye, buy, and eat; yea, come buy wine and milk without money and without price" (Isaiah 55:1).

For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: not of works, lest any man should boast" (Ephes. 2:8-9).

 

  1. Living water is given by asking for it.
    1. Jesus said: "If thou knewest...thou wouldest have asked."
    2. The woman had never received living water because she had never known about it and had never asked for it.
    3. It was now available simply by asking for it.
  2. living water is from a Person much greater than a religious father. The woman saw clearly that Jesus was making an unusual claim. She did not yet understand what the claim was, but she knew He was getting at something. She noticed He had no leather pouch with which to draw water, so she asked two significant questions.
