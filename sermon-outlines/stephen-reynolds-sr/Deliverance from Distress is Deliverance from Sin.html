Between 1729 and 1746, Sarah Edwards gave birth to a child virtually every other year. This means that she spent nearly half of her time over the course of 17 years in some stage of pregnancy.

 

Jonathan Edwards made a careful list of the birth of every child, noting the day, the hour, and the conditions into which each came into the world. The order was as follows: Sarah, Jerusha, Esther, Mary, Lucy, Timothy, Susannah, Eunice, Jonathan, Elizabeth, Pierpont. By a strange coincidence, 6 of the 11 children were born on the Sabbath.
