I. Definitions necessary in understanding the Rapture

A. Rapture

The word "rapture" does not occur in the original languages. However, the Greek verb Harpazo occurs 13 times in the N.T. and is translated "caught up."

 

The Latin equivalent used in this sense is Rapio.

 

Harpazo is a seizing, a carrying off by force, a snatching or catching away speedily.

 

Paul was (Harpzo) caught up into paradise (II Cor. 12:2). The

saints will be (Harpazo) caught up...in the clouds (I Thess. 4:17).

 

B. Tribulation

Jesus spoke of the great tribulation such as has not been from the beginning until now (Matthew 24:21).

 

The tribulation is described as a period of great persecution—first 31/2 years and judgment last 31/2 years.

(Matthew 24:9) The tribulation is described as God's judgmental wrath on the ungodly(Matthew 24:29). The

tribulation is described in Revelation as a time of Satanic influence through the Anti-Christ. Daniel speaks of this

“tribulation” as the "seventieth week
