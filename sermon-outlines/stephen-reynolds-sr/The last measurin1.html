The First Principles of the Oracles of God

The Duty of Self-Denial

Self-denial is the first principle of Christianity! It is the life-blood which must run through the whole soul. Self-denial is not learned at an academy, but from the oracles of Scripture.

Luke 9:23 And he said to them all, If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow me.

Proverbs 19:11 The discretion of a man deferreth his anger; and it is his glory to pass over a transgression.

Morality is a good Jacob's staff to walk with among men

Morality without Holiness is a bad Jacob's ladder to climb up to heaven!

Heb 12:14 Follow peace with all men, and holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord:                                                                                                                                         Isa 64:6 ¶ But we are all as an unclean thing, and all our righteousnesses are as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have taken us away.

A Christian must deny SINFUL FASHIONS. Romans 12:2, "Be not conformed to this world." That is—do not be conformed to the fashions and mode of it. If the old Christians were to rise out of their graves—our new fashions might frighten them into their graves again!

There may be going to heaven without comfort—but there is no going there without self-denial.
