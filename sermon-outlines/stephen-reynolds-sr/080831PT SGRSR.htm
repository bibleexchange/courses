August 31, 2008, Sunday Pm

TITLE: The Unspeakable Gift

TEXT: I Corinthians 9:15

 

  • A gift
  • An unspeakable gift.
  • 2Co 9:15  Thanks be unto God for his unspeakable gift.
  • It is about Him, the giver.
  • There is a false that goes along with the real.
  • Because Christ died on the cross, he has made a provision for us to be saved.
  • There is a whole lot more to receive because of who He is. Salvation, heaven and escaping hell would be enough but he has so much more.

 

2Co 12:4

How that he was caught up into paradise, and heard unspeakable words, which it is not lawful for a man to utter. {lawful: or, possible}

 

Amazing grace.

 

2Co 9:14 And by their prayer for you, which long after you for the exceeding grace of God in you.

 

  • Amazing to save us and amazing to keep us saved.
  • Thank God for His exceeding great grace. That lifts us up not just out of sin, but into a palce where we may be established with peace in our mind. Sound mind.
  • Without the gift of grace thee is no peace.

 

I Peter 1:8      ßJoy Unspeakable

Whom having not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see him not, yet believing, ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory:

 

3Jo 1:2

Beloved, I wish above all things that thou mayest prosper and be in health, even as thy soul prospereth.

 

Don’t ever lose sight of the unspeakable gift.

 

He shows to us things about who He is and it is wonderful.

 

In his presence is fullness of joy and at his right hand are pleasure For evermore.

 

It is a gift of mercy.

 

 

2Co 8:9

For ye know the grace of our Lord Jesus Christ, that, though he was rich, yet for your sakes he became poor, that ye through his poverty might be rich.

 

2 Cor. 5:21

For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him.

 

We have no righteousness of our own.

 

Many love the presence of God, but don’t know and appreciate the nature of God.

 

2Co 9:8

And God is able to make all grace abound toward you; that ye, always having all sufficiency in all things, may abound to every good work:

 

There is a responsibility that comes with the grace of God.

 

Thanks. 2Co 9:15  Thanks be unto God for his unspeakable gift.
