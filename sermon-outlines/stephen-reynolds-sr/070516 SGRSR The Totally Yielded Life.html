Rev. Stephen G. Reynolds, Sr.

May 16, 2007

 

TITLE: The Totally Yielded Life

TEXT: Philippians 2:1-30

 

Foundational work is done under the ground. A lot of work in preparation.

 

vv.1-4 We are at a decision point. A decision time.

 

This context deals with a total yielded life.

 

You can’t take these promises and make them work. You must come to the end of yourself.

 

The yielded life is useful. As we surrender, God can operate, and we can act in response to what God has done.

 

At the end of myself other become more important to me.

 

vv.5-11 The supreme example of yielding

v.6 thought it not robbery—something to grasp for. Not taking something that didn’t belong to him, but it was HIS. In eternity past, Jesus had the fullness of power, but laid it aside. As Jesus the man he was a fully surrendered yielding himself ot the father. You cannot reach the things in God by ‘reaching after’ but by God’s doing. Striving has its place, but not until you have yielded yourself to the Lord.

 

We can’t do it.

 

These promises come as we yield to Him and do what He says.

 

He did not grasp at something to make it his, but was honored and ordained by the Father. He gave up everything and yielded it all.

 

vv.12-16a A challenge bringing us to a decision point. A message to learn of the completely surrendered life

 

v.14 this verse is not randomly placed here, but is part of the test. “Do all things without murmurings and disputings:” This is the very thing that you will fight with in your flesh until you are totally yielded. NO SELFISHNESS.

 

If God has said something that is what is going to help us and not paying attention to the voices of opposition.
