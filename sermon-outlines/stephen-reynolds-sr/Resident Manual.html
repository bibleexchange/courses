Introduction

 

Rev. James R. Reynolds, Sr., founded the Deliverance Center in 1967, with the aid of several volunteers. In time, Rev. Reynolds' own family members have become a part of daily functions at the Deliverance Center along with several very dedicated volunteers. Initially, the Deliverance Center helped and worked for the transformation of alcoholics and drug addicts. This focus is especially seen in its name, as being a place of deliverance from alcoholism or drug addiction. The Deliverance Center was also established as a local church at that time. Then, in 1969, it expanded to include an educational facility. The first of its schools, Deliverance Bible Institute, was established to train young adults for Christian service in a three-year course. Christian Academy of Portland was added in 1979, which extended the student body and curriculum to include grades K-12.

 

This handbook has been prepared to assist each member of our large family at the Deliverance Center. As each "family member" carefully observes the information in this handbook, each will find residency at the Deliverance Center most productive and rewarding. Please understand that in a large family, 'others' is the key word. To be able to maintain our family environment, consideration for others is important. It will keep harmony in the home. The rules and guidelines in this handbook were made in an effort to be fair with everyone. Each resident must recognize that it is a privilege to be part of this family and must show a willingness to be part of this family. One's willingness is especially indicated when it is expressed by a cheerful attitude towards authority, obeying the rules, and performing one's duties.

ABSENCE FROM GROUNDS

 

 

1. Permission must be obtained from those in authority to leave grounds for any and all reasons.  If permission is granted, resident must sign out.

 

2. It is the resident's responsibility to see that his or her duties are taken care of and that the Duty Supervisor has been informed.

 

3. Residents will be expected to plan their days in advance so they can get necessary authorization while one of the authorizing staff is available.(Exceptions for urgencies are understood but once or twice in a month is enough.  No exceptions will be made Sundays.)

 

4. Residents may be absent from the grounds only at times permitted by the rules and schedule.

 

5. Each resident must be specially authorized before they may work. If authorized, residents may work Monday through Saturday; but will not be allowed to work on Sunday.

 

6. To be gone over night or to miss any services, special permission must be obtained from the Pastor and confirmed by the resident authority.

 

 

 

ALCOHOL & DRUGS, ETC.

 

1. No alcoholic beverages of any kind shall be bought, stored, or used, on or off the premises.

 

2. No narcotics will be possessed, bought, sold, or used in any form, on or off the premises.

 

3. No tobacco products will be possessed, bought, sold, or used in any form, on or off the premises.

 

4. No medicine is to be given to any resident by another resident.  No resident is allowed to keep medicine in his or her room, it must be checked in and out with

designated staff.

 

 

AUTHORIZATION

 

1. Only authorized staff creates and gives directions.  No resident will make controlling decisions for anyone except with regard to his or her own person and

possessions.  In the event a resident is asked to direct the behavior of another resident, situation, etc. (e.g., baby-sitting, watch duty, phone duty, dog-sitting, etc.),

he or she must have special authorization.

 

2. Authorization for telephone use, leaving the grounds or for special permission, etc., will be obtained when authorizing staff is available. NOTE:  Please be considerate of the time and privacy of staff and faculty members.(3 Minute limitation)

 

 

BATHROOMS

 

1. Be as brief as possible.  Remember OTHERS (10 Minutes)

 

2. No showers or baths before 6:00 AM Monday through Saturday. ABSOLUTELY NO SHOWERS OR BATHS SUNDAY MORNING.

 

3. Bathtubs, showers, floors, walls, ceiling and drains will be left clean (from hair, toiletries, face cloths, and towels, water etc.).

 

4. Clothes will not be left in the bathroom or soaking in a sink or bathtub.

 

 

 

CHAPEL SERVICES/DEVOTIONS

1. All Residents, except those who receive special permission to do otherwise, are required to attend all chapel services and devotions.

 

2. Permission to be absent from chapel or devotions for any reason must be obtained from the Pastor before the absence occurs.

 

3. There will be no passing of notes or Texts during chapel or devotions. Residents are expected to reverence the chapel services and devotions as they would any other church service and keep cell phones off.(not even on vibrate)

 

4. Residents will sit in the right section facing the pulpit for chapel and devotions. When C.A.P and/or D.B.I students are present, residents will sit within three rows directly behind the last row of students, but not in the last row. When C.A.P and/or D.B.I students are not present, residents will sit where directed to do so at the time.

 

 

 

 

 

CHORES

  1. Whether assigned verbally or in writing, each resident is responsible to have his or her chore done at the stated or posted time. Verbal times will supercede posted times unless otherwise directed.

 

  1. Each resident will be assigned a chore and is responsible to have his chore done at the posted time. Sunday chores are generally done Sunday afternoon between services and are for the purpose of tidying or sprucing up each area.

 

  1. No resident may substitute for another except with permission. The substituting resident must notify the Duty Supervisor when there is a duty change or substitution.

 

  1. Duties must be attended to and the Supervisor advised before resident leaves the grounds.

 

  1. If a resident is sick, he will be responsible to have the Duty Supervisor notified of his absolute inability to perform his duties.

 

  1. Residents will return all cleaning tools to the utility closest after each use.

 

  1. No dirty cleaning water will be dumped into the kitchen sink. Use a flush; or, if it is biodegradable, dump it out back away from travel areas.

 

 

CHURCH SERVICES

 

1. Residents must attend Wednesday night service, Sunday School, Sunday church services, Chapel service, morning and evening devotions, and any special services sponsored by the Deliverance Center. Residents may be required to attend special services supported by the Deliverance Center.

 

2. Passing notes is not allowed in services, except those directed to the platform.

 

3. Permission must be granted from the Pastor to stay out of church or chapel for any reason.

 

4. Residents must sit within the front four rows of the two middle sections.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

DINING ROOM & KITCHEN

1. Meals:

Breakfast—Will be over by 8:45 AM (No exceptions on Sunday)

Lunch—Will be served about 12:30 PM (Except Sunday; served after AM service)

Dinner—Will be served 5:00 PM (None served Sunday)

 

2. No resident is to loiter (eating, sitting, standing, walking, etc.) in the kitchen at any time. Residents will only be in the kitchen to perform duties, or by staff request.

 

3. No kitchen or dining utensils are to be taken to rooms or outside.

 

4. All meals and snacks must be eaten in the dining room.

 

5. No one is to walk around eating or drinking.

 

6. No one is to chew gum in classes or church. Dispose of gum properly and do not leave it stuck anywhere.

 

7. Please do not bother the staff members while they are eating. If they are at your table, they will converse with you in a congenial manner (Mark 6:31b).

 

8. Residents may have toast, water and/or coffee between meals if they can do it without interfering with the kitchen staff. If milk is needed for coffee, plan your drinking habits according to the availability of someone who is specially authorized to get into the refrigerator.

 

9. Refrigerator(s) and freezer(s) are off limits to all residents at all times unless special authorization has been given to put goods in one of them or to get something out of them. (Care must be taken to close each door immediately after getting the intended item). At no time will any refrigerator or freezer be opened to "just see" what is in it.

 

10. When CA.P. students and staff are using the kitchen and dining area, residents are expected to stay outside of these areas until C.A.P. has left.

DORMITORY

 

 

1. Male residents are not allowed in the girls' dorm and female residents are not allowed in the men's dorm, except by request of staff.

 

2. Residents will not put holes in the walls or mark them in any way.

 

3. All furniture in the room has been arranged for the best advantage to all concerned.  No rearrangement of this furniture is to be made without special permission from the Dorm Supervisor.

 

4. Each resident is responsible for keeping his/her own room neat and clean at all times.  Rooms will be inspected. There is a checklist of requirements to pass inspection in each room.

 

5. All rooms must be left unlocked unless the Dorm Supervisor has two keys and each occupant of the room has one.

 

6. Lights will not be left on in an unoccupied room unless there is fluorescent lighting and someone will use the room within 15 minutes.

 

7. The use of electrical appliances is prohibited unless special permission has been given.  Permission will be given only for the following appliances: Clocks, razors,

hair dryers, sewing machines, irons, lamps, radios, tape players, CD players, and record players.  The last four are allowed only at specific times along with permission.

 

8. No stoves or heating devices of any type may be used in the dormitory rooms.

 

9. No food or cooking of any type is permitted in the dormitory rooms except sealed candy and sealed drinks.

 

10. Incense or candles may not be burned in rooms.

 

11. No heating lamps are to be used in rooms.  No more than 60-watt bulbs are to be used in rooms, and no more than one light on per person in a room.

 

12. Residents who are sick will not be allowed in the room of another resident.  The sick must stay in his or her room.

 

13. Residents are not allowed to go into the room of a sick person, unless it is their room or they are praying or ministering in some way, with permission.

DORMITORY (cont’d)

 

14. There must be no wrestling or roughhousing in the dorms.

 

15. Residents must be in bed with lights out by the time the curfew bell finishes ringing.

(10:00PM)

 

16. Lights must stay out.  There will be no whispering, talking, studying, eating, drinking, etc. after the (10:00PM) bell.

 

17. Residents returning from outside ministry or for any other reason after the curfew bell will not visit with other residents.  A dim light may be used when preparing for

bed.  Resident should retire within 15 minutes.

 

18. No one is allowed to go into the room of another resident and borrow something without that resident's permission.

 

19. No resident should enter an unoccupied room of another resident.

 

20. Residents will not enter staff's rooms unless invited.

 

21. Residents must not run in the building.

A. Floor Chore

Sweep the floors in the kitchen, dining room, nursery, main hallway, and wash each floor. Scrub floors with nylon bristle brush; remove all blacks marks, stuck-on food, gum, tar, grease, and dirt. If this chore is started before 6:00 PM Saturday, then it may need to be wet mopped before curfew.

 

B. Stairs and Throw Rug Chore

Sweep and wash front and back stairs in main hallway going to the second floor. Take all throw rugs that are in the kitchen, dining room, main hallway, and church vestibule outside and "shake" them out. Return them to their respective places. DO NOT DO THIS CHORE WHILE OTHERS MAY BE SLEEPING IN LADIES’ DORM OR IN THE APARTMENT. Scrub stairs with nylon bristle brush; remove all blacks marks, stuck-on food, gum, tar, grease, and dirt. Clean windows in dining room, and in back door. Clean the woodwork in the main hallway (front and back): baseboard, trim, doors, and walls. It may be necessary to use a toothbrush to reach hard-to-get-to areas. Polish both banisters with furniture polish.

 

C. Kitchen Chore

Scrub clean all kitchen counters, refrigerators, stove and oven (especially the sides of the stove), and the inside and outside of the microwave. Move items on the floor and clean under and behind them. It may be necessary to use a toothbrush to reach hard-to-get-to areas. Clean the windows, walls and woodwork: baseboard, trim, and doors.

 

 

 

D. Dishes Chore

Prep dishes and fill the dishwasher throughout the day. Put away all clean dishes. Wash all pans and items that are not washed in the dishwasher. Clean all items that cannot be washed in the dishwasher and all small appliances, such as: toaster, mixer, coffee maker, etc. Visually inspect for cleanliness all dishes, containers, pots, pans, and utensils in the cupboards and drawers and organize each area. Wash table/tablecloth and arrange items on the table neatly, or remove them.

 

E. Trash Chore

Empty all trashcans and put in new trash liners. (Kitchen, dining room, nursery, hallways, and restrooms in basement) Lawns and back yard need to be cleared of paper trash and waste

from consumable products such as plastic drink containers, etc. Clean all dirty trashcans.

Thoroughly inspect lawns, backyard, and driveways; and. remove all trash. Be sure to ask proper authority before discarding anything that may be someone's property.

 

F. Vacuum Church Chore

Pick up all loose items in the sanctuary-books, pens, pencils, clothes, and coats, etc.-and put songbooks and Bibles on table in vestibule and place them neatly on the coat rack. Vacuum sanctuary and platform. DO NOT move anything on the platform or remove anything from the platform. Inspect chairs for cleanliness. Make sure they are free from bits of trash, lint, hair, gum ,food, and burdocks. Be sure to vacuum between each row as well as the common aisles. Arrange seats according to the established pattern.

 

G. Laundry & Boiler Room Chore

Sweep floors and stairs. Pick up all loose clothes and trash. Wipe down machines. Dust boiler, pipes, and plumbing fixtures. Wash windows. Knock down cobwebs. Clean the food storage room: dust shelves and all items on them; wash window; sweep and wash floors.

 

H. Men's Dorm Restroom Chore

Wipe down sinks, counters, mirrors, shower stall, and toilet tank. Using a toilet brush, clean the entire toilet bowl, inside and out. Sweep, wash, and disinfect the floor. Empty the trash. Clean window, walls, and woodwork. Clean and disinfect shower and wall around toilet bowl.

 

I. Church Restroom & Vestibule Chore

Wipe down sinks, counters, mirrors, and toilet tanks. Using a toilet brush, clean both toilet bowls, inside and out. Sweep, wash, and disinfect the floors. Vacuum upper landing, throw rugs, and steps. Empty the trash. Clean window, walls, and woodwork. Clean and disinfect wall around toilet bowls. Be sure all paper products are sufficiently replenished for Sunday usage.

 

J. Men's Dorm Chore

Vacuum all hallways and stairs in men's dorm and the landing at the foot of the stairs by the kitchen. Report any articles or items that are in these areas. Dust and tidy tape and book table. Polish handrail, and the door and trim to balcony. Clean hall window, walls, and woodwork.

 

K. Church Basement Chore (Open Area, Classrooms)

Sweep open area and hallway, and vacuum classrooms. Wash floor in open area every 2 to 3 weeks. Wash travel area to classrooms and hallway floor weekly. Clean windows (glass), clean windowsills and wall "step outs. "Arrange all furniture in an acceptable manner (SEE Duty Supervisor). Dust and polish woodwork, podiums, and desks, as needed. Wash walls every 6 to 8 weeks. Empty trash containers when full. DO NOT CLEAN MARKER BOARDS WITHOUT DUTY SUPERVISOR’S AUTHOR-IZATON AND INSTRUCTION.

 

L. Yard Chore

Thoroughly inspect lawns, backyard, and driveways; and, remove all trash. Be sure to ask proper authority before discarding anything that may be someone's property. Growth season: Mow and trim the lawn. Snow season: Keep walkways clear, sanded, and/or salted.

  • Bottom tray: plates, pitchers, bowls, any other large items that are not too big for the dishwasher
  • Door tray: silverware, sharp knives, cooking utensils, etc.

C. If the dishwasher is full of unwashed dishes, then place one heaping teaspoon of detergent (stored under the kitchen sink) in each soap cup (bottom right of door); close the door, and carefully turn the knob clockwise until the cycle begins. Do not change any of the other settings.

 

6. If the dishwasher is full of washed dishes, then dry them and put them in their proper places. When in doubt—ASK.
