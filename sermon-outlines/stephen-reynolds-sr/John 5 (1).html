John 5:19 Then answered Jesus and said unto them, Verily, verily, I say unto you, The Son can do nothing of himself, but what he seeth the Father do: for what things soever he doeth, these also doeth the Son likewise.

The crucial importance of this point. Jesus said, "Verily, verily"; that is, listen, listen. Pay close attention to what is said.

        1. He did exactly what He saw the Father do. There was no difference of opinion whatsoever between the Father and Jesus.

There are three claims here.

Jesus was in perfect, unbroken communion with God.

Jesus was of the very same nature and person as God.

Jesus acted as the Son of God because He is God the Son: He did exactly what the father did. He did "the very same things" (tauta) in "the very same manner" (homoios). He acted and behaved exactly as the Father acted and behaved.

Jesus Christ was perfectly obedient; He acted exactly in the nature of God. (What a lesson on obedience! A challenge for obedience!)

 

      1. The second proof that Jesus was equal with God was His greater works.

20 For the Father loveth the Son, and sheweth him all things that himself doeth: and he will shew him greater works than these, that ye may marvel.

        1. The Father loves the Son.
          1. The Father continues to love and never stops loving the Son.
          2. There is never a moment when the love diminishes.
          3. It is a perfect love that never ceases to give.
        2. The Father showed the Son all things which He did.
          1. All things which Jesus did were the very things which the Father did.
          2. Jesus said that the Father was going to show Him greater things, greater things than the healing of the impotent man.
            1. Jesus would be controlling the forces of nature (storms on the Sea of Galilee).
            2. Feeding multitudes by multiplying food.
            3. raising the dead / healing multitudes of people.
            4. Establishing Grace and Truth
            5. Making new Creatures
