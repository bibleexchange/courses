  • Become a student of your marriage.

1Peter 3:7—Likewise, ye husbands, dwell with them according to knowledge, giving honour unto the wife, as unto the weaker vessel, and as being heirs together of the grace of life; that your prayers be not hindered.

1 Peter 3:7 calls them the weaker vessel, and tells us to honor them as such.

"Weaker" does not mean inferior, but "weaker" as in the way a tooth pick compares to a crowbar.

True, a crowbar will not break as easily as a tooth pick, but there are a lot of things a tooth pick can do that a crowbar can't do without hurting somebody.

So learn what those differences are, and honor your wife.

Women are very, very different from men in a lot of ways.

Recognize that your wife, by design, reveals a different dimension of the image of God than you.  

Learn everything you can about your marriage. Read and study your Bible always pray about this relationship; show your personal happiness, not look to find happiness but look to bring your happiness into the marriage no matter what.

  • Become a student of your wife. observe her, study her, and learn what she loves and what she hates and what hurts her and what thrills her.

Be patient. She is different than you. As she needs to be changed, you will do that not by being harsh with her or by criticizing her, prying and picking away. You will change her by your unconditional love and adoration. Think about it, that's how Jesus changes us. He accepts and loves us into His image as we learn to fully obey Him. Paul tells us to love our wives like that.

Eph 5:22—Wives, submit yourselves unto your own husbands, as unto the Lord.  

  • Your primary role is to love her not control her.

Titus 2:5—To be discreet, chaste, keepers at home, good, obedient to their own husbands, that the word of God be not blasphemed.

Praise her, extol her virtues. Notice the strengths and assume the weaknesses and not visa versa. Over time, the love you show her and the model you are to her will produce more change in her than one of your brilliantly crafted lectures.

  • God's purpose for you in your marriage is to teach you to love like He does.

Ephesians 5:25—Husbands, love your wives, even as Christ also loved the church, and gave himself for it;

  • Meditate on the Gospel. As Paul shows us in Ephesians 5:22-31, it is only by knowing Christ's love for you that you can love her properly. Think on it often, and love your wife as you have been loved.
