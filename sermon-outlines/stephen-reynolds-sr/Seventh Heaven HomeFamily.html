Dad’s Story

By Phil Berchtold

March 22, 2010                                   

   I was born in Boston in 1950 into a dysfunctional family and moved to Sharon, Massachusetts in 1954.  The stability and affluence of this quaint town inspired me to a better life.                                   

            Outside my family I found peace and joy in Sharon. The clean lake, the woods behind my house,

the nature trails at Moose Hill, all provided a fun and safe environment. I remember when I was five,

my mother sent me to Sharon Heights Shopping Center for baby bottles. I got lost, spent the money

on candy and stayed in front of Morse’s Food Mart all afternoon. It was a different day in 1955.

 

Being the poor kid on the block, I always felt on the outside looking in. My peers had nice shoes where the soles of my shoes flapped as I walked. I was embarrassed by my family, my status, my life, and I determined to change it all. I managed to be the first of my seven siblings to attend college and I was excited that I was escaping the chains of what appeared to be my destiny of defeat.

 

I wanted a chance at the Brass Ring.

 

It didn’t take long for me to succumb to the college party scene. The desire for some joy in my life overrode my better judgment and I dropped out of three colleges. My twenties became a blur of parties and drinking to drown out my failures, during which I smashed up five cars. As I told the inmates at the James River Correctional Facility when my family sang for them recently, “I’d be right there with you if it weren’t for God’s mercy.”

 

A few months before my thirtieth birthday I got the job of my life and met Clareann.

Immediately I was drawn to her beauty and her God. She introduced me to Jesus Christ

in a way I had never known, and I said, “This is the God for me!” I repented of my sins,

asked Him into my life, and married Clareann on Valentine’s Day 1982.

 

They say love is blind, and that was me. The fact that Clareann had seven young daughters

was not an issue. Some thought I was deaf, dumb AND blind! But I always wanted marriage

and children, so I felt blessed to get the whole package at once. The girls were very loving

and well behaved and immediately accepted me.

 

In Clareann I had found my soul-mate, and leaving bachelorhood for toddlers and teens

didn’t faze me. Her husband had just left her, ad I was to be her knight in shining armor.

I had found the love of my life, and once again, hope had rekindled to reach for the Brass Ring.

 

Although I had become a “New creature in Christ” the habits of my unfortunate upbringing did not just disappear, and The Miracle Worker had no problem with that! In our Home Church we worship God, read the Bible, and “Confess our sins and pray for one another that we may be healed” (1 Peter 3), and I have been healed physically and emotionally in 28 years of our fellowship.

 

In 1989 the girls wanted a change so I left my job as Sales Manager at Wise Potato Chips and we all

moved to California. Six years later I adopted all seven sisters, five of whom remain in my home to this day.

 

My new job in the Foothills of the Sierra Nevada Mountains became our Home Church Ministry and what amounted to a girls boarding house. During this time we purchased a home recording studio. When I fell in love with Clareann, I also fell in love with her music. It was anointed of God, touching the soul, and her voice rivaled that of Linda Ronstadt, my favorite singer. She had taught the girls to sing and we recorded CDs of her original songs now numbering 1,500.

To keep us happy, the girls (who are all in the medical field) sent us out to explore exotic and

interesting places to vacation, a family favorite being Israel.   It’s been a joy to hear them break

out in song all over the world, especially in Romania where our youngest daughter has lived

for seven years. In visiting her (for the ninth time in May) we’ve been able to help Romania’s

poor and abandoned babies.

 

After 12 years in California, we moved to Pennsylvania, then to Virginia in 2006.

We feel very blessed to live here in a 7 bedroom home where the world can now

hear our music on our website at www.seventhheavenmusic.net.

 

Little Clare came into our lives in 2007 and no one is more excited to be a Papa

to this beautiful child than I am. Born premature (25 weeks) at 1.5 pounds, she was

neglected in the NICU for six months, had 17 medical diagnoses, and a feeding tube.

We praise God that she is now a healthy, happy 3 year old. Adopted July 24, 2008,

Clare is the joy of everyone she meets.

                                            

We we are all a work in progress, I can now say I not only got the Brass Ring,

but I got the Golden “Crown of  Life which the Lord has promised to

 those who  love Him” (James 1).

 

And I pray you will encounter the same “God of Love”  

Mom's Story

                   I began my walk of  faith at 5 years old when I saw things in my Boston back yard that horrified me.  I sold water to the Italians for 5 cents a bucket for their gravesites, then off to Jack’s Store for penny candy.  People were putting their loved ones in the dirt and weeping and wailing as they walked away.   

So at age 5, I made it my ambition to go to Heaven where my mother said there was perfect happiness and no one dies. 

                                        SALVATION

 

          At age 25 when important things changed in my Catholic Faith, I began to question my eternal destiny.  Billy Graham was preaching from the Bible, the Best Seller of All Time and he made Heaven and Hell even more real to me.  I decided since men like Abe Lincoln and George Washington loved the Book, that I’d go to a Bible Study.  

 

They told me  in order to go to Heaven and escape the fires of hell, I would need to ask Jesus into my heart, repent of my sins, and live by the Bible the best I could.  I was thrilled!  I was Born-again 39 years ago, and I have felt the Presence and Peace of Jesus every day since.                                         

                                              SEVENTH HEAVEN

 

    When I gave God my musical talents and taught my girls to sing, Seventh Heaven was born.  Since then I've written over 1,500 songs and bought a home recording studio.  We moved from MA, to CA, to PA, to VA, studio and all, and recorded several CDs of our music which is heard around the world.                     

                                            DIVORCE

 

    When my parents left me behind after moving to Cape Cod, I became pregnant at age 16.  By the time I was 30 with 7 girls, my husband said, “I’m just not cut out to be a father. ”  After 17 years of marriage, and many years of troubled behavior, he walked out.    

 The Bible says, "I hate divorce", and for good reason.  It is a  tragedy that deeply hurts everyone, especially children. Being raised Catholic I was against divorce, “What God has joined together, let no man put asunder.”  But the priest who married two teenagers knew nothing of us and was paid for his duty.  And I knew nothing of Jesus’ Sermon on the Mount when He made exception for divorce “for the cause of unchastity”. Matt. 5:32

                                           MERCY

 

      He also told the Multitudes on the Mountain that day, “Blessed are the merciful, for they shall obtain mercy.”  

God did not join 2 teens together; a man did.  And when a handsome bachelor asked for my hand 2 weeks after I was left alone with 7 children, my Savior showed me great mercy.  He saw my tears, revealed this exception, to divorce and set me free!  

                                RE-MARRIAGE/ADOPTION

      Phil, without prior marriage or children said on our first date, “I think I’m meant by God to pick up where one man left off.”   I had never  known love could be like this!  Phil and I were married on Valentine’s Day 30 years ago, and we know it is Jesus who has given us a love that grows with every year we serve Him.   In 1995 Phil adopted all 7 girls.

         Four years ago God blessed us with our 8th daughter, a Special Needs infant from Virginia Foster Care.  Born at 1 lb. 10 oz. God has done miracles to heal her terrible introduction into this world. The day Clare was born, Kim got a job as Case Manager at the same hospital. At 6 months old we took her from the NICU.
