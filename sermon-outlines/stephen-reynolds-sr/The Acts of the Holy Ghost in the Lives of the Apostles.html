The Book of Acts is documentation on the work of the Holy Ghost to gather together the Holy Ghost filled Christian church to spread the gospel of the Lord Jesus Christ.

  1. All of Acts is devoted to the Word and work of the Holy Ghost in the Apostles Life.

  1. Structure:
    1. The book is structured around 2 Key Apostles leading men with the Word of God into prayer for great outpourings of the Holy Ghost.
      1. The ministry of the apostle Peter. Acts 1 to Acts 12:24
      2. The ministry of the apostle Paul. Acts 12:25 to Acts 28:31

Many verses are given in the Book of Acts to proclaim the Word increased and the church grew.                                Acts 2:41,47; 4:4; 5:14; 6:7; 8:6,8,14,17; 9:31,35; 11:21,24; 12:24; 16:5; 19:20;                                                 The Word was preached even as prisoner Acts 28:31; (2Tim. 2:9) Word not bound.

The structure of the Book of Acts is ordered from the commission in Acts 1:8 and Mat.16:16-19; 28:18-20; Mark 16:15-20; Luke 24:45-49; John 20:21

Witnesses spread the gospel in Jerusalem, once the capital of the Southern kingdom, Acts 1:1-6:7
Witnesses spread the gospel in all Judea and Samaria, ex-capital of Northern kingdom, 6:8-12:24
Witnesses spread the gospel in all places, to Rome, the capital of the Gentiles, Acts 12:25-28-31

    1. The book is structured around six points of growth.     Acts 1:1-6:7
  1. The Gospel is spread throughout Jerusalem and all Judea.      Acts 1:1-6:7

 

Acts 1:1-11

 

Jesus commissions his apostles to be his witnesses. Jesus ascends into heaven.

 

Acts 1:12-26  

 

The disciples gather in Jerusalem to pray and Peter the leading spokesman of 120 disciples urges them to elect an apostle to succeed Judas.

 

Acts 2:1-42:

 

On Pentecost, in fulfillment of God’s promise his disciples received the power of the Holy Ghost poured out on them. They spoke of the wonderful works of God in other tongues.

Peter preaches powerfully to the multitude to repent and to be baptized in the name of Jesus.

What the Holy Ghost did on Pentecost is the opposite of what the Lord did at Babel (Genesis 11)

 At Babel the Lord confused the language of the people who were set on glorifying themselves, not God. On Pentecost the disciples spoke in unknown tongues as the Spirit gave the utterance and many heard them in their own language proclaiming the wonderful works of God. As a result the church grows with 3.000 souls added to the Church. Pentecost marks the birth of the New Testament Church.  (Acts 2:41)

Acts 2:43-4:4:

Peter and John preach the Word of the Lord in Jerusalem and are opposed by the ruling council of the Jews. But many more Jews who hear Peter’s preaching are added to the church and the church grows with 5,000 souls. (Acts 4:4)

Acts 4:5-5:16:

Peter and John are tried by the ruling council of the Jews and afterwards the apostles pray for power to preach the Word boldly. In answer to their prayer the Holy Ghost fills them and they speak the Word boldly. After the incident involving Ananias and his wife, the apostles perform miracles and teach in the temple. Multitudes of men and women are added to the church. (5:14)

Acts 5:17-6:7:

The apostles are arrested by the ruling council of the Jews but are released by an angel to speak in the temple.

They are again taken into custody for trial and are released after being flogged.

Each day afterwards they continue teaching in the temple and from house to house.

To assist the apostles in their ministry the church elects its first deacons to wait on  tables.

 

Acts 6:7 And the word of God increased; and the number of the disciples multiplied in Jerusalem greatly; and a great company of the priests were obedient to the faith.

  1. The gospel is spread throughout Judea and Samaria, Acts 6:8-12:24.
  1. This first part of this mission was worked by the deacons 6:8-9:31

 

Acts 6:8-7:60:

  1. The persecution, trial, and murder of Stephen set the stage for an all out persecution of the church in Jerusalem by the ruling council of the Jews.

Acts 8:1-4:

  1. The persecution scatters Jesus’ disciples and spreads the church into Judea and Samaria, where they preach the Word. The Lord uses the persecution for the good purpose of spreading his church and the preaching of his Word. The fact that the Jewish disciples go into Samaria and preach the Word to the Samaritans is a significant act. It shows the Jewish disciples have overcome their hatred of the Samaritans and that the Word will not be bound by such hatred and prejudice.

Acts 8:5-40:

  1. The ministry of Philip in Samaria, where the Samaritans rejoice in the gospel in their city (8:8). They believe and are baptized (8:12). The gospel is preached to many Samaritan villages.
  2. Philip shares the gospel with the Ethiopian eunuch, a Gentile proselyte to the Jewish faith, who is baptized.
  3. Philip takes the gospel as far as Caesarea in northern Samaria. Acts 9:1-31:
  4. The conversion of Saul (Paul) who preaches the word of Jesus to the Jews in Damascus, Syria.

 

Acts 9:31—Then had the churches rest throughout all Judaea and Galilee and Samaria, and were edified; and walking in the fear of the Lord, and in the comfort of the Holy Ghost, were multiplied.

 

 

  1. Peter spreads his gospel in Judea and Samaria, Acts 9:32-12:24

 

    1. Peter preached in Lydda , Sharon of Judea, Samaria and they believed and turned to the Lord.     (Acts 9:32-35)
    2. Peter preached in Joppa of Judea/Samaria where many believe in the Lord. Acts 10:1-48
    3. Peter preached to the Gentiles of Cornelius’ household in Caesarea of Samaria, who receive the baptism of the Spirit like the disciples did on Pentecost. The Spirit plainly shows that in the church of Jesus there is no difference between Jew and Gentile.
    4. Peter defends his ministering to the Gentiles, which he did by the Spirit’s direction. Acts 11:1-18
    5. The disciples who were persecuted in Jerusalem at the time Stephen was killed also scattered to Phoenicia, the island of Cyprus, and to Antioch in Syria. They bring the Word to the Jews in those places.     Acts 11:19
    6. Some disciples from Cyprus and Cyrene preach the gospel of Jesus to the Gentiles in Antioch, Syria. A large number there believe in Jesus. The church of Antioch is established of both Jews and Gentiles.           Acts 11:20-30
    7. Herod kills John’s brother James and persecutes Peter.     Acts 12:1-24

Acts 12:24—But the word of God grew and multiplied.

  1. The gospel is preached to the ends of the earth, to Rome, the capital of the Gentiles, Acts 12:25-28-31.
    1. Paul makes his first missionary journey to Cyprus, Pamphylia, and Galatia,                                                               Acts 12:25-16:5
    2. Paul and Barnabas preach at Antioch, Syria, by the Spirit to be missionaries to the Gentiles.            Acts 12:25-13:3
    3.  The church at Antioch, headquarters for foreign missions was not a huge congregation with unlimited resources, but was motivated by conviction and commitment to be missionary minded.
    4. Paul’s ministry on Cyprus starts at Salamis, where he brings the Word to the Jews, and ends in Paphos, where the Gentile proconsul Sergius Paulus hears the Word and believes.      Acts 13:4-12
    5. Paul’s ministry in Pamphylia      Acts 13:13
    6. Paul’s ministry at Pisidian Antioch in Galatia, where he brings the Word of the Lord to the Jews and then to the Gentiles. 13:48,49 Many Gentiles believe and the Word is spread throughout the whole region. Unbelieving Jews instigate persecution and drive out Paul and Barnabas.     Acts 13:14-50
    7. Paul preaches to Jews and Gentiles in Iconium, Galatia, where a great multitude of Jews and Gentiles believe. But again unbelieving Jews arouse the Gentiles, inflame persecution, and drive out Paul and Barnabas.              Acts 13:51-14:6
    8. Paul preaches in Lystra and Derbe of Lycaonia and  Galatia. Jews from Pisidian Antioch and Iconium track Paul down in Lystra, stirred the crowds of Lystra, stoned him and left him for dead. Acts 14:6-19
    9. Paul goes back to Derbe, Lystra, Iconium, Pisidian Antioch, strengthening the converts and having elders and pastors elected in the local churches. Then through Pamphylia and returns to Antioch, Syria, where he begun his first missionary journey.                    Acts 14:20-28
    10. The First Council of the Church is held at Jerusalem
      1. to settle the Judaistic Controversy.
      2. Jewish converts called Judaizers contend that the Law of Moses, especially circumcision, must be followed by Gentile believers for them to be saved.
      3. The council agrees that all are saved by the grace of Jesus alone without observing the law of Moses.
      4. Jewish disciples extend a greeting and welcome to the Gentile disciples by means of a letter, which brings joy and unity in the gospel to the church.
      5. The freedom of the gospel that we are saved by grace through faith  in Jesus Christ as Lord is proclaimed.

Acts 16:5 And so were the churches established in the faith, and increased in number daily.

  1.  Paul makes his second missionary journey to Macedonia and Achaia in Greece,      Acts 15:35-18:22

Paul and Barnabas preach and teach in Antioch, Syria, the mother church, until they part company to strengthen the churches. Barnabas takes John Mark with him to Cyprus.

Paul takes Silas to the churches in Syria, Cilicia, and then to Derbe and Lystra in Galatia.   

          Acts 15:35-16:5

The Spirit prevents Paul preaching in Asia and Bithynia, but leads him to Troas where the Spirit directs Paul by  a vision of a man in Macedonia, the northern province of Greece.    Acts 16:6-10

    1. Paul’s ministry in Philippi of Macedonia in northern Greece, where he with Silas brought the Word to the Jewish women, including Lydia’s household, and to the Gentile jailer’s household. There the church of Philippi is established and the Word of the Lord takes a foothold on the continent of Europe.      Acts 16:11-40
    2. Paul’s ministry in Thessalonica of Macedonia, where he first brings the Word to the Jews. Some Jews believe and Greeks convert to the Jewish faith.   The unbelieving Jews form a mob and persecute the newly formed congregation of disciples who met at Jason’s house.      Acts 17:1-9
    3. Paul’s ministry in Berea of Macedonia, where the Jews believe the Word as well as a number of leading Greek citizens, both men and women. The unbelieving Jews from Thessalonica come to Berea and stir up a persecution of Paul and the church in Berea.     Acts 17:10-13
    4. Paul’s ministry in Athens of Achaia, the southern province of Greece, where he preaches to the leading citizens and philosophers of the Areopagus, among whom some believe.      Acts 17:14-34
    5. Paul’s ministry in Corinth of Achaia, where he works with Aquila and Priscilla, a husband and wife, as a tentmaker and teaches the Word on the Sabbath to the Jews and Greek proselytes. Then he begins a full time ministry of preaching to the Jews. When the unbelieving Jews resist the Word, Paul starts a church in the house of Titius Justus, which is next door to the Jewish synagogue. Many Jews and Gentile Corinthians believe. Paul teaches there for more than 1 and 1/2 years.

Acts 18:1-17

    1. Paul leaves Corinth, travels by sea to Ephesus in Asia where he makes a brief visit and speaks the Word to the Jews. He promises to return, the Lord willing. He then returns to Antioch, Syria, which completes his second missionary journey.       Acts 18:18-22    
  • Paul makes his third missionary journey, Acts 18:23-21:14
      1. Paul revisits the churches in Galatia while Apollos is preaching in Ephesus.               Acts 18:23-28
      2. Paul’s ministry in Ephesus. He instructs about 12 disciples of John the Baptist, whom he baptizes and on whom the Holy Spirit comes mightily. Paul stays in Ephesus teaching the Word, first to the Jews until some harden their hearts, and then to the Gentiles. Many believe.                       Acts 19:1-19

    Acts 19:20—So mightily grew the word of God and prevailed.

      2. Paul spends 3 months revisiting the churches in Macedonia, Greece, and then sails to Troas in Asia, where he spends the day teaching the Word.    Acts 20:1-6
      3. Paul traveled from Jerusalem to Miletus in Asia, where he speaks to the elders and pastors from Ephesus and bids them farewell.         

    Acts 20:7-38

      1. Paul sails from Miletus, past Cyprus, to Tyre in Syria, and arrives in Caesarea of Samaria. Paul is warned not to set foot in Jerusalem.
