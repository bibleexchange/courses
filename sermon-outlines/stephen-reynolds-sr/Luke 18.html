Luke gives a disclaimer at the beginning of this parable for a reason:

 

  1. Speaking to those of the certain that trust in themselves and despised others…
  • “Certain”
    • Not necessarily just Jewish Pharisees
  • Applying to more than Jewish Pharisees

 

  1. Luke learned some things of how to decipher parables to the fullest meaning
    1. A direct addressing…
    2. A clear and precise assessment of their condition
    3. An exhortation to direct them out of their mindsets

 

Two things especially make our prayers void and of no effect:

  1. Confidence of our own righteousness.
  2. Contempt of others

A humble heart is contrary to self-confidence and contempt of others

The Pharisee did not recognize himself as a sinner, the publican did.

The point here is not whether Godly men sin or not or how much they sin but why and how men call upon God in honesty and humility.

The principle of humility was shown by the publican praying in humility and honesty.

    • He feared God

    • He received from the Lord
    • The publican came as a sinner and identified himself that way, confessed his own sin, received mercy and left a righteous saint, went down to his house justified
