The Love and Wrath of God

John 3:36—He that believeth on the Son hath everlasting life: and he that believeth not the Son shall not see life; but the wrath of God abideth on him.                                                                                                                  3:3—Jesus answered and said unto him, Verily, verily, I say unto thee Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God.                                                          3:5—Jesus answered, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God.

  • There is a life giving power for the believer, (obedient).
  • There is a blinding power in disobedience, (unbelief blinds).
  • The wrath of God abides on the unbeliever , (disobedient).

Romans 11:33—O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past finding out!                                                     Psalms 36:6—Thy righteousness is like the great mountains; thy judgments are a great deep: O LORD, thou preservest man and beast.

 (blind astray lost) are words to describe the sinner’s  condition but they only scratch the surface.

Without salvation it is death, hopeless and Wrath.

With Salvation is everlasting life and abundant life.

Who is God and how does He think?                                                                                                                                                                           

  1. God is Wrath as much as God is Love.
    1. Wrath is one of God’s attributes.
      1. Most will come to know the Wrath of God because of sin, without ever receiving the Love of God that He demonstrated on the cross to free man from all sin.

Romans 5:8—But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, while we were yet sinners, Christ died for us.                                                                            11:30—For as ye in times past have not believed God, yet have now obtained mercy through their unbelief:

 {believed: or, obeyed – “not believed” or disobeyed}

31—Even so have these also now not believed, that through your mercy they also may obtain mercy.                             32—For God hath concluded them all in unbelief, that he might have mercy upon all.

        1. The Wrath of God gives the highest penalty against sin.

        1. The Wrath of God came upon His own Son on the cross to free man from the penalty of sin and the power of sin.

      1. To receive the fullness of God’s Love we must fully obey His Word.

God’s Love is manifested in the life of those that live according to all the counsel of God and the beauty of His Holiness.

Romans 5:5 And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

      1. God’s Wrath is against all disobedience, every spot and any blemish in your life that you don’t turn away from.
  • Wrath is God’s judgment against sin.
        1. Sin is every transgression against His law.
        2. God’s Wrath is always against sin and never will not be against it and will only forgive the sinner not sin; God will never change His opinion against sin.
  • The Wrath of God is an eternal detesting of sin.
        1. Men of any sin will be brought to hell and in the Day of Judgment will be cast into the Lake of fire which is the second death which is eternal separation from God.
        2. That’s why men that are cast into the lake of fire will never come out because God will never stop detesting sin; He hates iniquity, He abhors every transgression, His fury is forever against every single sin.
        3. God does not test for sin in man but He loves and tests man for repentance.  He detests sin, while He tests man.

    Salvation from sin has been provided.

    An Escape from the Judgment of sin is only through the salvation God has provided.

    Heb. 2:3—How shall we escape, if we neglect so great salvation; which at the first began to be spoken by the Lord, and was confirmed unto us by them that heard him;

    1Thess. 1:10—And to wait for his Son from heaven, whom he raised from the dead, even Jesus, which delivered us from the wrath to come.

    5:9—For God hath not appointed us to wrath, but to obtain salvation by our Lord Jesus Christ,

    Psalms 39:12—Hear my prayer, O LORD, and give ear unto my cry; hold not thy peace at my tears: for I am a stranger with thee, and a sojourner, as all my fathers were.

    Tears, weeping, wailing, crying and praying is needed to get into a place where the Word of the Lord is completely in control of our hearts but faith is the Key and the love of God makes it all work.

    Gal. 5:6—For in Jesus Christ neither circumcision availeth any thing, nor uncircumcision; but faith which worketh by love.
