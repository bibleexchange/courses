Abraham Was Visited By God Himself

  1. The Godly have A Growing Faith—
    1. How to Be a Friend of God, 18:1-15

Introduction:

    The greatest problem man faces today is how to be a friend of God.

A friend is a person who holds affection and respect for another person.

The affection and respect attaches them together.

If they have feelings and respect for one another, they become attached; they become friends.

If they do not have affection and respect for one another, they are not friends; they are enemies.

This is the problem man has with God. Man does not hold affection and respect for God, not enough to become attached to God, not enough to become a close, intimate friend with God.

 Man just does not care and respect God enough to subject himself to God, to elevate God to the position He rightly holds: the Creator and Sustainer of life, the Sovereign Lord and Master of the universe.

As Christ said: "Ye are my friends, if ye do whatsoever I command you" (John 15:14).

 

But man is not willing to do what Christ commanded, not willing to love and serve God nor to keep His commandments.

Man breaks God's commandments and rebels against God.

Man stands up against God and refuses to obey Him.

Thus man stands as an opponent of God, as an enemy of God.

However, this is not true of every single person upon earth.

 

Some persons want to be friends with God.

 

 They have the deepest affection and respect for God, and they are forever seeking a deeper friendship.

 

In fact, some are seeking the deepest and most intimate friendship possible with God. Such a passion should burn within the heart of every person. It did within Abraham.

18:11-15) Unbelief— Lying— God, Power; Knowledge of: How can we become a friend of God? Conquer unbelief. Note three significant things.

1. The causes of unbelief are clearly seen in this experience of Sarah.

a. Sarah and Abraham were looking at an impossible situation, humanly speaking. They were both beyond childbearing

 Abraham was one hundred years old and Sarah was ninety years old.

b. Sarah allowed negative thoughts to enter her mind.

 she had heard the Lord's promise to Abraham, but she knew the impossible circumstances. The whole situation just seemed laughable to Sarah, and she actually laughed to herself, somewhat mockingly and skeptically. She murmured silently to herself, "How can I who am so old bear a child, and Abraham also being so old—impossible! Sarah was responding to God's promise in unbelief: she just did not believe God's promise, did not believe it was possible. It was physically impossible to her.

she was thinking of the circumstances, not thinking of God and His promise and power.

2. The answer to unbelief is twofold (Genesis 18:13-14).

a. God's omniscience, His knowing all things. God asked Abraham why Sarah had laughed, revealing that He knew all about Sarah's unbelief.

b. God's omnipotence, His power to do all things (Genesis 18:14). "Is anything too hard for the Lord" (Genesis 18:14). God can do anything, no matter how impossible it seems. He clearly said that He would give Sarah a child despite the deadness of her womb Ro 4:19

I know that thou canst do every thing, and that no thought can be withholden from thee (Job 42:2).

But Jesus beheld [them], and said unto them, With men this is impossible; but with God all things are possible (Matthew 19:26).

For with God nothing shall be impossible" (Luke 1:37).

The conquest of unbelief (Genesis 18:15). Sarah lied: she denied that she had laughed, for she feared a severe rebuke from the Lord and perhaps from Abraham. But the Lord made it clear: she did laugh—laughed in unbelief—and He knew all about her failure to believe the promise of the promised seed. Moreover, she was now lying about the matter.

What the Lord was doing was telling Sarah that she needed to confess, repent, stop the deceit and lying, and believe the promise. God could perform the miracle, overcome the circumstances of life, no matter how impossible they might appear. God could fulfill His promise. Sarah could conquer unbelief only if she confessed, repented, and believed the promise of the promised seed.

Unbelief will always keep us from being a friend of God. The friend of God believes God; he never disbelieves. We must, therefore, conquer unbelief:

⇒ We must not look at circumstances as though they are impossible for God.

⇒ We must not allow negative thoughts to surround the promises of God, questioning His promises.

⇒ We must believe that God knows all things and can do all things

⇒ We must confess and repent of our sin of unbelief, of distrusting the promises of God.

This is the only way to conquer unbelief. This is the only way to become a friend—a true friend—of God.

And what is poverty of spirit?

Poverty of spirit is a consciousness of my emptiness and my nothingness. This only results from the Spirit’s work within, but He never leaves us empty but He Fills us with His virtue, knowledge, temperance, patience, godliness, brothertly kindness, Love.
