2. John's Great Witness

 

Acts 13:25 And as John fulfilled his course, he said, Whom think ye that I am? I am not he. But, behold, there cometh one after me, whose shoes of his feet I am not worthy to loose.

 

Having told them that Jesus was the promised Seed of David, Paul gave John’s testimony for a witness for Christ. John the Baptist had been popularly acknowledged by the common people as a prophet. He had announced unmistakably and emphatically that he was the herald of the coming King, that his ministry was to announce and introduce the Messiah.

Israel's holy mission was to prepare the world for the coming of Christ.

The wide dispersion of the Jews among the Gentiles had helped do that, for despite their unbelief, and cold traditionalism, both Hebrews and greeks clung to their messianic hope, a hope that had been fanned to flame by the ministry of John.

(b) Israel's Hideous Mistake (13:26-37)

i. The Challenge to His Hearers (13:26)

Men and brethren, children of the stock of Abraham, and whosoever among you feareth God, to you is the word of this salvation sent.

It was a crisis now for Israel. It was now or never for their souls. God's salvation had come in the person of the Messiah, Jesus the Savior. It had come now to them, the Jews and God-fearers in Pisidian Antioch, news from a far country, news from home, news brought by the visitors to the synagogue that Sabbath morn.

Paul was now about to plunge into the sad story of Israel's murder of the Messiah. First, however, he wanted his listeners to know that salvation was available for all through the very Savior heralded by John.

ii. The Charge Against the Hebrews (13:27-37)

a. The Tragedy of Judaism: The Rejection of Christ (13:27-29)

1. Their Woeful Ignorance

 

Ac 13:27 For they that dwell at Jerusalem, and their rulers, because they knew him not, nor yet the voices of the prophets which are read every sabbath day, they have fulfilled them in condemning him.

 

(a) Of the Person of Christ

For they that dwell at Jerusalem, and their rulers because they knew him not.

What an amazing statement. They knew Him not. For three-and-a-half years He had journeyed the length and breadth of the land, had been to Jerusalem again and again, had performed miracle after miracle—so many that the world itself could not contain the books that could have been written (John 21:25). Yet they knew Him not! He had revealed Himself to them. He had discoursed with them, taught with authority and not as the scribes, had declared Himself to be the Son of God as positively as He had demonstrated Himself to be the Son of God. John had proclamed Him the Messiah; He had proved Himself the Messiah. Yet the common people and their rulers alike "knew Him not." What more proof did they want? What greater credentials could He have shown? His character, His conduct, and His conversation all proclaimed Him to be the Son of God. Here was guilty, criminal ignorance. Here was blindness of the most persistent kind.

(b) Of the Prophecies of Christ

... they knew him not, nor yet the voices of the prophets which are read every sabbath day, they have fulfilled them in condemning him.

There was no excuse. The many Old Testament prophecies that spoke of Christ were read systematically in the synagogues week after week. They knew what the Scriptures said about His ancestry; they had only to check the Temple records to see that He was indeed the Son of David. They knew what the prophet said about His birth at Bethlehem, what Malachi had said about a forerunner, that the Messiah would minister in Galilee, that the Spirit would be given without measure to Him, and that He would ride in triumph into Jerusalem.

  • They knew what Daniel had said about His being "cut off.

Da 9:26 And after threescore and two weeks shall Messiah be cut off, but not for himself: and the people of the prince that shall come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary; and the end thereof shall be with a flood, and unto the end of the war desolations are determined.

 

They could work out the very date. They knew Isaiah 53; Psalm 22; 69; Zechariah 12:10; 13:6-7.

 

They knew He was to be sold for thirty pieces of silver, that the blood money would be used to buy a potter's field.

 

They knew He would be led as a sheep to the slaughter; that His hands and feet would be pierced; that He would be mocked and given vinegar to drink.

 

They had ignorantly fulfilled the prophecies they knew by heart.

 

Truly their ignorance was willful ignorance.
