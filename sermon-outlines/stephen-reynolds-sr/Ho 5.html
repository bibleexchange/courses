    As an eagle against the house of the Lord, because they have transgressed my covenant, and trespassed against My law.—

    The lukewarm Church

    These words are singularly abrupt, and indicate the suddenness of the threatened invader.

    By "the house of the Lord"

    We are to understand Israel as a section of the professed people of God.

    And we are of the professed people of God today.

    1. We are endangered. How comes the eagle?
      1. Suddenly, swiftly, and powerfully.
      2. A lukewarm Church is in greater danger than any secular community, because—
        1.  Its guilt is greater.
        2.  Its influence is more deceptive.

    Whose influence on society is the most destructive—                                                     The man who denies God?          The man who ignores Him?                                                                                         Or The man who misrepresents Him?

    The lukewarm Church gives society a misrepresentation of God and true religion.

    1. As warned. Blow a blast that shall thrill every heart in the congregation. Why sound the warning?

    1. Because the danger is tremendous.

    2. Because the danger is at hand.

    3. Because the danger may be avoided.

    What is wanted now is a humble honest crying out to God from each individual. Preparing themselves for Him.

    Re 19:7 Let us be glad and rejoice, and give honour to him: for the marriage of the Lamb is come, and his wife hath made herself ready.                                                 8 And to her was granted that she should be arrayed in fine linen, clean and white: for the fine linen is the righteousness of saints. {white: or, bright}

    Ministry of warning the lukewarm Churches is available to a sent evangelist.

    1. Be truly repentant not religous.
      1. "Israel shall cry unto Me, My God, we know Thee."
      2. Oh hasten the day when all Churches shall be brought to a deep and experimental knowledge of God and His Son!
    2. When this transpires the dense cloud that has concealed the sun of Christianity shall be swept away, and the quickening beam shall fall on every heart.

    God coming in judgment

    The eagle, the great principle remains from age to age that God comes in judgment in various forms, always definitely, always, plainly, not only inflicting vengeance as a Sovereign whose covenants have been broken, but condescending to explain the reasons upon which His most destructive judgments are based.

    "Because they have transgressed My covenant, and trespassed against My law": the covenant had been broken by idolatry, and the law had been violated by social sins.

    It is needful to mark distinction with great clarity, because it shows the breadth of the Divine commandment. God is not speaking about a mere ceremonial law,—or a law which can only be interpreted by the greatest minds, and put into operation on certain occasions of life;

    He is speaking about a law which has its religious aspects, but also its social, practical, tender phases, in whose preservation every man, woman, and child in the kingdom ought to be interested.

    Ho 8:12 I have written to him the great things of my law, but they were counted as a strange thing.

    God has made it clear that sin is always a crime.                                                                           Whoever sins against God sins against his own soul.

    Once God's laws are violated, the man not only suffers spiritually but practically in quantity and quality, he actually suffers in body and land.

    Ps 119:18 Open thou mine eyes, that I may behold wondrous things out of thy law.

     

    The Holy Scriptures are plain enough; but the heart of man is darkened by sin.

    The problem is with our vision, heart, soul, spirit.

     

    When his commandments are "counted as a strange thing," he will account our worship as sin, and put to shame all our earthly confidences.
