  1. And thou shalt—
    1. In Deut 6:5 We have the language of law, One word is given to express the Divine Law. And what a word!

Had the essence of the divine law consisted in deeds, it could not possibly have been expressed in a single word; for no one deed is comprehensive of all others embraced in the law.

It consists in an affection of the heart, soul and mind; one word expresses it--but only one fully, LOVE.

Fear, due to God and commanded, is limited in its sphere and distant in character. Trust, hope, and faith, though essential features of a right state of heart towards God, are called into action with personal necessity, they can have reservations for self, (respect to our own well-being).

LOVE is an all-inclusive affection, embracing every other affection, as love spontaneously seeks to please God, it is the native well spring of a voluntary obedience. It is the most personal of all affections. One may fear an event, one may hope for an event, one may rejoice in an event; but one can love only a Person. It’s tender, most unselfish,  divine of all affections.

 

    Thou shalt love—We must have this glorious affection.

 

    1. The Object of that demanded affection.
      1. Thou shalt love the Lord, thy God--that is,
        1. Jehovah, the Self-Existent One, who has revealed Himself as the "I AM," and there is none else;
        2. by His name JEHOVAH is unapproachable but through His Son, and bears to thee a real and definite relationship, out of which arises His claim and your duty--of LOVE.
      2. With what are we to love Him?
        1. Four things are here specified.
          1. "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God"  with thy heart
            1. An undivided Heart
              1. The center of thoughts and feelings
          2. "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God" with thy soul.
            1. This is designed to command our emotional nature:
              1. Thou shalt have feeling and warmth in your affection.
          3. "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God" with thy mind
            1. This commands our intellectual nature:
              1. Thou shalt put intelligence into thine affection
              2. in opposition to a blind devotion, or mere machine.
          4.  "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God"  with thy strength
            1. This commands our energies: Thou shalt put intensity into thine affection

Taking these four things together, the command of the Law is, "Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy powers--with a truthful, fervent, intelligent, an energetic love."

 

But this is not all that the Law demands.

God will have all these qualities in their most perfect exercise.

 

"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God," says the Law, "with all thy heart,"

A perfect trueness    True Holiness is when your whole heart is involved

 

"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy soul,"

with utmost fervor

 

"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy mind,"

in the fullest exercise of an enlightened reason

 

"Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with all thy strength,"

with the whole energy of our being
