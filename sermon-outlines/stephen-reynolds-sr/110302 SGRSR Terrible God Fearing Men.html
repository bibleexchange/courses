INTRODUCTION: Psalm 65—A Hymn of Harvest, A Psalm of Thanksgiving

 

Hymn of Thanksgiving---A Thanksgiving Psalm   

Inspired for the Ocassion for Prayer, Commitment to vows, thanksgiving and rejoicing.

Prayer for God to break the boundries of the Gentiles to be called and chosen to the Gospel and the benefits which flow from it. The Psalmist prays particularly that God would continue his former kindness to the Jewish people

 

  1. 1 Praise waiteth for thee, O God, in Sion: and unto thee shall the vow be performed.  

 

We do not just make noise, speak words, vain repetition, but wait for inspiration from God!

 

  1. 2 O thou that hearest prayer, unto thee shall all flesh come.

 

God inspires prayer. The fact that he HEARS us, encourages our prayer!

This is an awe-inspiring psalm. God is an awe-inspiring God! God’s Word inspires our heart, to teach us to pray. We don’t even know how to pray as we ought. (Rom. 8:26)

If in our life we win ONE soul, we have won more value than someone who has ‘won’ all the riches of the world. This psalm is historical and prophetic!

Psalm 65:1—Praise waiteth for thee, O God, in Sion: and unto thee shall the vow be performed.

A vow kept is like a shining sharp sword sheathed

A vow kept in obedience to the Will of God will clear the conscience

A vow unkept will burn the conscience like a hot iron.  

Making a vow is as simple as raising a sword.

Keeping a vow will be a hard fought battle

Vows of service, of sacrifice, of donation, of praise, or whatever they may be, are no trifles; and in the day of grateful praise they should, without fail, be fulfilled to the utmost of faith with power.
