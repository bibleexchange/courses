Rev. Stephen G. Reynolds, Sr.

January 8, 2005

Sunday Evening

Title:  "The Lord is Our Redeemer"

Text: Romans 3:24,25

 

Christ was chosen by the Father to be Redeemer and He submitted to the Father.

There is no reason to seek any other help.

 

There are many changes in Portland with a lot of problems and confusion. If you hold on to God in the places of famine and hardship having been prepared for it you can overcome. This is a time to fear God. The fear of God will bring us to the place where the love of God will cast out all fear so that we will not be given to anger, rage, and all sorts of problems. We will find the joy of the Lord to be our strength and that He is everything that we need.

 

Judgment is upon us. This message is to help us understand where most people are at.

 

Hosea 7:13 “Woe unto them! The fled from ME!” (They have forsaken me. Jer. 5:7) The Word ME in these verses refer to the Lord and are EMPHATIC.

 

In the midst of Israel’s fleeing from God, God sends them the prophet Hosea to sound his voice as a trumpet that if they would continue in their sin, then they would be judged severely. God wants you for Himself, and for you trust Him. The Lord comes by to awaken and arouse. While people have forsaken Him you can remember what He has done. Psalm 143:9

 

Hosea 7:8-13 Ephraim IT IS NOT THERE. IT HAS BEEN TAKEN. Running from the very One and only One Who could help them.

 

Hosea 7:13 “I have redeemed them.” Micah 6:4
