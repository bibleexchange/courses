Ga 3:13 Christ hath redeemed us from the curse of the law, being made a curse for us: for it is written, Cursed is every one that hangeth on a tree:

14 That the blessing of Abraham might come on the Gentiles through Jesus Christ; that we might receive the promise of the Spirit through faith.

  1. Redeemed
    1. "Christ has redeemed us from the curse of the law."
      1. The word "redeemed" means to buy back or to buy from or to ransom.
        1. Christ has bought man back
        2. Christ ransomed him from the curse of the law.
          1. Man has broken the law of God is unquestionable;
            1. Every honest man knows this.
            2. Every man stands guilty before God
            3. Every man must be judged and condemned to bear the curse and punishment of the law.
        3. The glorious news is Jesus Christ has redeemed us
        4. Jesus Christ redeemed us from the curse of the law.
          1. How?
            1. Jesus Christ was made a curse for us.
            2. Jesus Christ took our condemnation, doom, death, and punishment upon Himself and bore them all for us.
            3. Jesus Christ took our place as the lawbreaker and guilty party before God, and He bore the punishment of the lawbreaker for us.
          2. How was this possible?
            1. Jesus Christ obeyed and kept the law of God perfectly.
              1. He had never broken the law, not once.
              2. He was sinless and perfect.
              3. He had secured the Ideal Righteousness/Perfection before God.
              4. He bore no guilt, penalty, mark of death.
              5. He had the right to stand before God claim eternal life, right to be accepted by God and never again experience death
              6. He was perfect;
              7. He secured incorruptible righteousness.
  2. We are Redeemed because God’s love is in us.
    1. The glorious gospel is that instead of claiming the right to live in God's presence, Christ determined to share His perfection and righteousness to the diligent believer, and take their sin upon Himself. He was determined to swap His ideal righteousness for man's unrighteousness, to swap His ideal life for man's sinful life.
    2. Christ substituted Himself, His perfect life, for man's sinful life.
      1. He substituted His obedience to God for man's disobedience
      2. He bore man's sin and punishment so that man might stand righteous and perfect before God.
      3. Jesus Christ bore the curse of the law for us.

 

De 21:23 His body shall not remain all night upon the tree, but thou shalt in any wise bury him that day; (for he that is hanged is accursed of God;) that thy land be not defiled, which the LORD thy God giveth thee for an inheritance.

This does not mean that a man is cursed because he is executed upon a tree, but rather that a man who is executed upon a tree is there because he is cursed, having been judged as a lawbreaker.

Jesus Christ was the lawbreaker, taking the place of the transgressor; therefore, He was cursed (condemned) to die as the unlawful and disobedient sinner.
