Launching Effective warfare against sin

Pentecost shows the effective campaign against sin for the Christian

Before Pentecost, men and women simply did not have the desire or power to mount an effective campaign against sin. Not even God's law given on tablets of stone during that ancient Pentecost at Mount Sinai could help in the struggle. But all that changed when the Holy Ghost came on Pentecost

Before the day of Pentecost, it was not possible to understand that character grows out of the spiritual war taking place in the converted mind. People simply did what came "naturally."

    THE FEAST OF PENTEC0ST                                                                                                     Lev. 23:15-22

    Fifty days after the feast of the First Fruits the Feast of Pentecost was observed. This is the exact period between the resurrection of Christ and the formation of the Church on the day of Pentecost.

    It was not until the day of Pentecost had fully come that we have the fulfillment of the type.                                                                                                                                                        

    Acts 2:1—And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place.

     2—And suddenly there came a sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind, and it filled all the house where they were sitting.

     3—And there appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of fire, and it sat upon each of them.

     4—And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance.

    At the feast of Pentecost a new Meat offering was to be offered. It was made from the New Harvest and speaks of the New born Church, fresh from the hand of God. The grain was prepared and ground  into fine flour for two wave loaves (representing the Jew and Gentile) that were baked with leaven.

    Lev. 23:17—Ye shall bring out of your habitations two wave loaves of two tenth deals: they shall be of fine flour; they shall be baken with leaven; they are the firstfruits unto the LORD.

    Christ is typified by the separate sheaf, but the Church is set forth by many grains ground and blended together as one.

    The Holy Spirit at Pentecost united the disciples into one body.                                    

    1Cor. 10:16—The cup of blessing which we bless, is it not the communion of the blood of Christ? The bread which we break, is it not the communion of the body of Christ?                                                                                                       17—For we being many are one bread, and one body: for we are all partakers of that one bread.                                                                                                                                       

    12:12 For as the body is one, and hath many members, and all the members of that one body, being many, are one body: so also is Christ.                                                                  13— For by one Spirit are we all baptized into one body, whether we be Jews or Gentiles, whether we be bond or free; and have been all made to drink into one Spirit.                                                                                                                         20—But now are they many members, yet but one body.

    Notice the loaves as well as the sheaf are called “first fruits” (Lev. 23:17)

    This is setting forth the Church’s identification with Christ. The Harvest was marked with the same characteristics that were in the sheaf. The true Church not only has the nature of Christ but was brought into being through His death and resurrection and the first fruits company is to be fully revealed at Christ’s coming.

    1Cor. 15:23—But every man in his own order: Christ the firstfruits; afterward they that are Christ's at his coming.

    Divine Provision has been made for the Church.

    The loaves were baked with leaven but with the wave-sheaf no leaven was offered for there was no evil in Christ.  “sin shall not have dominion over us” but in the wave-loaves, typifying the church, there is evil

    Evil was divinely recognized and divinely met. The Sin Offering with the bread, the Burnt, Meal, Peace and the Drink offering were all offered.

    Christ our Sin Offering in His perfections are provided also for the church that through them, God might see us “accepted in the Beloved” and perfected in Him.

    The Drink Offering first mentioned in connection with the Sheaf (Christ)                 Lev. 23:11-13 is also in connection with the Bread (The Church) Lev. 23:13.

    How fitting that the Drink-Offering typical of the joy of the spirit should be associated with Christ in resurrection.                                                                                                 What joys pulsated through the being of our Mighty Conqueror.

    What complete provision, that the Church might be presented “perfect and entire wanting nothing”. All was freely offered on the Day of Pentecost.

    We find an unexplainable lukewarmness in people to enter into the inheritance which God has promised and provided. Miracle after miracle took place, with little or no exertion of their own left them ineffective. When all the people went to battle, they went with a measure of confidence, expecting miraculous help from God, and confiding in their numbers, but when each tribe found fought for itself, in order to its establishment and the extension of its borders, it was discouraged, and chose rather a life of inglorious ease than the possession of an inheritance which would cost it much labour to conquer.

    1. Joshua's rebuke came against the lukewarm, complacent and half-hearted commitment of the seven tribes in claiming their inheritance

     

    Jos 18:2 And there remained among the children of Israel seven tribes, which had not yet received their inheritance.

     3 And Joshua said unto the children of Israel, How long are ye slack to go to possess the land, which the LORD God of your fathers hath given you?

     

    1. They failed to come before Joshua and request their inheritance.

     

      1. Complacency and sluggishness set in.
      2. Apathy, lethargy, and indifference had gripped them.
      3. A spirit of inactivity and apathy had overtaken them.
      4. They were "at ease in Zion" and self-satisfied with what they had, at least for the moment.
      5. They lacked zeal and dedication in laying claim to God's wonderful gift, the inheritance of the promised land.
    1. Joshua’s rebuke was sharp .

     

      1. He called the leaders of the seven tribes before him,
        1. charged them with being slack and neglectful,
        2. with being complacent and half-hearted,
        3. lacking zeal and dedication.
        4. He asked them how long they would be sluggish.
        5. How much longer would they show half-hearted commitment in laying claim to the land that the Lord had given them?
