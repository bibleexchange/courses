TITLE: The Greatest Trust

TEXT: Luke 18:22

 

The greatest trust has to do with your faith working by love.

 

Everybody can receive from God.

Luke 18:18,22

 

And a certain ruler asked him, saying, Good Master, what shall I do to inherit eternal life?

 

Without eternal life it is all in vain. Great ministry, what is it worth if in the end the minister does not have eternal life? ALL MUST HAVE ETERNAL LIFE.

 

Now when Jesus heard these things, he said unto him, Yet lackest thou one thing: sell all that thou hast, and distribute unto the poor, and thou shalt have treasure in heaven: and come, follow me.

 

Do not leave this place unsaved.

 

The greatest trust is that your faith work by love, that you can have eternal life.

 

Faithfulness in our love and obedience that we may ENDURE TO THE END.

 

Mt 10:22

And ye shall be hated of all men for my name's sake: but he that endureth to the end shall be saved.

 

We are upon the evening shadows.

 

“Yield our life to you and when we yield to you we find eternal life.”

 

Let there be a stirring in our heart.

 

The children responded specially to Jesus, the touch of God.

 

Get beyond the shallowness of a movement, feeling, excitement os that we do not go away ‘exceedingly sorrowful.’

 

The rich ruler was VERY RICH, having so much that we could sell and give away and it wouldn’t bother him one bit. But Jesus says sell ALL.

 

If you are going to know about the kingdom of God you have to sell it ALL. Leaving no credit for self.

 

EVERY PART OF SIN REJECT AND BE SICK TOWARDS ITS PRESENCE.

 

Mt 19:17

And he said unto him, Why callest thou me good? there is none good but one, that is, God: but if thou wilt enter into life, keep the commandments.

 

You can’t just call Christ Good, you must also acknowledge He is God.

 

The greatest trust is when you surrender your life completelty to him, you take what he gives you from His hands. All that I have put it on the altar and put myself trusting Him. He will give that back which pertains to eternal life, but there are also rewards of obedience and faith for they bring great rewards.

 

The ruler was blinded to who Jesus was and so was blinded to what He could do. The ruler had ideas for his money but no idea what God could do with his life.

 

God has need of you!

Lu 19:30 Saying, Go ye into the village over against you; in the which at your entering ye shall find a colt tied, whereon yet never man sat: loose him, and bring him hither.

 Lu 19:31 And if any man ask you, Why do ye loose him? thus shall ye say unto him, Because the Lord hath need of him.
