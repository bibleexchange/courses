As Christians, we believe the marriage between One Man and One Woman is Honorable. Marriage is a life-time vocation for a man and a woman to join together in Holy Matrimony to bring children into this world and teach them morals, respect, right from  wrong. Because we want to preserve the traditional form of marriage does not mean that we are a bad person. We have an obligation to object whenever our Legislature enacts a law that is morally wrong. There is no in-between;  No Neutral ground it is morally right or it is wrong, good or evil, true or false, Pleasing to God or sin.

Our laws in this country were based on the 10 Commandments. The fifth commandment given to us by God is: Honor thy father and thy mother. Are we to ask God to change the wording of this as our Legislature decided to do? I think not.  Absolutely not.

If I turn my back on a law that devalues marriage and leaves the word meaningless, I am being irresponsible. It is up to every citizen of this state to preserve the institution of marriage for our future generations. To repeal the same-sex marriage law, we must vote "yes" on Question 1 on November 3, 2009.

Fight the good fight of faith now or lose every right you have to even voice your opinion at all later.

Our perspective is set in the right direction from the very beginning. If we neglect or reject God we are denying life to ourselves.                                                                      The fifth commandment is ordering us to honor our parents, God requires us to preserve honor and to perform the duties belonging to everyone, in their respective places and relationships, whether it be in the family, in the church or in society at large. He expressly directs our hearts to submit and obey our superiors, for that is a sign of our love for Him, for He is pleased to govern us through parents, civil magistrates and Pastors for our own good.
