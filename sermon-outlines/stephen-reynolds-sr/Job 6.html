Job 6:24 Teach me, and I will hold my tongue: and cause me to understand wherein I have erred.

Is to err, to sin?     Absolutely yes, to err is to sin.

Show me where I have erred, and I will confess my sin.

  1. “Teach me”
    1. Show me where I am mistaken.
    2. Bring proper arguments to convince me of my errors
      1. Any bad behavior
      2. Any selfish motive
      3. Any questionable attitude
      4. Any certain action

 

  1. “Teach me… cause me to understand”
    1. Convince me by solid arguments.  
    2. Made to understand

 

  1. “I will hold my tongue”
    1. Learn to surrender to God
    2. Learn to listen to God and to His methods
    3. Learn to teach quietness
    4. I will patiently hear and gladly receive your counsels.
      1. Instead of insinuations, give me plain instructions evidence and solid arguments, then I am ready to acknowledge my error:
      2. Teach me, and I will hold my tongue; for I have often found, how forcible right words are.

The context of this verse is found in Job 6:22-30

Job 6:25 How forcible are right words! but what doth your arguing reprove?

  1. A loose tongue will never make converts:
    1. What doth your arguing reprove?
      1. If your assumption is false,
      2. If your surmises are groundless,
      3. If your management is weak,
      4. If your application cross and callous.
  2. When your tongue is loose, suffering is hard, if suffering is hard to err is easy, but not always easy to know where you have erred.
    1. “Cause me to understand wherein I have erred”
      1. When did I err?    When your tongue was loose.
      2. What caused me to err?     A loose tongue will make you to err.
      3. Why did I err?
        1. When taught to understand did not hold your tongue.
