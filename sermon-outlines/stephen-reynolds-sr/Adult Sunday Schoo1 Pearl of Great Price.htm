V.            What the Merchant found

Mt 13:46 Who, when he had found one pearl of great price, went and sold all that he had, and bought it.

The merchant found “one pearl of great price “- a costly pearl. By geological standards the pearl is dust and irritations.. The lowly oyster can make one in three to five years, while it takes many years of fierce heat and pressure to create a diamond. Crush a pearl and all you have is a little heap of lime dust.

This natural Gentile treasure pearl is made of calcium carbonate, the same substance we find in ordinary chalk. Yet modern man, for all his skill and genius, cannot take calcium carbonate and turn it into a pearl.

Man can no more capture the living miracle of a pearl than he can capture a sunbeam and turn it into a star. He can make artificial pearls, cultured pearls by implanting smooth beads of mother-of-pearl into oysters so that they can grow the imitation. The counterfeit is so close to the natural that only experts can tell the difference, but there is no comparison in their value. One is real; the other is fake.

It is the same between a professing Christian and a pearl of Great price Christian. You cannot produce this on your own, only as He works in you as you pay the full price through the blood of His Cross and through the Word of His Cross..

Though God forgives his penitent believing people, He does not deliver them from all the consequences of their sins.  The evils of them they will feel, in numerous ways, to the end of life, and even transmit to future generations; while none who do not repent, and turn heartily to him, can escape eternal destruction.

 

He will not clear the impenitent guilty, that go on still in their trespasses:

He will not clear the guilty without faith and true repentance.
