One night of many in 1967 Annabelle at 18 years old was hiding in the shadows of a street meeting in Lincoln Park of Portland Maine, as she stood behind a tree she prayed Lord if you will forgive me of my backsliding send the preacher tonight to pray for me, she testified that as she turned to walk away that night she froze in her step and she said I know he is coming toward me. She turned and she broke out in tears as the man of God was coming toward her, she told him she was a backslider and needed to get right with God.

One night under the big Gospel tent after many nights Annabelle was on guard watching the tent, Dad had thought it was best to give her a break and he asked someone else to watch the tent that night, it was raining, all wet and cold  but, when Annabelle heard she cried and said that tent is my baby I am going to watch it and no one else.

 

Annabelle worked harder than grown men putting up and taking down her Tent.

To say the least we didn’t have the luxury of Porta-potties in the 1960’s and 70’s but no job got past Annabelle.

Annabelle would sell Potatoes and Apples with Dad to help make ends meet day after day into the nights. Sometimes we would catch her carrying the 50 lbs. of Potatoes or box of apples and we would have to run after and carry them for her.

 

She made all the flyers for the outside meetings with the old printing press, that was a weeks work in itself every meeting.

 

Annabelle always remembered everybody’s birthday and was so kind and generous with a nice card or at least a phone call.

 

Annabelle touched all of our lives forever and will be greatly missed in so many ways.
