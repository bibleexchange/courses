Silent prayer by itself does not edify

Speaking in tongues by itself does not edify as far as does not communicate to us the truth by itself without another gift working with it.

Gifts that edify the church.

 

The believer's zeal is not to be limited even if he has been misinformed and emphasizes the wrong gift. He is to straighten out his emphasis, keep his zeal, and direct his energy to edifying the church.

 

Keep your zeal.

The important gifts are those that are needed the most right now that build up faith for Christ.

 

Spiritual Gifts

 

1Co 14:1 ¶ Follow after charity, and desire spiritual gifts, but rather that ye may prophesy.

  1. Returning to Love
    1. “Follow after charity”
      1. Follow
        1. To pursue
        2. To press forward
        3. Keep your eyes on Jesus
    2. “Desire spiritual gifts “
      1. Desire
        1. To covet earnestly
        2. To have warmth of feeling for
        3. To be ambitious
        4. To be zealous
      2. The more we love God and men, the more we desire the gifts of God so that we can minister and help more effectively.
    3. “rather that ye may Prophesy”
      1. Prophesy
        1. foretell events
        2. Divine will revealed and spoken,
        3. Speak under inspiration,
        4. Exercise the prophetic office

 

Tongues

 

Tongues are a sign to warn unbelievers; prophecy is a gift to edify believers.

To Prophesy—The gift of speaking under the inspiration of God's Spirit both prediction and proclamation

1Co 14:22 Wherefore tongues are for a sign, not to them that believe, but to them that believe not: but prophesying serveth not for them that believe not, but for them which believe.

 

1Co 14:3 But he that prophesieth speaketh unto men to edification, and exhortation, and comfort.

      1. Edification
        1. Building
        2. Building by faith a great structure
      2. Exhortation
        1. Comfort- intreaty-standing close beside
        2. Plead
      3. Comfort
        1. Consolation-consoling until they are consoled

1Co 14:12 Even so ye, forasmuch as ye are zealous of spiritual gifts, seek that ye may excel to the edifying of the church.

If we will excel in spiritual gifts, we must seek the profit of the church.  

Seeing ye affect so much to have spiritual gifts, seek that ye may get those by which ye may excel in edifying the Church.

  • Seek that ye may excel
  • Seek that you may be able to convey truth in a clear and plain manner;

seek to be distinguished for that. It is one of the most rare and valuable endowments of the Holy Spirit.

"spiritual gifts"

Are zealous; earnestly desire. Excel to the edifying of the church; abound in these gifts in such a way as to edify the church.

Seek that ye may excel to the edifying of the church. If you seek for spiritual gifts, seek rather those that will build up the church, such as prophesying and teaching.

The edifying of the church. The purpose that should be controlling.

1Co 14:26 ¶ How is it then, brethren? when ye come together, every one of you hath a psalm, hath a doctrine, hath a tongue, hath a revelation, hath an interpretation. Let all things be done unto edifying.
