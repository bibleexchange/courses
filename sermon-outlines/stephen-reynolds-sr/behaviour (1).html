A Dramatic Lesson on How to Treat Others

 

How should we treat other people?

 

Luke 10:35—And on the morrow when he departed, he took out two pence, and gave them to the host, and said unto him, Take care of him; and whatsoever thou spendest more, when I come again, I will repay thee.

 

1Tim. 3:5—(For if a man know not how to rule his own house, how shall he take care of the church of God?)

 

Mat. 7:12—Therefore all things whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them: for this is the law and the prophets.

Luke 6:31—And as ye would that men should do to you, do ye also to them likewise.

 

Ephesians 4:32—And be ye kind one to another, tenderhearted, forgiving one another, even as God for Christ's sake hath forgiven you.

 

  • There is absolutely no limit to kindness
  • There is absolutely no limit to tenderness
  • There is absolutely no limit to forgiveness.

 

  1. The manner in which we treat others

 

The manner in which we treat others has a lot to do with the kind of persons we are.

  • If we are kind we treat others kindly.
  • If we are disrespectful, we treat others disrespectful.

 

Our actions reinforce either a kind nature or a disrespectful nature.

  • Kindness bears the fruit of kindness;
  • disrespect bears the fruit of disrespect.

 

So with all the good virtues and bad character traits.

 

    1. Have a mind for unity—      Eph. 4:3
    2. Have a method for unity—  Eph. 4:13

 

Disunity is not of God. God did not put us upon this earth to fight and argue and war against each other. God created us to live as brothers and sisters, walking together throughout life in a spirit of unity, building together that which is productive and fruitful and fulfilling.

 

  • Unity and brotherhood

 

This is the clear teaching of God's Holy Word.

Matthew 22:39—And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.

Romans 12:9—Let love be without dissimulation. Abhor that which is evil; cleave to that which is good.

1 Corinthians 1:10—Now I beseech you, brethren, by the name of our Lord Jesus Christ, that ye all speak the same thing, and that there be no divisions among you; but that ye be perfectly joined together in the same mind and in the same judgment.

2 Corinthians 13:11—Finally, brethren, farewell. Be perfect, be of good comfort, be of one mind, live in peace; and the God of love and peace shall be with you.
