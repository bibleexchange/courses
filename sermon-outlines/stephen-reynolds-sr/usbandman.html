Abiding in Christ with Joy and abounding in fruit is absolutely necessary.

  1. Abiding and abounding.
    1. The Branch abiding in the Vine is to be Fruitful.
      1. The attached branch receives nourishment by
        1. Revelation
        2. Answered prayers
        3. Converts

 

      1. The Fruitful branch is attached to the vine.

 

The believer is attached to Christ: he abides, dwells, lives, and walks in the very presence of Christ and never faces a moment when he is not attached and walking in Christ. A genuine believer walks in constant fellowship and prayer with the Lord. He is always abiding and sharing with the Lord.

      1. The vine is always nourishing the abiding branch to become more fruitful.

Christ is always sharing His life-giving Word, through Revelation, for the nourishment of the believer, always answering prayer and meeting the needs of the obedient believer.

    3).Believers are branches of this Vine.

    4).The root is unseen, and our life is hid with Christ;

    5). The root bears life to the branches and in Christ are all supports and supplies.

     

     The branches of the vine are many, All new creatures in the root, all are of one vine; thus all true Christians are Born Again in Christ.

     

    Believers, like the branches of the vine, are weak, and unable to stand but as they are borne up. (The Father is the Husbandman). Never was any husbandman so wise, so watchful, about his vineyard, as God is about his church, which therefore must prosper. We must be fruitful.

     

    From vines we look for grapes, from a Christian we look for a Christ like temper, disposition, and life.

     

    We must honour God by obeying His Word, and live righteously, godly and soberly; this is bearing fruit. The unfruitful are taken away. And even fruitful branches need pruning; for the best have notions, passions, and humours, that require to be taken away, which Christ has promised to perfect the sanctification of believers.       2Co 7:1; 1Jo 3:3

    The word of Christ is spoken to all for a cleansing virtue, as it works grace in, and corruption out. The more fruit we bring forth, the more we abound and the Lord is glorified. In order for fruitfulness, we must abide in Christ, We must have union with him by faith.        John 15:4-7

     

    The great concern of all Christ's disciples, is constantly depend upon Christ the only True Vine, and commune with him as you submit.   (1Pe 5:5)

    True Christians find by experience, that any interruption in the exercise of their faith, causes holy affections to decline, corruptions to revive, and comforts to disappear.

     Those who abide not in Christ, though they may flourish for awhile in outward profession, yet come to nothing. The fire is the fittest place for withered branches; they are good for nothing else.

    Let us seek to live more simply in and of the fulness of Christ, and to grow more fruitful in every good word and work, so may our joy in Him and in his salvation be full.
