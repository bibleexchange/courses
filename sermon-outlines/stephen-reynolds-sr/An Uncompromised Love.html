Charity is a special word for a Special Kind of Love to show to others for God’s Glory

Follow after Charity

1Cor. 14:1—Follow after charity, and desire spiritual gifts, but rather that ye may prophesy.

 

  1. Gifts are to be desired, especially prophecy.

 

A. Love is to be pursued above all else in life. Gifts, abilities, and service are important; but they are insignificant in comparison with love to the Glory of God. Love is the greatest need and the supreme answer to all the needs of men. It is when we love a person that we meet the needs of a person. In fact, if we truly love a person, then we will do all we can to edify that person to the Glory of God.

⇒  The word "follow" (diōkete) means to pursue, to persist, to continue on and on, never giving up until our love is to the praise of the Glory of God’s Grace.

 

B. Spiritual gifts are to be desired. We are to pursue love first, the more we love God and men, the more we covet the gifts of God so that we can minister and help the world of men more effectively.

⇒  The word "desire" (zēloute) means to covet earnestly; to be zealous and ambitious for.

The Kind of Person Christ Calls to be His Disciples

 

Mat. 4:19—And he saith unto them, Follow me, and I will make you fishers of men

 

What kind of person does Christ call?

Christ calls ordinary people who will simply make themselves available to Him to make them faithful.

 

Where were they when Jesus called His first disciples. They were out in the work-world. This is not to de-emphasize the importance of learning, but it does teach at least two things.

        First, position and power, wealth and security, religion and ever learning and not coming to the knowledge of the truth, can hurt and keep a person away from God. Such things can make a person so self-confident with personal assurance that he becomes useless to God. God is unable to work His power through him. The man's own abilities and energy block God's gifts and power from flowing through him.

 God can use those who love to the Glory of God, able and willing to respond together faithfully.

 

I.  The first men called. Traits seen in the men, traits that show what kind of person is called by Christ.

1.  The men called by Christ were brothers who worked together.

A brotherly spirit—the kind of spirit Christ desires of His followers. The kind of kingdom Christ is building is a kingdom of followers with a brotherly spirit.             with (Uncompromised Love)

 

Mat. 22:39—And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself.

John 13:35—By this shall all men know that ye are my disciples, if ye have love one to another.

15:12—This is my commandment, That ye love one another, as I have loved you.

Romans 12:10—Be kindly affectioned one to another with brotherly love; in honour preferring one another;

1Peter 1:22—Seeing ye have purified your souls in obeying the truth through the Spirit unto unfeigned love of the brethren, see that ye love one another with a pure heart fervently:

b.  Togetherness—  cooperation—a nature and willingness to serve together to the Glory of God

2.  The men called by Christ were industrious and frugal workers

 

Peter, Andrew, James and John were busy at work when Christ called them. Too many believers are sitting and are inactive. Therefore, they miss out on the higher calling of God.

3.  The men called by Christ were called to follow Him and they were to immediately follow Him.

The disciple is called to follow Christ personally, to attach himself to Christ before he does anything else. Personal discipleship, that is, personal attachment, is essential A person must first learn Christ by love, following Him, before he can serve Christ faithfully.

Mt 11:29 Take my yoke upon you, and learn of me; for I am meek and lowly in heart: and ye shall find rest unto your souls.

 The Lord's call is to a personal relationship, to become attached to Him, “Follow Me and I will make you” Our Love and our lives to His Glory
