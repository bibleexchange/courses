Herod was in perplexity when he heard some say John the Baptist was risen from the dead. It is a metaphor taken from a traveller, who in his journey meets with several paths, one only of which leads to the place whither he would go; and, not knowing which to take, he is distressed with perplexity and doubt. The verb comes from a, negative, and porov, a way or passage.
