Jer 23:29 Is not my word like as a fire? saith the LORD; and like a hammer that breaketh the rock in pieces?

 

It enlightens, warms, penetrates and consumes every part.

 

When it is spoken to the true prophet, it is like a fire shut up in his bones; he cannot retain it, he must publish it: and when published, it is like a hammer that breaks the rock in pieces; it is with Divine power, that causes both sinner and saint to feel its weight and importance.

 

Are not all my words strong, like fire?

 

Heb 4:12 For the word of God is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged sword, piercing even to the dividing asunder of soul and spirit, and of the joints and marrow, and is a discerner of the thoughts and intents of the heart.

 

Is not my word powerful, like fire?" or, Is not the power of my word like fire?

 

The sacred writings enlighten, convince, and convert the soul, with the Holy Spirit. Fire must be applied by an agent in order to produce its effects; and surely the hammer cannot break the rock in pieces, unless wielded by an able workman. And it is God's Spirit alone that can apply it; for we find it frequently read and frequently spoken, without producing any useful effects. True preachers of the Word of God may be distinguished from the false, those who run, though they are not sent,

 

Jer 23:21 I have not sent these prophets, yet they ran: I have not spoken to them, yet they prophesied.

 

The word of him who has his commission from heaven shall be as a fire and as a hammer; sinners shall be convinced and converted to God by it.

But the others, though they steal the word from their neighbour-borrow or steal a good sermon, yet they do not profit the people at all, because God did not send them,

 

Jer 23:32 Behold, I am against them that prophesy false dreams, saith the LORD, and do tell them, and cause my people to err by their lies, and by their lightness; yet I sent them not, nor commanded them: therefore they shall not profit this people at all, saith the LORD.

 

The power of God does not accompany the word in their ministry.

A meek spirit---gentle, tender, humble, mild, and considerate.

A spirit that is disciplined and under control at all times.

It does not flare up, talk back, act defensively, cut in, rant, rave, or go on and on, talking and talking. Neither does a meek spirit whine or whimper or act persecuted or take on a martyr complex

A meek spirit is: gentle, tender, meek, considerate, disciplined, and controlled.

But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, gentleness, goodness, faith, meekness, temperance: against such there is no law" (Galatians 5:22-23).

Wherefore lay apart all filthiness and superfluity of naughtiness, and receive with meekness the engrafted word, which is able to save your souls" (James 1:21).

 

But let it be the hidden man of the heart, in that which is not corruptible, even the ornament of a meek and quiet spirit, which is in the sight of God of great price" (1 Peter 3:4).

A quiet spirit means a quiet and peaceful spirit, a spirit that is at peace with God and with itself and that builds peace with its husband.

A quiet spirit spreads peace all throughout its home and around to everyone who enters its home.

The meek and quiet spirit is of great value to God. God has His eyes upon the Christian woman who has a meek and quiet spirit: "in the sight of God [they are] of great price.

Stand in awe, and sin not: commune with your own heart upon your bed, and be still (Psalm 4:4).

Be still, and know that I am God: I will be exalted among the heathen, I will be exalted in the earth (Psalm 46:10).
