Righteousness is Now Revealed

Romans 3:10-31

 

TEXT: Romans 3:21-26

 

The context is the whole chapter but especially verses 10 through 31 are dealing with the righteousness of God.

 

I.                   God’s Righteousness is Revealed in Jesus Christ

 

A.   “But now the righteousness of God without the law is manifested...” Rom. 3:21a

 

I Thank God today that we can understand that righteousness ib y Jesus Christ. If it was left to us we would fail. The law has shone to us that man can’t keep the law, cannot obey God on his own but needs a savior. Christ is the revelation from God.

 

1.     God righteousness for man is revealed without the law.

 

It is important that we understand where our righteousness lies. It is not in keeping the law for with that thinking it is only a matter of time before you mess up and if you break one part of the law the nyou are guilty of it all. Jesus Christ is the One that is going to provide for me and sustain me. If you try to keep yourself you’ll find yourself in a great battle and be defeated at some time. I need you Jesus. Without his help we are guaranteed to fail. Just relying on our own good nature and good works we will be in trouble some day. Jesus Christ is our righteousness. Our boast is in Him. Sometimes you feel so good inside and humble in his presence and tears come down your face. Other times it feels that everything is against you and yet the Joy of the Lord is in our heart. Th erighteousness of God is without the law that you can make it without the trouble that Isreal had. Israel had a lot of trouble.

 

Romans 3:23 We fail and we fail miserably. If it wasn’t for Jesus Christ to restore us and refresh we would be in trouble. If you are relying on your works of righteousness to save you will relize one day how dangerous that really is.

 

With the righteousness of God imparted to your heart yo uhave a different heart, a different character. 2 Cor. 5:17 he will manifest himself to you and then impart his righteousness to you to the point that you will be like him.

 

a.      The law requires obedience but can’t produce it. Rom. 3:19,20; Isa. 64:6

 

Grace doesn’t excuse sin, but produces the power to obey God. You can be obedient to the Lord, but not just by your own grit and grind. You have got to believe the Lord. If you are still failing you are trusting in yourself. To trust in myself and do things my way I have to trample under my feet his blood.

 

Heb. 6:6 If they shall fall away, to renew them again unto repentance; seeing they crucify to themselves the Son of God afresh, and put him to an open shame.

 

(You are going to have trust God in order to understand truth and for it to be revealed.)

 

vs.1,4 If you come to know Christ as Saviour and know his grace how He works and you refuse and reject him.

 

At the dump the other day. “We give no benefits to any church.” If you don’t it is fine, but I just saw his attitude and the look on his face was so hard. Perhaps he could have been hurt by people or a particular church body and have reason to be bitter, but there is no reason for him to have that kind  of feeling about Jesus.

 

It is impossible because there is nothing you can say they don’t already know. The Holy Spirit has drawn and spoken to them already and they have refused. You can not come to God just at any time you want.

 

Let Jesus be savior he’ll not only take away the pain, but that which causes it---sin. Once you know Christ as Saviour and you trample him under you feet, there remains no other means of salvation. It is one thing to walk over the factthat he shed his blood, but it says trampling over Christ himself. We must be fully surrendered to him.

 

 

 

b.     The righteousness of God through Jesus Christ is given to every believer. Rom. 3:22;

Rom. 1:17; 10:4; I Cor. 1:30; Phil. 3:9

 

If you show that you love him (John 14:21,23) He will reveal Himself to you. As you keep his commandments, he will reveal himself to you. Do what you do know and God will speak to you.

 

There has been times when I have need to get right off a ladder at work and cry to the Lord, I am your child, I need you. As we surrender in a humble such as that the warmth and presence of god heals and brings joy, a song and even tears with a smile. Love still works when it is worked against. If you have the Love of God in your heart you can be laughed at hated and it not effect your spirit. This can be used to your advantage to reach out the unbeliever.

 

c.      The righteousness of God is given to every Believer to possess.

 “unto all” (righteousness to all) Rom. 3:23; II Peter 1:4

 

UNTOL ALL THAT BELIEVE; we have all sinned but He comes by and restores.

 

d.     The righteousness of God laid “upon” the believer as a covering.

Eph. 4:24; Col. 3:3,10; II Cor. 5:21; Rev. 3:18; 19:8

 

Not just possess as in our spirit, but UPON. He will clothe you and lay it upon you. That is for you to feel to know that he is there and real. How do you know that God is real? I can feel the results of what God has laid on my Spirit .If you want the warmth of God’s love in your heart, it is the work of the Holy Ghost. We see inconsistencies in believers because there is a lack of covering. Feeling God about what we are doing only builds self righteousness. How can you lay in bed and be lazy and not day what god has asked and yet say you have faith? When you are sick is the best time to come to church! You can have an immediate manifestation of god’s power in our body at these times. And receiving this, we have a testimony. Know that the word of God is true, trust His Word, and watch Him produce the miracle.Witht his righteousness upon us, we must receive it and act.

 

2.     God’s righteousness through His Son is witnessed by the law and the prophets. Rom. 3:21

 

This is no new thing on God’s side. For a long time man has struggled to do his own thing his own way. The Holy Ghost points everything to Christ today. Heis the Savior healer, deliverer. He knows how to fix it.

 

It was given then. They didn’t receive it not understanding it, but it was given.

 

a.      Righteousness is imputed to our hearts by faith. Rom. 4:1-5, 9-25

 

‘look unto Abraham’ a testimony of Abraham what God can do for every believer by FAITH. As you believe and obey his righteousness is imputed so that our heart and mind change.

 

On Mount Moriah Abraham was willing to sacrifice his son having enough understanding of the sacrifice provided by God. “seeing the place afar off”

 

How do you know that you have a kite up there? It being way up in the clouds. “I can feel the tug on the line.”

 

Abraham was the man God spoke of as His friend. Ex. 3: Moses when they ask who sent you tell them the God of Abraham. Luke 16 “Abraham’s Bosom” “they have Moses and the prophets” We are important today with Christ in our heart. This vessel is the house of God and temple of the Holy Ghost. It is Christ in you that has made this temple great. Having an understanding of who Christ is, the least in the kingdom of heaven is greater than John the Baptist.

Not everybody listens to what is preached. Acts 19—we are john’s followers we haven’t even heard if there be any Holy Ghost. He was preaching things that they weren’t hearing. When we know truth we have a responsibility to preach it. And when people don’t listen, we can’t at every turn just say ‘ you viper’ timing is so important. The Scripture speaks much of the cup of iniquity being full and God’s timing for judgment. We have to be careful that we don’t just say things to hard hearted people just to correct them. John had the right attitude and died a martyr. If you aren’t careful and try to be like John and you could end up like him. Theirs is something more than just being like a john, Isaiah, Abraham. The least in the King of heaven is greater than John. If you will obey Christ he can take you to a level above all of those beforehand. It is Christ in your life that makes your life important. “His gentleness has made me great.”
