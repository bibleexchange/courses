3056.  logov  logos,  log'-os      (All the WRITTEN WORD -  All the SCRIPTURES)

something said (including the thought); a topic (subject of discourse), also reasoning (the mental faculty) or motive; Method;

 

specially, (with the article in John) the Divine Expression (Christ):--account, cause, communication,

 X concerning, doctrine,  X intent, matter, mouth, preaching, question, reason, + reckon, remove, say(-ing), shew, X speaker, speech, talk, thing, + none of these things move me, tidings, treatise, utterance, word, work.

 

John did not say that "the Word" was the God (ho theos).

He says "the Word" was God (Theos).

He omits the definite article (the). John was saying that "the Word," is Jesus Christ

  • Jesus Christ is of the very nature and character of God the Father, but He is not the identical person of God the Father.
  • Jesus Christ is a distinct person from God the Father, but He is of the very being and essence (perfection) of God the Father.
  • Jesus Christ Is the Divine Expression (Christ) The Eternal Son of God

When a man sees Christ, he sees a distinct person, and he sees a person who is of the very substance and character of God in all of His perfect being.

 

The Word is eternal, timeless, supernatural, DIVINE

The Word is not a creation that came into existence at "the beginning,"

The Word is before the beginning.
