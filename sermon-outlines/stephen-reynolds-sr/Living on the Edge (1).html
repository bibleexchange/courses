Living on the Edge

 

Job 26:14 Lo, these are parts of his ways: but how little a portion is heard of him? but the thunder of his power who can understand?

 

What we know of the ways of God is but little, very little, compared with what remains to be known, and which will be unfolded with greater and greater clearness and glory for ever.

 

     14. parts--Rather, "only the extreme boundaries of," and how faint is the whisper that we hear of Him!

    thunder--the entire fulness.  The opposite to "whisper"

6 questions to examine your life with, am I living on the edge?

1. How hast thou helped him that is without power?

2. How savest thou the arm that hath no strength?

3. How hast thou counselled him that hath no wisdom?

            4. Hast thou plentifully declared the thing as it is?

5. To whom hast thou uttered words?

6. Whose spirit came from thee?

Job had shown himself not inferior to the friends' inability to describe God's greatness, so now he describes it as manifested in hell (the world of the dead), Job 26:5,6; on earth, Job 26:7; in the sky, Job 26:8-11; the sea, Job 26:12; the heavens, Job 26:13.

Parts (only the extreme boundaries of visible evidence)

 

    1. V.5  Dead things are formed from under the waters

 

      • Job begins to declare the force of God's power and providence in the mines and metals in the deep places of the earth.

Oil, Gold, silver, pearls, diamonds, precious stones.

 

    1. V.6 Hell is naked before him and destruction hath no covering.

 

      • V. 6.  Hell-Is in his presence, and under his providence.  Hell itself, that place of utter darkness, is not hid from his sight. Destruction-The place of destruction, soul’s of men that have perished.

 

    1. V.7 He stretcheth out the north over the empty place, and hangeth the earth upon nothing.

 

      • Ps 104:2 Who coverest thyself with light as with a garment: who stretchest out the heavens like a curtain:

 

      • V. 7.  North-The northern part of the heavens, which is put for the whole visible heaven,  Job and his friends lived in a northern climate.  Nothing-Upon no props or pillars, but his own power and providence.
