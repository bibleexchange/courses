The prophecy of Habakkuk is concerning the rise of the Chaldeans to power.  

God called Abram, the man of idolatry, from the Ur of the Chaldees and made him Abraham, the man of faith.  

Judah is now facing judgment from God by the hand of the Chaldeans because they have turned to idols.  God used the Chaldeans to judge Judah even though they were heartless, cruel and bent wholly on evil, but in the end he would avenge His people of their adversary and restore His people.

 

Hab. 1:6-3:2

 

The book begins with a wail of despair, but ends with a shout of confidence.  The secret of this faith, Hab. 2:4, is with those that dwell with the Lord in His temple.  2:20

2:4   “His faith” – full of faith

2:20 “His holy Temple” – the fullness of His presence

3:19 “His strength is “my strength” – the fullness of His power
