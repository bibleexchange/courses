This consecration of the priests was a shadow of good things to come.

  1.  Our Lord Jesus is the Great High-Priest of our profession,
    1. Called of God to be  consecrated for evermore,
    2. Anointed with the Spirit above his fellows
    3. He is called Messiah, Christ
      1. Clothed with the holy garments, even with glory and beauty,
      2. Sanctified by his own blood,
        1. Made perfect, or consecrated, through sufferings, Heb. 2:10
        2. Not that of bullocks and rams (Heb. 9:12),
        3. This was a perpetual statute to be fulfilled in Christ and believer’s

Ex 29:9 And thou shalt gird them with girdles, Aaron and his sons, and put the bonnets on them: and the priest's office shall be theirs for a perpetual statute: and thou shalt consecrate Aaron and his sons.

Heb 9:9 Which was a figure for the time then present, in which were offered both gifts and sacrifices, that could not make him that did the service perfect, as pertaining to the conscience;

  1. All believers are called to be Spiritual Priests,
    1. To offer spiritual sacrifices (1 Pt. 2:5),
    2. Washed in the blood of Christ, and so made to our God priests, Rev. 1:5, 6.
    3. They also are clothed with the beauty of holiness,
    4.  Have received the anointing, 1 Jn. 2:27.
    5. Their hands are filled with work, to which they must continually attend;
    6. It is through Christ, the great sacrifice, that they are dedicated to this service.
    7. His blood sprinkled upon the conscience purges it from dead works, that they may, as priests, serve the living God.
