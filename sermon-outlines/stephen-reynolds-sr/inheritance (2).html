I Peter 1:3-5

 

  1. INHERITANCE DEFINED
    1. “Kleronomia” – (noun)
      1. Property which passes from father to son upon the father’s death.
      2. That which is received by a son when he becomes of age.
      3. That which is received as a gift.
      4. The heavenly reward of the saved. – Col. 3:24, Heb. 9:15
    2. “Kleros” – (noun)
      1. A lot (as in casting of lots)
        1. What is obtained by lot.
        2. An allotted portion.
      2. A portion or share of something.
      3. A piece or portion of land.
    3. “Kleronomeo” – (verb)
      1. From two words.
        1. “kleros” – a lot.
        2. “Nemonmai” – to posses.
      2. To inherit – to obtain God’s promises and portion for the saints, to receive as ones own.
