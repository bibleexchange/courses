  1. God's knowledge of sin

 

Ac 24:22 ¶ And when Felix heard these things, having more perfect knowledge of that way, he deferred them, and said, When Lysias the chief captain shall come down, I will know the uttermost of your matter.

The excellency of the knowledge of Christ]  That superior light, information, and blessedness which come through the Gospel of Jesus Christ; justification through his blood, sanctification by his Spirit, and eternal glory through his merits and intercession. These are the blessings held out to us by the Gospel, of which, and the law, Jesus Christ is the sum and substance.

 

  1. God sent His Son to bless us.
  1. O God, thou knowest my foolishness.
  2. My sins are not hid from thee.
    1. It is an encouragement to repent.
      1. It is foolish to endeavor to hide any sin from him
      2. It is impossible to know and confess all our sin to him without His help.
    2. It is an encouragement to hope for pardon.
      1. Because, in the full knowledge of sin, he has declared himself to be merciful and ready to forgive.
      2. Because he has made provision for pardon, not according to our knowledge of sin, but his own.

 10 When I wept, and chastened my soul with fasting, that was to my reproach.

Ver. 10-11.  That Christ was derided and scoffed at is plain, from Mark 5; for, when he said, "The girl is not dead, but sleepeth, they laughed him to scorn;" and when he spoke of the necessity of giving alms, "Now, the Pharisees, who were covetous heard all these things, and they derided him."   1. Jesus was being persecuted
  1. “they persecute him whom thou hast smitten “
    1. He bore to tears in prayer the pain, ridicule and persecution that we deserved
      1. As believers we will face persecution
      2. As believers we can rejoice in suffering for His name sake
    2. He bore to death the sin and the shame in judgment what we deserved
      1. As Believers through faith in His death there is a death to sin
      2. As Believers through faith in His resurrection there is an end to being ashamed, condemned, conquered.  Rom.1:16; 8:1, 31-39
  2. “they talk to the grief of those whom thou hast wounded”
    1. Being in agony He prayed more earnestly
      1. As unbelievers men’s rage reaches heaven
      2. As our Saviour He suffered and died in our place
      3. God can make even our enemies to be at peace with us
