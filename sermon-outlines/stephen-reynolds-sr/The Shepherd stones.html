⇒ Judah was assigned leadership of Israel and appointed to be the line of the very special promised seed, the Savior of the world

⇒ Joseph, given the double portion of the birthright that usually went to the firstborn son; that is, Joseph received the inheritance which included a double portion of the promised land. The first two sons were to receive an equal portion with the other sons of Israel. Joseph himself was not personally assigned a part of the inheritance, but his two sons each received an equal share. Thus Joseph was represented in his two sons.

The prophetic blessing that is pronounced upon Joseph is very descriptive. the prophecy refers to his two sons, Ephraim and Manasseh.

  1. Joseph was to be like a fruitful vine sitting near a spring that has fruitful branches climbing all over a wall

Genesis 49:22 Joseph is a fruitful bough, even a fruitful bough by a well; whose branches run over the wall:

This is a picture of fruitfulness. The tribes of Ephraim and Manasseh were to bear large numbers of people and strong leaders. As an example, these two tribes produced such leaders as Joshua and five of the fifteen judges of Israel: Gideon, Abimelech, Jair, Jephthah, and Samuel.

  1. Joseph was, however, to be harassed and hated by his enemies

Genesis 49:23 The archers have sorely grieved him, and shot at him, and hated him:

 

The attacks against him would be like an archer shooting at him with bitter hatred and hostility. History would later show that Ephraim and Manasseh, along with Judah, would have to bear the major thrust of the opposition against Israel.

  1. Joseph was to stand firm and steady. God was to make him strong

Genesis 49:24 But his bow abode in strength, and the arms of his hands were made strong by the hands of the mighty God of Jacob; (from thence is the shepherd, the stone of Israel:) 25 Even by the God of thy father, who shall help thee; and by the Almighty, who shall bless thee with blessings of heaven above, blessings of the deep that lieth under, blessings of the breasts, and of the womb:

 

The tribes of Ephraim and Manasseh were to be like bows that would stay firm, and God was to make their arms strong so that they could just keep on letting the arrows fly against their enemies. Again, history has shown that the two tribes, especially Ephraim, were often victorious in battle.

But note why: because of God. The descendents of Joseph were to be made strong and victorious because of God. This fact is stressed five different times. Five different names or titles are given to God declaring that the strength of Joseph's descendents was due to God and to God alone.

a. The mighty One (God) of Jacob (Genesis 49:24): God had strengthened Jacob time and again. Jacob had sometimes been weak, but when he called upon God, God had strengthened him. Therefore, he knew that God—the mighty God—would strengthen Joseph and his descendents.

But there is more here. Note that God is "the mighty God of Jacob." This is personal: Jacob had a personal relationship with God, a very close relationship. He knew God personally: walked and talked with God and experienced the strength of God day by day. Jacob had such a close relationship with God that Scripture often refers to the God of Jacob, especially in the Psalms and Prophets.

 

The LORD of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our refuge....The LORD of hosts is with us; the God of Jacob is our refuge" (Psalm 46:7, 11).

c. The Stone or Rock of Israel (Genesis 49:24): this is the picture of God as the Foundation and Support of life. Jacob (Israel) had built his life upon God: he had made God the very foundation and support of his life, and God had held him up and given him a sure and lasting foundation. His life had been built upon God; therefore, God was going to fulfill His great promises to him. God was going to allow him-his life, the building and structure of his life—to endure forever and ever. And God was going to do the same thing for Joseph. This, too, is the first time God is called the Rock or Stone in Scripture.

d. The God of Jacob or the God of your father who helps you (Genesis 49:25): God had always helped Jacob. Time and again Jacob had faced problems, sometimes terrible circumstances, but God had helped him through them all. The personal relationship between God and Jacob is again being stressed. The God of Jacob was the true God; therefore, He was able to really help Jacob. Joseph was following the God of his father; therefore, the true God would really help Joseph and his descendents.

e. The Almighty (El Shaddai) (Genesis 49:25a): this name stresses that God is the God of all might and strength, the true God of heaven and earth. He is the God who can do all things for His people. The almighty God is, therefore, the One who will strengthen Ephraim and Manasseh against their enemies.

4. But Joseph will not only be strengthened against his enemies; he will be richly blessed with three things:

⇒ with the blessings of heavenly rain and fertile soil (Genesis 49:25b).

⇒ with the blessings of springs and streams (Genesis 49:25b).

⇒ with the blessings of many offspring or descendents (Genesis 49:25c).

5. But even that is not all: Joseph was to be blessed above all his brothers (Genesis 49:26). Jacob declared that he had been blessed above his own fathers, Abraham and Isaac. Therefore Joseph, as the recipient of the birthright, was to be blessed above all his brothers. Jacob actually called out for his own great blessings to rest upon the head of Joseph, for the great blessings he had known to be experienced by Joseph.

Ehud the second judge, and Saul the first king, were of this tribe; and so also in the last times Esther and Mordecai, by whom the enemies of the Jews were destroyed, were of this tribe. The Benjamites ravened like wolves when they desperately espoused the cause of the men of Gibeah, those men of Belial, Jg 20:14. Blessed Paul was of this tribe (Ro 11:1; Php 3:5); and he did, in the morning of his day, devour the prey as a persecutor, but, in the evening, divided the spoil as a preacher.

This was to be a contentious tribe.  Though Benjamin stood high in his father's natural affection, he did not dare for that reason to invent a blessing for him, but speaks the word of the Lord neither less nor more.  To fight from morning to night is a sorry business, unless it be against sin.
