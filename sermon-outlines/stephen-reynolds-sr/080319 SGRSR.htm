Deliverance Center – Portland, Maine

Rev. Stephen G. Reynolds, Sr.

March 19, 2008, Wednesday PM

Title: Our Responsibility to Guard the Field

Text: Matthew 13:24-30

 

INTRODUCTION

 

We have a responsibility. Where god holds us responsible he has provided much. He has guaranteed to reward us if we remain faithful.

 

v.25 While men slept the enemy sold tares

 

God has given us a responsibility to guard the field and pay it careful attention. God has planted some good seed into our heart and into our life. We must guard against the enemies sowing of tares.

 

I.                  We Must Realize We Have a Responsibility to Keep the Enemy Away and Keep Bad Seed Out of Our Lives

 

We have a responsibility. If we don’t realize our responsibility we’ll miss church, give excuse for un-involvement in things you once loved.

 

People let the devil come by and put wedges of doubt and questions in their mind. “Oh, I don’t have time to spend with you today.” Love brought into question because of tares and wedges. As important as our family and friends are and we have a responsibility in protecting those relationships. We must make sure our hearts and lives are guarded because there is an enemy that comes by and loves to sow tares.

 

People wanted to go in and gather up the tares quickly, but you cannot change people. You cannot fix their problems for them. (Mote beam and splinter in your eye lesson from Jesus applies here as well.) UNLESS THEY wake up and realize something is wrong with them (Matthew 11:28) they can’t be changed.

 

II.               When We Realize His Gifts are Good, We have a Responsibility to Guard Those Things

 

He has promised to keep us and his keeping power is real. We would have no problem with the enemy or the flesh if we kept our faith in working order through a dedicated and consistent prayer life. If you are going to have His gifts you must come to Him. Come and keep coming. In a place of worship, prayer, and praise to the Lord we have FELLOWSHIP with God.

 

We must share what God has given us to others.

If it is not the seed God has planted it is a tare.
