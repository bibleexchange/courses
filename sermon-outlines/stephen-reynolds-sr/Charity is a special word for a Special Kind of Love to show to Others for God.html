An Uncompromised Love

Charity is a special word for a Special Kind of Love to show to others for God’s Glory

The King James translators purposely chose the word charity in some places and love in others. They saw an importance in distinguishing some of the uses of {“agape”} in a specific way.                             

Some form of the word charity is found in the Bible 29 times.

Our service is to be properly motivated if it is to be consistent. We must recognize our service to our fellow man is in reality service to Christ. A motivation to serve Christ will outlast a motivation to simply help people.

1Cor. 13:3—And though I bestow all my goods to feed the poor, and though I give my body to be burned, and have not charity, it profiteth me nothing.

“Charity” is more than giving, according to I Cor. 13:3. Giving is not enough.                                                    “Charity” is more than loving but loving for the Glory of God, by the Power of God and through the Grace and Knowledge of God to the edifying of those of like precious faith.
