TITLE: Freely Justified, Shamefully Ignorant

TEXT: I Corinthians 15:34; Romans 3:24

 

1Co 15:34-–Awake to righteousness, and sin not; for some have not the knowledge of God: I speak this to your shame.

 

Am I awake? You are not going to know righteousness just by being told, but must apply the truth to our lives.

 

Without God’s grace, I will not make it. God will do it if we trust Him. We need to walk closer to the Lord in worship. We need to live righteous and sin not.

 

Romans 3:24—Being justified freely by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus:

Justified = your sin has been dealt with; just-if-I’d never sinned

FREELY JUSTIFIED (Rom. 3:24) - SHAMEFULLY IGNORANT (I Cor. 15:34)

 

The initial evidence of being born again is cleanness inside, a feeling of freedom. There is a sense that something is gone and now I am free. What price did we pay? But what price did Christ pay? He paid the supreme price for our soul’s salvation. There is no reason for any soul to remain lost.

 

Stay with the Word. The Holy Ghost is faithful. We get in the way. We do not always allow the Spirit to move like He wants.

His grace is provided for us freely.

We ought to maintain an atmosphere of praise every time the saints come together, so that the anointing may be present to bring sinners to repentance.

 

1Ti 6:12—Fight the good fight of faith, lay hold on eternal life, whereunto thou art also called, and hast professed a good profession before many witnesses.

 

We must fight the fight of faith, it is our responsibility.

 

1Co 15:56–The sting of death is sin; and the strength of sin is the law. 57 But thanks be to God, which giveth us the victory through our Lord Jesus Christ. 58 ¶ Therefore, my beloved brethren, be ye stedfast, unmoveable, always abounding in the work of the Lord, forasmuch as ye know that your labour is not in vain in the Lord.

 

If there is going to be a victory has got to be a fight. But we need to have more than just a desire to fight. We need to know why and what we are fighting. Sin gives sin the power to operate.

1Co 15:42—So also is the resurrection of the dead. It is sown in corruption; it is raised in incorruption:

 

Those that are raised in incorruption will not receive the same reward as those that recognize death is an enemy and overcome it.

I Cor. 15 is based on the fact of Christ’s resurrection. Resurrection life is possible, but there is a greater reward in defeating death.

The Law gives strength. Sin becomes sin when the nature of God is revealed. Anything that is not according to His power, glory, and righteousness is sin.

We have no foundation to work from if we don’t convince men they are sinners. It is the Law that gives sin its power to sting and to destroy. You commit sin (any abomination in God’s sight) these things – It is the truth that makes men free.

If you commit sin, you are judged to death.
