1      The children in the orphanage are wearing clothes that are threadbare and shoes that are falling apart. Containers holding clothes and other supplies for the orphanage are not delivered frequent enough to meet the needs of the orphans. It is promised that a shipment will go out immediately, but it is several months to a year before the is a shipment.

2      There are no screens or glass in the windows of the dorms of the orphans and neither are they supplied with mosquito nets for their beds.

3      Jonas Kadaghe has affirmed that many of the good and new thing on the orphanage grounds have been installed or repaired right before a group has come from America. It is good to clean up before visitors come, but we also must remember that these children are there all year and deserve repairs meant for them and not the people coming to view the grounds.

4      The orphans have confirmed that special food is purchased and served at times when a group from America is on the grounds.

5      Regular meals at the orphanage consist of either rice and beans, stiff porridge, white corn and beans,  or breakfast porridge with hardly any allowance given to provide the children with fruits, vegetables, or meat to give the children proper nourishment.

6      L. K. Powers was told repeatedly that we needed earth-rods to provide proper grounding to electricity on the orphanage grounds, but he has neglected to issue their installment. This is a liability for physical danger due to electrical shock.

7      Matthew Derocher has personally witnessed Samson's church in Mwica and bears witness that the building is quite incomplete. In America he was lead to believe by L. K.  Powers that the building was completely finished. Jonas Kadaghe affirms that the $15,000 that was raised was not all invested in the project.

8      L. K. Powers decided to remove insurance from the Toyota truck, which was purchased for $24,000 that was raised by people in America. He deemed that it was too expensive though it was only approximately $600 per year. The vehicle was in an accident and we would have received approximately $15,000.

9      Instead of using a convenient and reliable method of transferring money (such as PayPal or a linked bank account) to Africa L. K. Powers chooses to send money through Western Union. This service imposes a hefty 10% fee and it is also to be noted that these transactions are very hard to trace.

10   The church built in Gonga is financially supported in large part by Elijah. As the founding organization of the church, Landmark World Missions could help with the churches it creates.

11   The orphanage not finished.

12   Money is owed to a local merchant named V.J. The money is for chairs purchased for the church in Gonga. As a good steward, this debt should be paid off before new endeavors are pursued.

13   The board of directors for Landmark World Missions does not meet very often and is not privy to major financial decisions.

14   Money is often displaced and is not used for the purpose for which it was raised. Finances were raised during Landmark Youth Camp 2011 for the purpose of buying many Bibles and money has been raised for a radio tower in the Rift Valley, but the money raised for these items was not used for the promised reasons.

15   No lesson has been apparently learned from the vehicle that was totaled without insurance. The new vehicle has no insurance either.

16   Where is the money that was raised in convention? Close to $20,000 in cash was raised at Landmark’s convention in April of 2012. Jonas says aprox. $3,000 USD has been put into the building project on the grounds of Safe Haven Orphanage since then.

17   Electricity bill went unpaid for four months. It was shut off because of being unpaid. It was turned on the day before Matthew Derocher arrived (Sept. 1).

18   A container from America has not been shipped in over two years.

What can be done to make Landmark World Missions a valid and trustworthy institution?

●     Changes in Africa will not be as lasting if there are not first changes in America in the way things are run.

●     Transparency--everything needs to be done above board. Instead of waiting for people to ask questions, we should give them answers before they think to ask.

●     Internal financial reports need to be made at least quarterly and reviewed by the board members.

●     A public financial report should be made at least annually, but better bi-annually.

●     The board should meet quarterly.

●     The board should vote on any major financial decisions before L. K. Powers makes any promises in Africa or stirs any hope of physical materials.

●     Separate accounts should be opened for large projects such as the building of churches that have received designated donations.

●     Local African workers should be evaluated for performance.

●     Contrary to the old adage, pictures do lie. We need to make the newsletter more of a solid presentation of facts instead of stories and empty promises. It would be good if several people contributed to the newsletter instead of just one, as it has been. If Jonas or someone else from Africa could give a report in them, then the people would have a clearer picture of what is going on.

 

Orphan program

●     Why cannot we run the orphanage by monthly sponsors of each orphan? At one time this method was attempted, but was not structured in a way so that it would continue.

●     Included in the cost should be their personal needs, food, & a percentage of the overhead (i.e. salaries of the workers & electricity).

●     The sponsors of the children must have a way to communicate with the children and vice versa. They should have a way to send emails and receive several pictures of the child each year.

●     Payment could be set up in an automatic way using merchant services on the internet.

●     When a child is discharged from the orphanage, the sponsor should be informed. They could be given an option to sponsor a new orphan if one is available, or receive an email notice when one is available if on is currently not.

●     If they ever want to stop sponsoring a child, there should be a clear and painless way to do so.

●     They should receive newsletters and financial reports so they can see where their money has gone.

●     Perhaps it would be needed to hire someone to take care of correspondence between the sponsors and the children.
