The Disciples Love for Christ

 

John 15:9 As the Father hath loved me, so have I loved you: continue ye in my love.

 10 If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments, and abide in his love.

 

It is a profound truth that The Father loves The Son,

And the Son loves us as believers with that same love.

The love that the Father and The Son has manifested is commanded to be in us.

 

  1. Every disciple of Christ must continue to walk and abide in Christ to know that love.

Eph 5:2 And walk in love, as Christ also hath loved us, and hath given himself for us an offering and a sacrifice to God for a sweetsmelling savour.

1John 1:6 And this is love, that we walk after his commandments. This is the commandment, That, as ye have heard from the beginning, ye should walk in it. 2:6 He that saith he abideth in him ought himself also so to walk, even as he walked.

 

The believer must study, learn, know and abide in Christ, in the Word of God, in the commandments; so the life and love of Christ is learned and followed.

Mt 11:29 Take my yoke upon you, and learn of me; for I am meek and lowly in heart: and ye shall find rest unto your souls.

Follow on to know the LORD:

Ho 6:3 Then shall we know, if we follow on to know the LORD: his going forth is prepared as the morning; and he shall come unto us as the rain, as the latter and former rain unto the earth.

 

  1. Every disciple must love the Lord Jesus Christ A person cannot keep a commandment unless he knows the commandment.

 

Jesus stressed the commandment of love. Apostles stressed the commandment of love.

We must all stress the commandment of love.           

 

1Co 16:22 If any man love not the Lord Jesus Christ, let him be Anathema Maranatha.

 

John 14:15 If ye love me, keep my commandments.

23 Jesus answered and said unto him, If a man love me, he will keep my words: and my Father will love him, and we will come unto him, and make our abode with him.

 

John 16:27 For the Father himself loveth you, because ye have loved me, and have believed that I came out from God.

17:6 I have manifested thy name unto the men which thou gavest me out of the world: thine they were, and thou gavest them me; and they have kept thy word.

 

1John 2:24 Let that therefore abide in you, which ye have heard from the beginning. If that which ye have heard from the beginning shall remain in you, ye also shall continue in the Son, and in the Father.

 

1Jo 4:19 We love him, because he first loved us.

 

We increase our love for Christ and show our love by aligning our thoughts and actions with God’s word.

 

Ro 5:5 And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

 

His pure love directs and encourages us to become more like Him, pure and holy.

His love inspires us to walk in righteousness—not out of fear or obligation but out of an earnest desire to become more like Him because we love Him

 

Love is:

 

  • The measure of our Faith
  • The inspiration for our obedience
  • And the attitude of our discipleship.

 

Faith becomes a life

 rather than just a religion, a law or an act.

Obedience becomes a joy

rather than a burden

Discipleship becomes being full of good fruit,       

         [quality] rather than quantity.

  • Through Love we grow and abide in Christ
  • Through Joy we abide & abound in Christ
  • Through Peace abound & show forth Christ
  • Through Longsuffering show/ grow in Christ
  • Through Gentleness grow and strengthen
  • Through Goodness strengthen and continue
  • Through Faith we continue, follow and abide
  • Through Meekness abide/show forth Christ
  • Through Temperance show Christ completely

 

Divine love ---every act an extraordinary service.

Divine love---the only motive that takes you through cross bearing and fruit bearing.

Divine love---the cause to overcome lukewarmness into blessed dedication and consecration.

Christ’s Love for the Disciples

 

John 15:9 As the Father hath loved me, so have I loved you: continue ye in my love.

 10 If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments, and abide in his love.

 

There is a vital and intimate relationship between Christ and His disciples for the purpose of bearing much fruit.

 

Jesus has a very special relationship with believers that deliver them from an existence that is

 barren, empty, lonely, meaningless and sad.

 

God’s purpose is to have fruit bearing disciples.

 

Christ loves believers with the same love God has for Him.

 

John 10:17 Therefore doth my Father love me, because I lay down my life, that I might take it again.

18 No man taketh it from me, but I lay it down of myself. I have power to lay it down, and I have power to take it again. This commandment have I received of my Father.

 

 

 

  1. Christ love is unparalleled in it’s greatness.

 

John 15:13 Greater love hath no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends.

 

  1. Love’s crowning deed
    1. Christ died in our place
    2. Christ suffered our reproach and death
    3. Sacrificial love
      1. Self-sacrificing love

 

  • Christ’s dying on the cross in our place is a revelation of His love for us.
  • Christ shedding His blood made the divine atonement for our soul.

 

  1. Christ loves us because we are God’s children

 

Romans 8:16 The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God:

 9:26 And it shall come to pass, that in the place where it was said unto them, Ye are not my people; there shall they be called the children of the living God.

Gal. 3:26 For ye are all the children of God by faith in Christ Jesus.

 

  1. Christ loves us because we are obedient

 

John 14:21 He that hath my commandments, and keepeth them, he it is that loveth me: and he that loveth me shall be loved of my Father, and I will love him, and will manifest myself to him.

John 14:23 Jesus answered and said unto him, If a man love me, he will keep my words: and my Father will love him, and we will come unto him, and make our abode with him.

15:10 If ye keep my commandments, ye shall abide in my love; even as I have kept my Father's commandments, and abide in his love.

14 Ye are my friends, if ye do whatsoever I command you.

 

  1. Christ’s Supreme Love is Known.

 

Rom. 5:5 And hope maketh not ashamed; because the love of God is shed abroad in our hearts by the Holy Ghost which is given unto us.

 

    1. If we deny self, and take up the cross daily, and follow Christ

 

Luke 9:23 And he said to them all, If any man will come after me, let him deny himself, and take up his cross daily, and follow me.

 

1John 3:16 Hereby perceive we the love of God, because he laid down his life for us: and we ought to lay down our lives for the brethren.

 

Christ wants His Love and His life to abundantly flow in each disciple.

 

Love is commanded     Jn.15:9-10

Christ always loves; His love is always there.

 

It is up to man to walk in that love.

 

A man can never know and experience the Lord's love unless he walks in it.

  1. Christ charges every believer to continue in His Word and to abide in His love.

 

  1. "Continuing" or "abiding" is conditional.
    1.  A believer can...

•  break fellowship with Christ.

•  cease to keep his thoughts upon Christ.

•  walk out of the love of Christ.

•  get away from the love of Christ.

•  turn back to the world and old worldly friends

2Co 6:17 Wherefore come out from among them, and be ye separate, saith the Lord, and touch not the unclean thing; and I will receive you,                             18 And will be a Father unto you, and ye shall be my sons and daughters, saith the Lord Almighty.

• give himself back over to the lust of the flesh, the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life

1John 2:15 Love not the world, neither the things that are in the world. If any man love the world, the love of the Father is not in him.                                                 16 For all that is in the world, the lust of the flesh, and the lust of the eyes, and the pride of life, is not of the Father, but is of the world.

Jesus said it’s up to the believer to continue in His love.

How?

The believer continues in the love of Christ by drawing near and being good and seeking to please Him—very simply by obeying His commandments.
