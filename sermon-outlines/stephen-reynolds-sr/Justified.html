JUSTIFY MEANS TO PRONOUNCE AND TREAT AS RIGHTEOUS. It is pardoned and a thousand times more than forgiveness.

 You may wrong me and then ask for forgiveness and

 "I forgive you." But I have not justified you. I cannot justify you. But when God justifies a man, He says, "I pronounce you a righteous man.

 I am going to treat you as if you have never committed any sin." Justification means sin is all past and gone -- wiped out -- not merely forgiven, not merely pardoned; it means clearing the slate and setting the sinner before God as a righteous man, as if he had never sinned, as if he were as righteous as the Lord Jesus Christ Himself

"Amazing Grace, how sweet the sound that saved a WRETCH like me!"

GOD’S GRACE gives what we do not deserve! forgiveness, eternal life, heaven, salvation, deliverance, justification, Redemption

GOD’S MERCY does not give us what we do deserve!   

Wrath, Judgment, Eternal punishment, The lake of fire

"Redemption" "being delivered by the payment of a price." The Lord Jesus paid the price with His blood. Our salvation is FREE (Rom. 3:24) but it is certainly not CHEAP! A price had to be paid! The justice of God must be satisfied. The full penalty for sin must be paid.

"Set forth" The CROSS was a public display. It was not done in secret; it was publicly witnessed by men and angels.

"Propitiation" The key word to describe the work of Christ on the CROSS, with emphasis on the power of God unto salvation. Rom.1:16; I Cor.1:18; 1 John 2:2; 4:10 "PROPITIATION" is translated "MERCY SEAT." which covered the ark.

"To declare His righteousness" -- Through the cross, God demonstrated His righteousness and vindicated His Character.
