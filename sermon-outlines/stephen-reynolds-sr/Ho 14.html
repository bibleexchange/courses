(the just shall walk in the ways of the Lord)

Ho 14:9 Who is wise, and he shall understand these things? prudent, and he shall know them? for the ways of the LORD are right, and the just shall walk in them: but the transgressors shall fall therein.

 

  • The Just shall walk the transgressors shall fall
  • The Just shall walk in the ways of the Lord, the transgressors shall fall

 

The just shall walk in them] This is a truth which he will always acknowledge; and illustrate it by a righteous and godly life.

 

  “the transgressors shall fall therein”

Howsoever good they might have been before, if they do not consider the necessity of always depending upon God; of receiving all their light, life, power, and love from him; ever evidencing that faith which worketh by love; maintaining an obedient conduct, and having respect to all God's precepts; they shall fall, even in the "way of righteousness."

When still using the Divine ordinances, and associating with

God's people, they shall perish from the way; and be like Ephraim, who once "spoke trembling," and "was exalted in Israel," who was "God's beloved son," and "called out of Egypt;" yet, by "offending in Baal," giving way to "the idols of his heart," fell from God, fell into the hands of his enemies, and became a wretch in a heathen land.  

 

      "Whoso is wise, let him understand these things!

       Whoso is prudent, let him know them!"-----

 

Be well instructed and make proper application of what is taught from the word; tremble at the threatenings, and embrace the promises, of God.

 

In the Targum they said   (Ezra 4:18, 23; 5:5)

  "The ways of the Lord are right, and the just who walk in them shall live for ever; but the ungodly, because they have not walked in them, shall be delivered into hell."
