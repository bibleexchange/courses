    Commitment, Sacrifice, Duty, Devotion and Loyalty: there were three soldiers who broke through the Philistine lines simply to secure water for David.

    These three soldiers were from the elite force known as The Thirty.

    During David's fugitive years when he was being fiercely pursued by Saul, he and his men were apparently camped out in the cave at Adullam. While hiding out there, a band of Philistines camped out in the Valley of Rephaim right below the cave stronghold, trapping and blocking any hope of escape for David and his soldiers. Sitting there in the cave with no fresh water supply, the men apparently began to discuss how refreshing a drink would be from the good well water of Bethlehem. At some point during the discussion, David wishfully stated how much he would love to have a drink of that refreshing water from Bethlehem.

    Being so deeply devoted to David, three of his warriors set out to fulfill the desire of their commander (2 Samuel 23:16). Somehow they fearlessly broke through the enemy line and secretly made their way to Bethlehem where the Philistines had set up a garrison to keep the Israelites of the area under their control. Without being spotted, the three devoted soldiers walked up to the well and drew water, then brought it back to David. David was so overwhelmed by the sacrificial devotion of the men that he refused to drink the water (2 Samuel 23:16-17). Instead, he poured it out as a drink offering to the Lord. He valued the water so much that he used it as a symbol of blood, for it had been brought to him at the risk of the three warriors' lives (blood). Being symbolized as blood, the water could be used only as a drink offering presented to the Lord. A drink offering symbolized that the person was pouring out his life as an offering to God, submitting himself to the Lord—all he was and had.

    1. selfless devotion demonstrated by these three soldiers! They were deeply, sacrificially committed to their commander David. And their commitment is a dynamic example for us, teaching us that we must be sacrificially devoted to the Lord Jesus Christ. Christ has done so much for us:

    ⇒  Saved us from the sin and bondages of this life.

    ⇒  Given us His Holy Spirit to guide, teach, protect, and provide for us day by day as we walk throughout life.

    ⇒  Given us the power to overcome and conquer all the temptations and trials that confront us.

    ⇒  Given us perfect assurance, guaranteeing us that we are acceptable to Him and will live forever in heaven with Him.

    The Lord Jesus Christ has done so much for us that it explodes the human mind. Because of what He has done, we owe our lives to Him—all we are and have. We must give our hearts to Him, be totally devoted to Him, making an absolute commitment to follow Him and obey Him, loving and sacrificially devoting our hearts and lives to Him.
