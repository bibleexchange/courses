“let us go on unto perfection”

Heb 6:1 ¶ Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection; not laying again the foundation of repentance from dead works, and of faith toward God,

2 Of the doctrine of baptisms, and of laying on of hands, and of resurrection of the dead, and of eternal judgment.

3 And this will we do, if God permit.

 

 

  1. The design of this chapter is to charge the believer to advance beyond the knowledge, wisdom, understanding conscience, instructions, prudence, convictions, character and conduct of what they have experienced.

 

Php 1:11 Being filled with the fruits of righteousness, which are by Jesus Christ, unto the glory and praise of God.

Col 1:10 That ye might walk worthy of the Lord unto all pleasing, being fruitful in every good work, and increasing in the knowledge of God;

11 Strengthened with all might, according to his glorious power, unto all patience and longsuffering with joyfulness;

 

  1.  To warn them against the danger of not going forward.
  2. The warning of dead works
    1. Unfruitful works of darkness
      1. Worldliness
      2. Religious
      3.  

 

Eph 5:11 And have no fellowship with the unfruitful works of darkness, but rather reprove them.

 Tit 3:14 And let ours also learn to maintain good works for necessary uses, that they be not unfruitful.

      1. Wrong motive not obedience and love
      2. Working out of obligation not Joy
      3. Been manipulated and pressured not peace.
      4. Become more conscious of yourself, Not longsuffering
      5. Following Instructions but Not in gentleness
      6. Prudent but Not in all goodness
      7. Personal Convictions but Not faith
      8. Liked Character but Not meekness
      9. Clever conduct, Meditation, reasoning but Not temperance
  • Make the highest attainments possible in the knowledge of Christianity to manifest the divine life.
      1. keep  from apostasy,
      2. make all the advances which they possibly could make;
      3. he prepare them to receive the higher doctrines of the Christian religion
