Dan

One of the 12 tribes of Israel that who later became the Jewish people.

The tribe was named after the first of two sons born to Jacob (also called Israel) and Bilhah, the maidservant of Jacob's second wife, Rachel.

Nine of the other 11 tribes were also named after sons of Jacob, while two bear the names of Jacob's grandsons, children of Joseph.

Jacob's first wife, Leah, bore him six sons: Reuben, Simeon, Levi, Judah, Issachar, and Zebulun. With the exception of Levi, each was the father of a tribe. Two other tribes, Gad and Asher, were named after sons born to Jacob and Zilpah, Leah's maidservant. Two additional tribes, Dan and Naphtali, were named after sons of Jacob born of Bilhah, the maidservant of Rachel, Jacob's second wife.

Rachel bore Jacob two sons, Joseph and Benjamin. Though a tribe was named after Benjamin, none bore the name of Joseph. Two tribes, however, were named after Joseph's sons, Manasseh and Ephraim.

Ge 30:6 And Rachel said, God hath judged me, and hath also heard my voice, and hath given me a son: therefore called she his name Dan.

 

Rachel recognized that God had given the child. This shows some true belief in God, but not the level of trust shown by Leah. The name Rachel used for God is Elohim, the name that stresses God as the source of creation and life. She was not yet confessing God as Yahweh, the God of salvation and redemption (the covenant Keeping God).                                                                                              Rachel was saying that God had judged her before by giving her no children, but now He was vindicating her to be worthy of children. But note: this was not true. She was still being self-sufficient, boasting in herself and declaring that she herself had gained the favor of God, that she was now in God's will. This was just not so. Rachel had sinned and was still sinning...

 

• by being so prideful and self-sufficient.

• by encouraging immorality, encouraging her husband to lie with her maid-servant.

• by refusing to pray and trust God for children.

 

God overruled again and gave another child through the maid-servant, Bilhah.

 

Rachel named this son Naphtali, which means "struggling, wrestling."

Note the carnal, fleshly nature of Rachel here: she named the child in honor of her struggle with Leah, her struggle to please Jacob and to secure his favor.

Rachel's envy seemed to reach a peak by declaring that she had finally won the struggle with Leah: she was the one who was now pleasing Jacob.

 

After the death of Moses, the Israelites were led into the Promised Land by Joshua, who divided the territory among the 12 tribes. The portion assigned to the tribe of Dan was a region west of Jerusalem. At least part of the tribe later moved to the extreme northeast and took the city of Laish, renaming it Dan.                                                                                                                      As the northernmost Israelite city it became a point of reference in the familiar phrase “from Dan to Beersheba.” (From North to South)                                                                                                Dan was one of the 10 northern tribes that disappeared from history after the Assyrian conquest of the kingdom of Israel in 721 BC. They are known are as the Ten Lost Tribes of Israel.
