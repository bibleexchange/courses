Cain & Abel

“Do that which is right and you can be content and not be jealous.”

 

Jealousy  Anger

 

Ge 4:1

And Adam knew Eve his wife; and she conceived, and bare Cain, and said, I have gotten a man from the LORD.

 2 And she again bare his brother Abel. And Abel was a keeper of sheep, but Cain was a tiller of the ground.

 

CAIN WAS  A FARMER

 3 ¶ And in process of time it came to pass, that Cain brought of the fruit of the ground an offering unto the LORD.

 

ABEL WAS A SHEPHERD

 4 And Abel, he also brought of the firstlings of his flock and of the fat thereof.

 

GOD SAID THE OFFERING MUST BE A LAMB

And the LORD had respect unto Abel and to his offering:

 5 But unto Cain and to his offering he had not respect.

 

CAIN GOT MAD AT GOD

And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.

 

WHY WAS HE MAD?

 6 ¶ And the LORD said unto Cain, Why art thou wroth? and why is thy countenance fallen?

 

DID HE HAVE A GOOD REASON TO BE MAD?

a) 7 If thou doest well, shalt thou not be accepted?

b) and if thou doest not well, sin lieth at the door.

 

 

And unto thee shall be his desire, and thou shalt rule over him.

 

Cain Disobeyed—Got Mad at God – Envied His brother’s blessing – Hated His brother – Got Angry

 8 ¶ And Cain talked with Abel his brother: and it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him.
