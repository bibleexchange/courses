Homiletics II

January 25, 2005

Peter’s sermon outline on the Day of Pentecost:

 

TITLE: “Jesus: Lord and Christ/Messiah”

TEXTS: Joel 2:28-32; Psalm 16:8-11

 

  1. Declared to be by the Day of Pentecost
    1. Joel prophesied of this Day.
    2. The Day was connected with men calling on the name of the Lord and being saved. (3,000 souls were saved in the name of Jesus that Day)

 

  1. Declared to be by Miracles and Wonders (Approved of God)
    1. Jesus of Nazareth, a man, performed Miracles and Wonders.
    2. The Miracles and Wonders were performed by God by Him.

 

  1. Declared to be by His Crucifixion
    1. He was delivered by the determinate counsel and foreknowledge of God.

(It was God’s purpose and plan that the Messiah would die.)

    1. Wicked hands crucified Him.

(You did not recognize Who He was, but wickedly crucified Him.)

 

  1. Declared to be by His Resurrection
    1. God raised Him up.
    2. Because of Who He was death could not hold Him.

 

  1. Declared to be by His Genealogy
    1. It was prophesied the Messiah would come in the lineage of David.
    2. Jesus was born in the lineage of David.

 

  1. Declared to be by His Sitting on the Father’s Right Hand

(Peter clarifies that David’s Prophecy did not speak of David but of the Messiah.)

    1. David is qualified as a verifiable prophet of God.
      1. David is a Patriarch.
      2. His prophecy of the Resurrection was Divinely inspired.
    2. Jesus fulfilled the prophecies of David.
