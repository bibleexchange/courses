Prophecy

May 3, 2006

Prophecy

Study Questions

 

1. What was Nebuchadnezzar’s decree concerning the image that he had made?

When the music of all kinds of instruments was played all were to bow down and worship the image and al that didn’t were to be cast into a fiery furnace.

 

2. Why did he make the image?

  1. The image he saw in dream was of him.
  2. The downfall of his kingdom had been foretold and he feared this.

 

3. What does this image set forth?

The power and glory of man

 

4. What number did this image bear?

6 which is the number of man.

 

5. What was the sign that was given for the people to worship?

When the band played.

 

6. How different kinds of instruments were used?

6 different kinds were used.

 

7. Why did the three Hebrew’s defy the king’s decree?

Because the commandment of the Lord was to not bow down to any image. Ex. 20:4,5

 

8. What is the principle of faith?

Knowledge is the principle of faith.

 

9. What is the persistence of faith?

Courage is the persistence of faith.

 

10. What is the pledge of faith?

Dedication is the pledge of faith.

 

11. What happened to the king when the three Hebrews gave their answer?

He lost his temper.

 

12. What was done to the furnace in regards to their answer?

It was heated seven times hotter than it was want to be heated.

 

13. What happened to the men that cast the three Hebrews into the furnace?

They were killed by the heat of the furnace.

14. Tell something about their deliverance from the furnace of fire?

It was Jesus that brought their deliverance and they came out without even the smell of smoke.

 

15. What was the king’s confession?

Recognized: God, faith, & real religion.

 

16. What was the king’s command?

It concerned all people and it concerned speech & severe punishment.

 

17. As a parable of the present what does this lesson teach?

Man is a religious being and even in a totalitarian state religion must be incorporated into it.

 

18. As a prophecy of the future what does it teach?

There will be an anti-christ who will demand worship of an image with a threat of death.

 

19. Tell a little about the second dream that Nebuchadnezzar had?

He saw a great tree. A watcher cried to hew it down, but leave the stump seven years for the intent that he might know God rules.

 

20. Tell what the interpretation was about?

It revealed God’s judgment upon Nebuchadnezzar who would graze like cattle and live in the field like a beast.

 

21. For how long was the judgment to last?

It was to last seven years.

 

22. Describe what happened to Nebuchadnezzar’s heart and the condition of his body.

His heart was full of pride. He was driven away from men, ate grass as oxen, body was wet with dew, his hair grew like eagle’s feathers, and his nails like bird’s claws.

 

23. What was the reason for the judgment?

Because of his sins and pride of his heart. It was a warning and an occasion for repentance.

 

24. How long was it before the judgment came after the dream was interpreted?

Twelve months later.

 

25. What were the two things that brought about the king’s restoration?

Repentance and Regeneration brought about his restoration.
