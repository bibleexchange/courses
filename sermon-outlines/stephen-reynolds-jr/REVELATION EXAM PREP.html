REVELATION EXAM PREP

 

Study Questions #1

 

  1. Who was this a revelation of?

It is a revelation of the Lord Jesus Christ.

  1. Who gave Him this revelation?

God gave this revelation.

  1. What is the substance of this revelation?

The essential substance of this book is that is is prophecy.

  1. Why is it a key to this revelation?

Prophecy is a key because this revelation is connected with the future and not the past.

  1. In what kind of language is much of this revelation written?

Much of it is written in symbolic (figurative and typical) language.

  1. Who is the scribe of this revelation?

John, the Beloved, the Apostle, is the scribe.

  1. What is the value of this revelation?

It is REWARDING and ENLIGHTENING.

  1. What are the sources of the salutation?

The Father, Son, and Holy Ghost.

  1. What are the subjects of this salutation?

1. The Churches, 2. Their Location, 3. Their Need

  1. What was the substance of this salutation?

1. Grace 2. Peace 3. Consolation 4. Confirmation

  1. What were the circumstances of this saluation?

Common: 1. Brotherhood 2. Suffering 3. Royalty 4. Promise 5. Place

  1. How did John receive the vision?

By the Spirit on the Lord’s day.

  1. What was he commanded to do with the vision?

He was commanded to write it in a book and share it.

  1. How was the vision announced?

By a great voice, as of a trumpet.

  1. What was the first scene of the vision?

The Seven Golden Candlesticks – The Church

  1. What was the second scene of the vision?

One Like unto the Son of Man – The Glorified Christ

  1. What was the third scene of the vision?

Seven Stars – The Lord’s Ministers

  1. Where does the Lord hold His ministers?

He holds them in His right hand.

 

 

 

 

 

Study Questions #2

 

  1. To whom were the seven letters addressed?

To the angels/ministers of the seven churches of Asia.

  1. Name the seven churches.

 

Ephesus

Smyrna

Pergamos

Thyatira

Sardis

Philadelphia

Laodicea

  1. What do these churches have to do with us?

Each church is representative of each church age; We are now in the Laodicean Church age. Within each church age is an element of the other ages.

  1. What do the letters reveal in regard to the ministry?

The Responsibility, Distinction, and Authority of the ministry.

  1. What is the number seven a type of?

Completeness, whole (7 church ages represent the whole church age.)

  1. How many out of the seven churches did Christ find serious fault?

Five out of the seven.

  1. What was the complaint against the church at Ephesus?

They left their first love.

  1. What was the key to the Smyrnian Age?

Persecution and tribulation was the key.

  1. What was the complaint against the Church of Pergamos?

Evil deeds and evil doctrine. Followed after the doctrine of Balaam and the Nicolaitans.

  1. What was the complaint against the Church of Thyatira?

Wicked seducers allowed to teach and seduce God’s servants.

  1. What was the complaint against the Church of Sardis?

Having a name that they were alive but were dead.

  1. What was the complaint against Philadelphia?

Hypocrisy and Falsehood.

  1. What was the complaint against the Church of Laodicea?

They were lukewarm.

  1. What was the counsel to the church of the Laodiceans?

Buy of Me gold tried in the fire, white raiment that thou mayest be clothed, and anoint thine eyes with eye salve that thou mayest see.

  1. Name the seven rewards to the Overcomers.
    1. Eat of the tree of Life and shall not be hurt of the second death
    2. Eat of the Hidden Mannah, a white stone, on it a new name
    3. Given power over the nations.
    4. Given White raiment, name not blotted out
    5. Made a pillar in the temple on it written God’s name, name of the city of God, and My new name.
    6. Made to sit with Me on My throne
    7. Inherit all things, I will be His God, He shall be My son

 

 

QUIZ #3

 

  1. What did John hear when the door was opened in heaven?

A voice as it were a trumpet.

  1. What did John see when the door was opened?

A throne and One sat on the throne.

  1. What was it that encircled the throne?

There was a rainbow round about the throne in sight like an emerald.

  1. What type of throne was it?

A throne of judgment.

  1. Who are the Twenty-Four Elders?

The Bride of Christ/Overcomers

  1. Who are the Four Living Creatures?

The Body of Christ/Church

  1. What do the Four Living Creatures give to Him that sat on the throne?

Give glory and honor and thanks unto Him that sat on the throne.

  1. What do the Four Elders do?

Fall down and worship Him, and cast down their crowns before the throne.

  1. Who was found worthy to take the book and to open the seals thereof?

The Lamb of God, Lion of Judah, Jesus Christ

  1. What was the number of angels that gave honor to the Lamb?

10,000 times 10,000 and thousands of thousands

  1. What do the four horses represent?

The judgments of God.

  1. What were the colors of the four horses?

Black Red White Pale

  1. Tell what these colors signify.

 

Black – dearth, famine

Red – blood, war

White – Righteous Judgment

Pale - Death

  1. Tell what happened under the fifth seal.

Souls under the altar slain for the Word of God.

  1. Tell what happened under the sixth seal.

Five Fearful Physical Events: 1. Great Earthquake 2. Sun Blackened

3. Moon to Blood 4. Stars Fall to Earth 5. Earth Effected by Stars

  1. Who are the 144,000 that were sealed?

The twelve tribes of Israel.

  1. How large was the multitude that was clothed with white robes?

It was an innumerable number of people.

  1. What did they have in their hands?

palms

  1. Where did they come out from?

Out of great tribulation.

  1. Tell what happened under the seventh seal.

A half hour of silence in Heaven and seven angels given seven trumpets.
