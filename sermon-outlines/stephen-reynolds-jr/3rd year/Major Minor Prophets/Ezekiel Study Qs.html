Major & Minor Prophets

May 18, 2006

Ezekiel

Study Questions

 

 

1. When did Ezekiel first receive the vision of the glory of the Lord?

On the 5th day of the 4th month of the 5th year of the captivity.

At the beginning of his ministry.

 

2. What is meant by “expressly” in 1:3?

“It came expressly, or accurately, to him; he did himself clearly understand what he said and was abundantly satisfied of the truth of it.”—Matthew Henry

 

3. How much time elapsed from 1:1,2 and 8:1?

One year and two months.

 

4. Describe the likeness of God’s throne that Ezekiel saw in vision?

Ezekiel 1:26. Above the firmament which was above the living creatures was the likeness of a throne. It had the appearance of a sapphire stone. Adam Clarke describes sapphire in this way, “The pure oriental sapphire, a large well cut specimen of which is now before me, is one of the most beautiful and resplendent blues that can be conceived.” Upon the throne itself was the likeness as the appearance of a man

 

5. Where is the glory of the Lord in 10:4?

It is in the House of God, the Temple.

 

6. Where is the glory of the Lord in 10:18,19?

It departs from the House of God and remains over above the Cherubim, the wheels, and every one that stood at the door of the east gate.

 

7. Where is the glory of the Lord in 11:22,23?

The Glory of the Lord went up from the midst of the city, and stood upon the mountain which is on the east side of the city; remaining yet above the Cherubim and the wheels with them.

 

8. Where is the glory  in Isa. 6:3?

The whole earth is full of His glory.

 

9. Describe with a short essay, the dramatized prophecy in 12:1-16.

Ezekiel brought forth his stuff by day, as stuff for captivity, and in the even he dug through the wall with his hand; He brought it forth in the twilight, and he bare it upon his shoulder in their sight. Ezekiel is a sign here to Israel that they will remove and go into captivity.

10. Who is the tender one in 17:22?

Jesus Christ, the Messiah.

 

11. Write out the 7 individual heathen nations and why they were judged.

1) Ammon 25:3 Profanity Against God’s House & People

2) Moab 25:8 Profanity Against God’s Judah

3) Edom 25:12 Offended and Revenged Against Judah

4) Philistia 25:15 Dealt with Judah in Revenge of Old Hatred

5) Tyre 26:2 said the fall of Jerusalem would be an advantage to her, Self-serving Business

6) Zidon 28:24 Were a Pricking Brier and Grieving Thorn to Israel

7) Egypt 29:3 Pride and Profanity

 

12. How long is a measuring reed?  40:5

6 cubits and a hand breadth (about 10 feet, six inches)

 

13. Comment on Eze. 47:9 and 37:10.

God is righteous and just and so the judgment, but God is also full of mercy.  He breathes life and flows as a river bringing healing. We see His mercy and his providence.
