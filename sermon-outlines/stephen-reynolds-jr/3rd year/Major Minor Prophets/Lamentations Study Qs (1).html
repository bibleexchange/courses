Major & Minor Prophets

May 19, 2006

Lamentations

Study Questions

 

1. What is the key verse in Lamentations?

Lamentations 1:12 “Is it nothing to you, all ye that pass by?”

 

2. What does the weeping widow mean?

The weeping widow means severe affliction.

 

3. What sign is given to show the extent of the affliction?

A cloud.

 

4. When should we pray at first?

Cry out in the night, in the beginning of the watches, pour out thine heart. 2:19

 

5. How can we find vision, perfection and joy?

  1. All Strongholds must be destroyed
  2. Crying unto the Lord and pouring out our heart like water before Him.

 

6. Why is it that the Lord does not pity Israel?

Because of their rebellion.

 

7. Who does the weeping prophet mainly mourn before?

He mourns before the Great Judge.

 

8. Describe Zion’s pitiful estate.

1) The gold is become dim—gold (with emphasis on its brightness; shimmer) is become dim.

2) The most fine gold changed – gold (ore as it is when it is originally mined) has been altered or changed

3) The stones of the sanctuary are poured out in the top of every street.

It is dismantled, destroyed, demolished.

 

9. How can one turn to God?

If the Lord would turn a man then he can be turned to God. We cannot come unless He first draws us. (John 6:44)

 

10. What should be the condition of our hearts?

We need to have a joyful heart and our whole heart given to God.

 

11. What must we recall every evening before we face another night?

(Remember, don’t wait till morning.) Lamentations 3:22,23 His mercies are new every morning and great is His faithfulness!

If you are with Him in the beginning, let Him keep you till the end.
