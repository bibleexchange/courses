Major & Minor Prophets

May 12, 2006

Micah

Study Questions

 

 

1. What does Micah’s name mean?

Who is like Jehovah?

2. Who did he prophesy to?

Micah prophesied to Judah and Israel.

3. What were the three kings’ names during this time?

Jotham, Ahaz and Hezekiah, kings of Judah

4. Give the key word.

Hear (Dr. A.T. Pierson says, Controversy)

5. Give the key thought.

Who is a God like unto thee. Micah 7:18

6. What are the four words that outline this prophecy?

A. Witness, B. Comfort, C. Pleading, D. Pardon

7. Name the instruments of judgment that God uses.

Mountains molten, Valleys as wax, Fire, A Steep Place, Babylon, Assyrians

8. Note the objects of His judgments.

The head cities of the two kingdoms: Samaria and Jerusalem.

9. What catches your attention in the prophecy of a funeral of the ruined

kingdom?  1:8-16

The opening statement is an attention getter, “I will wail and howl, I will go stripped and naked.”

10. Explain the two extreme measures of humility.

The prophet wailing with his coat off giving the appearance of one that has been attacked and robbed and the attack is not over.

11. Contrast the true prophet with the false prophets.

 

True Prophet

Divine Power and Authority

in his Message

Divine Origin of his message

Divine Content in his message

Divine Ability in His ministry

False Prophet

Perverted Justice

Building by violence and extortion

Working for only personal gain

12. Micah prophesied of whose birth?

Of the Messiah’s birth, the Christ.

13. Why did God send the rod of correction?

6:9 He chastises that they might repent.

14. God remembered His covenant with what two men?

7:20 Jacob and Abraham

15. List God’s three main requirements found in Micah 6:8.

1)Do Justly, 2)Love Mercy, 3)Walk Humbly with thy God

16. Who is a God like unto the Lord our God?

There is none like Him.
