Major & Minor Prophets

May 18, 2006

Study Questions

Zephaniah

 

1. Read the book of Zephaniah, make a comment on the overall view of this prophecy.

It contains the elements of: Judgment is Eminent and Near, but not anticipated, but only peace is expected and men relax. The majority will face great judgment, but God will still have a remnant that He promises to “HIDE IN the day of the Lord’s Wrath.” Since, the prophecy is addressed to the Hebrew people, I believe interpretation of it must be made in light of that fact. As far as I understand, the prophecy reaches still yet into the future, into the last three and a half years of the Tribulation hour where God deals with Israel again as a nation. The remnant, I suggest, could be the 144,000 preserved IN judgment and not actually taken out of it. 3:8 sounds like the Battle of Armageddon to me.

 

2. What is the Key Word?

Jealousy 1:18; 3:8

 

3. Define:

Baal –lord; master; an idol of the Phoenicians; sun god for the Canaanites; often found in the plural Baalim

Chemarims –idolatrous priests

Malcham –a king; royal

 

4. God will have _all_  _our_  _heart’s_ devotions.

 

5. What are the words that begin the prophecy? (1:2)

I will utterly consume all things from off the land, saith the Lord.

 

6. a. Give the 5 steps of the last call to repentance.

  1. Keep Silent, Hold thy Peace
  2. Seek Ye the Lord
  3. Wait Ye upon the Lord
  4. A Pure Language…to serve Him with one consent
  5. With all your heart

 

b. Define the word “consent”

from 7925; the neck (between the shoulders) as the place of burdens:--back, X consent, portion, shoulder.

 

c. Write your consent to the will of God.

I will do Thy will Oh God and not my own.

7. Give Key Phrases with a reference for each.

The Day of the Lord 1:7,8,14,18; 2:2,3

The Day of Wrath, the dreadful day 1:14-18

In the midst 3:5,11,12,15,17

 

8. Why does the judgment of God fall on the rich?

Not because they have things, but because their things have them.

 

9. Is the day of the Lord’s wrath kindled against Israel completely?

1:18—It is kindled in the whole land of Israel upon the wicked.   &

3:13—He will have a remnant.

 

10. How is Israel to sing?

With all the heart and with rejoicing.

 

11. What is the cause of judgment?

The immoral state/condition is the cause of judgment. 1:5,6,17; 3:1-7

 

12. Sing till you feel God’s presence in a special way, then tell Him you love Him, praise Him for awhile, ask for His imputed righteousness to be imparted to your walk, seek to be merciful as His is merciful, then explain on this paper the results of your communing with the Lord.

The nearest I need can describe it is ASSURANCE. I know these things don’t happen in an instant and they are an impossible task for me to do on my own, but when in His presence I sense an assurance that He is able and near. His nearness is most comforting because that tells me He cares that I make it and it isn’t just me begging God for something he could care less one way or the other about. I sense I walk not alone, but with my hand in His.
