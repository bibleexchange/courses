Habakkuk

Study Questions

 

1. Define: Habakkuk, 1:1 – embrace, to clasp, to cling or fold the hands

 

Burden, 1:1 – tribute, porterage, an utterance, chiefly a doom, burden, carry away, prophecy

Faith, 2:4 – firmness, security, morally fidelity, stability, steady, truth

Rejoice, 3:18 – to jump for joy, exult, triumph

Joy, 3:18 – to spin around (under the influence of any violent emotion); usually rejoice

 

2. What was Habakkuk’s first complaint in 1:2-4?

His complaint was against the Lord’s silence: O LORD, how long shall I cry, and thou wilt not hear! even cry out unto thee of violence, and thou wilt not save!

 

3. What was God’s reply?  1:5-11

He would raise up the Chaldeans to bring His people to captivity.

 

4. What was Habakkuk’s second complaint in 1:13, 14-15, 16-17

That God would look so favorably on this heathen nation allowing them to continually slaw at the expense of even His own people, a more righteous sort.

 

5. What was God’s reply in 2:4?

Those that are filled with pride and self-righteousness is not upright, but the just shall live by faith.

 

6. What did God tell him to do with the answer? 2:2

Write the vision, and make it plain upon tables, that he may run that readeth it.

 

7. Give a verse in Habakkuk that proves the righteous shall triumph.

Habakkuk 3:19 The LORD God is my strength, and he will make my feet like hinds' feet, and he will make me to walk upon mine high places. To the chief singer on my stringed instruments.

 

8. Draw the picture on paper of what the prophet is relaying in 3:17-19.
