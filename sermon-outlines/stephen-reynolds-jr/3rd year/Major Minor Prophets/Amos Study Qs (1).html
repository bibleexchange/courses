#11 Essay on Israel’s restoration 9:11-15.

 

  1. The Need of Restoration

The Tabernacle of David is fallen. The place of worship and the place designated for the Shekinah Glory of God to come and meet with men is in disarray with many breaches or gaps. It is in ruins.

 

  1. The Time of Restoration

v. 11 “In that day…” Following the time of Jacob’s troubles is a time promised when God will restore Israel. It will be in a time following God’s judgment upon the sinners of His people. It may seem to Israel the day may never come, but it will come. v. 13 “The Days come…” v.14 “And I will bring again the captivity of my people Israel.”

 

  1. The Character of Restoration

The Tabernacle of David will be restored. All the gaps and breaches closed up. It will be built as in the days of old. The restoration will be of great blessing and abundance. To the point that the seasons following in natural order will overlap each other for the abundance of produce with the gather right on the heels of the one sowing seed. The people will be restored to their land and they shall build again the waste cities and inhabit them. They shall plant vineyards, drink wine, make gardens, and eat the fruit of them. The Lord will plant them upon their land they shall no more be pulled up out of their land which He has given them.
