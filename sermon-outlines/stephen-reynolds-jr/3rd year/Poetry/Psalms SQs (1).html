1. Psalms were songs of Trust, comprised of 150 poems set to music for worship.
  2. There are four attributes which the Psalms exalt:
    1. Wisdom, majesty, & power
    2. Mercy
    3. Infinite Knowledge
    4. Creative Power

 

  1. The psalms that speak of Jesus Christ are called Messianic Psalms.
  2. In Psalm 22, Jesus is referred to as the Good Shepherd.
  3. In Psalm 23, Jesus is referred to as the Great Shepherd.
  4. In Psalm 24, Jesus is referred to as the Chief Shepherd.
  5. There are nine classified sections of the Psalms:
    1.  
    2. Instruction
    3. Praise
    4. Thanksgiving
    5. Penitance
    6. Trust
    7. Distress
    8. Aspiration
    9. History
    10. Prophecy

 

 

  1. There are six leading ideas of the book of Psalms:
    1.  
    2. Trust
    3. Praise
    4. Rejoicing
    5. Loving kindness
    6. The Wicked
    7. Enemies

  1. The key verse of the book of Psalms is Psalm 29:2:

Give unto the LORD the glory due unto his name; worship the LORD in the beauty of holiness.
