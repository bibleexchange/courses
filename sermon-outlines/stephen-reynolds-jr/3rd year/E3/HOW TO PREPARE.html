HOW TO PREPARE

 

  1. Setting Your Objective
    1. Choose a topic
    2. Choose a General Objective

To inform/teach/train

To stimulate/motivate/inspire

To persuade/convince/sell

To explore/debate/negotiate/

To amuse/entertain

    1. Determine your Specific Purpose

Narrow to one central specific theme.

Never leave off prayer and seeking direction from the Lord no matter what the venue.

    1. Determine your Desired Responses

 

Your objective must answer the questions:

Why am I speaking and what do I want the outcome to be?

 

  1. Researching Your Audience
  1. Ask Yourself
  1. Who are they? To whom am I speaking?
  2. Why are they there?
  3. What do they expect?
  4. What do they want or need?
  1. Ask Your Contact
  • Who?
  • Where?
  • When?
  • What?
  • How?
  • Duration?
  • Other Speakers?

 

  1. A Map of Ideas

Make one

    1. Draw circle and write subject in it
    2. Jot down any ideas on lines radiating from it
    3. Build over a period of at least two different settings

 

  1. Selection
  1. Look at your objective and Audience Profile
    1. Will it help you achieve your objective?
    2. Is it relevant to your audience?
  1. What are your audience’s needs?
  2. Be very selective.
