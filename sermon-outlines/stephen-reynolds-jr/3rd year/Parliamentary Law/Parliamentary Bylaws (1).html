House of Bread

 

Church Bylaws

 

An official and legal document of House of Bread

designed in the fear of God in

accordance with the laws of the United States.

 

MISSION

 

To be a church that proves and declares the pure Word of God through the power of the Holy Ghost, which alone is effectual to the saving of the lost, the comfort of the hurting, the healing of the sick, and the perfecting of the saints.

 

Psalm 107:20; Isaiah 61:1; Ephesians 4:15; Colossians 1:25; 2 Timothy 3:16,17; 4:2

 

FOUNDATION

 

The Word of God will be our only light and the Holy Ghost our only power and the foundation upon which we function which cannot be substituted with any other foundation in order to change for culture, fads, or the personal preference of any person or group of people because the Old time Gospel which the apostles preached, the reformers died for, and the men of revival found to be sure and steadfast is eternally relevant and applicable to any people and any age.

 

Psalm119:89, 105; Matthew 24:35; Mark 2:2; Hebrews 13:9

 

GOVERNMENT

 

The pastor will be spiritual and practical head of House of Bread. Every person in a position of authority or membership must conduct church business in complete submission to the guidance and will of the pastor. The only exception to this is if the pastor is found to be unfit to continue in his role as pastor. The rule by which this fact is established is prescribed under “PASTORSHIP.”

 

The positions of leadership under the pastor will include Assistant Pastor, 5 Trustees appointed by the pastor, Secretary, Sunday School Superintendent and Teachers, and any other positions delegated by the pastor. Each church leader is bound to the standards of House of Bread which are prescribed in this document under the heading “Church Standards” and must live free of the actions mentioned in paragraph “PASTORSHIP” and labeled a), b), c), and d). The purpose, authority, and rules regarding each individual in these positions are defined in this document.

 

 

 

PASTORSHIP

 

The pastor is bound to the “CHURCH STANDARDS.” The pastor of House of Bread is Stephen Gordon Reynolds, Jr. by Divine appointment. Pastor is a lifetime position. There will be no confirmation votes or votes concerning discharge of the pastor taken unless 1) the pastor wills it, or 2) The pastor is openly and publicly found to be involved in at least one of the following:

  1. Has been convicted of a felony by the United States court system.
  2. Has married after he has been divorced.
  3. Has been openly and publicly accused of with evidence substantial enough to cast suspicion in the minds of a minimum of four of the trustees of sexual harassment, physical abuse, inappropriate interaction with children, or adultery.
  4. Has partaken during his time as pastor in smoking cigarettes or cigars, drinking any alcoholic beverage, or using any non-doctor-prescribed drug with the exclusion of caffeine.

The pastor possesses authority to delegate to whomever he desires any responsibilities and or privileges. The pastor may veto or change decisions made by other church leaders.

 

TRUSTEES

 

The sole purpose of the trustee is to provide accountability for the pastor. The pastor will appoint five trustees. Appointment and reappointment are made by the pastor and made official solely by the pastor’ public announcement before the church body the next Sunday morning following the pastor’s selection. One individual among the five will be designated by the pastor as “Head Trustee.” His authority and responsibility is defined in paragraph “Discipline – Of the Pastor.” Each will remain in their position as trustee without necessary reappointment for 3 years. The exception to this rule is if the individual is being publicly disciplined or dismissed by the pastor. The trustee is volunteer and will receive no church compensation for his service.

 

SUNDAY SCHOOL SUPERINTENDENT AND TEACHERS

 

Teachers will be expected to faithfully perform their duties or provide for their performance in unity with the superintendent. The superintendent must act in agreement with and in a spirit of unity with the church bylaws and the convictions and preferences of the pastor. These positions are volunteer and will receive no church compensation for their service.

 

SECRETARY

 

The secretary is in charge of church records. It is his responsibility to keep record of financial flow, changes to these bylaws, and official church actions and transactions.

 

 

 

MEMBERSHIP AND NON-MEMBERSHIP

 

Until such a time as there is instituted a church board there will be no official membership. When an official board is instituted it will be one of their first responsibilities to design a system of membership under the guidance and authority of the pastor.

 

CHURCH SERVICE ORDER

 

Regular meetings will be held weakly: Every Sunday there will be Sunday school at 10 a.m., a morning service at 11 a.m., and an evening service at 6:30 p.m. Every Wednesday there will be a service at 7 p.m. Other meetings and special services may be instituted at any time by the pastor. Church business (i.e., a request to see church records made during a testimony time is out of order) may not be conducted during these weekly service times unless initiated by the pastor.

 

DISCIPLINE

 

Of the Pastor

 

Any complaints or accusations concerning the conduct of the pastor will be presented in a private meeting attended wholly and solely by all five of the trustees, the pastor, and individual (optional: the presence of their spouse or legal guardian) with the complaint or accusation.

 

If the pastor is accused of any of the acts listed in the paragraph “PASTORSHIP” labeled a), b), c), and d) in the proper order and manner prescribed in the previous sentence, It is the responsibility of the Head Trustee to privately meet with the pastor and discuss the accusation. If the Head Trustee is absolutely, without reserve, standing in the fear of the terrible and great God convinced of the pastor’s innocence then the accusation must die and any further action concerning the situation will be prescribed by the pastor. If the Head Trustee is unsatisfied then a private meeting must be called for and attended by all five trustees to discuss with the pastor and only any other individuals the pastor desires to be present the accusation at hand. If the five trustees are convinced of the pastor’s innocence then the accusation must die and any further action concerning the situation will be prescribed by the pastor. If the five trustees are unsatisfied then a meeting must be called for inviting the entire membership to discuss the accusation. The making of this announcement for a will be announced publicly, inviting exclusively church members, any others the pastor desires present, the original accusers if they are not members, and is the responsibility of the Head Trustee. The meeting will be conducted and presided by the Head Trustee

 

 

 

 

Of Trustees

 

It is within the authority of the pastor alone to dismiss a trustee. The only time this liberty is not afforded the pastor is during a time the pastor is under Trustee scrutiny for improper conduct as prescribed in paragraph “PASTORSHIP.”

 

Of other Church Leaders

 

Any complaints or accusations concerning church leaders not including the pastor must be presented to the pastor in a private manner. It is within the authority of the pastor alone to dismiss or discipline a church leader. Such an action may or may not be made public knowledge according to the judgment of the pastor.

 

Of Members

 

A pastor and associate pastor may in joint decision revoke an individual’s membership if they have failed to consistently meet the criteria of membership. Such discipline must be announced to the five trustees and printed and filed as a church record with their signatures on the document which merely indicates they have been informed. It is left to the pastor’s judgment if this discipline is made public information or not.

 

TENETS OF FAITH

 

We Believe…

  1. In the plenary inspiration of the Scriptures.
  2. In the absolute trinity of the eternal God head.
  3. In the pre-existence, and deity of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  4. In the personality and deity of the Holy Spirit.
  5. In the vicarious atonement through the death and resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ.
  6. In complete salvation by grace through faith.
  7. In the life of holiness, without which no man shall see the Lord.
  8. In water baptism by immersion.
  9. In the observance of the Lord’s supper.
  10. In tithing, as God’s financial plan for the Church.
  11. In the Bible revelation of Divine Healing.
  12. In the personal Baptism of the Holy Spirit as received by the Apostles.
  13. In the gifts of the Holy Spirit.
  14. In the Church which is the body of Christ composed of Holy Ghost Baptized, Born-again Believers.
  15. In a five-fold ministry given by God to the Church for the perfecting of the saints.
  16. In the Bride of Christ which is composed Overcomers out of the Body of Christ.
  17. In the personal and imminent return of our Lord.
  18. In the everlasting punishment of the impenitent.
  19. In eternal life for all true Believers.
  20. In the millennial reign of Christ.
  21. Intentional abortion of a baby is murder.
  22. Homosexuality is a sinful lifestyle and a choice and not the way an individual is born.
  23. In sin having an absolute wage of spiritual death which must be atoned for by the application of the blood of Christ through faith and repentance which is a complete change of mind and direction (the sin must be stopped or there remains no other atonement and forgiveness.)

 

CHURCH STANDARDS

 

  1. The Word of God is to be revered and held as the absolute and primary standard by which all conduct is judged.

 

  1. The standards listed in this document are not exhaustive, but as the Word of God is preached and taught each individual is expected to make appropriate changes. The standards of House of Bread will include the essential convictions so defined by the pastor as he declares them from the pulpit.

 

  1. Women must not wear the apparel of men—absolutely no slacks—at the church or in the general public; men must not wear the apparel of women at any time or in any place under any circumstances.

 

  1. The laws of the United States of America must be kept unless they conflict with the laws of God

 

  1. No church leader may teach, preach, or propagate a message among the church body that is contrary to the church’s tenets of faith and standards.

 

  1. Partaking of Alcohol, non-doctor-prescribed drugs (excluding caffeine), cigarettes, cigars, or any other tobacco product is a sin, which condemns the soul.

 

  1. Those participating in leadership roles or ministry roles such as music ministry and testimony must live a life consistent with the tenets of faith and church standards. Otherwise, they may not be welcome on the church’s platform or perform in a leadership role.

 

CHURCH FUNCTIONS AND OUTREACH

It is within order and the mission of House of Bread to conduct a:

  • Sunday School
  • Street Meeting
  • Missions Support
  • Nursing Home Visitation
  • Aid and Support of Leadership and Membership
  • Ordination of Ministers
  • Any other functions approved by the pastor

FELLOWSHIP

 

House of Bread may not publicly unite with any cult or religious group in a setting of equality and brotherhood which deny the deity of Jesus Christ, that salvation is in God through Jesus Christ and in no other, or worship any other God than the God of the Holy Bible, of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob Who is the Creator of Heaven Earth Who sovereign over all and above Him is no other.

 

FINANCES

 

House of Bread will operate under the stipulations and classification of US 501-C status. A bank account will be opened with a bank approved by the pastor. This account will be setup having three validating signatures: 1) the pastor’s 2) the assistant pastor’s 3) the secretary. Up to the sum of $5,000 the pastor may spend of church funds without another person’s confirmation or approval. All church checks will require a minimum of two signatures one which must be the pastor’s signature. Church records are not public record. Any display of church records must be approved by the pastor. The pastor has liberty to make this decision upon the source of his own preference.

BYLAWS AND THEIR PLACE

 

There are six pages to this document. Each page must be signed by the pastor near the bottom in order for it to be official and legal. This document is legal and binding for all church leaders and members. Amendments and changes may be made by pastor consent and the consent of a minimum of three of the trustees. This document is valid and applicable as long as House of Bread (or whatever the name of this assembly is if it is changed) functions as a church. Amendments made to this document can under no circumstances include the omission of the prominence of the Word of God and preeminence even above this official and legal document of House of Bread.
