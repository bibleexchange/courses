Stephen G. Reynolds, Jr.

Homiletics III

April 5, 2006

Study Questions

 

1. What is an expository sermon?

It is a thorough illuminating or exposing of the theme of a text.

 

2. List three various kinds of expository sermons.

  1. Scripture Words
  2. Scripture Phrases
  3. Complete Passage

 

3. What are the rhetorical elements of the expository sermon?

Explanation – Argument – Illustration – Application

 

4. Explain the value of breaking up a passage clause by clause.

Breaking up a passage clause by clause will help in understanding the meaning by its division into “chewable” parts.

 

5. What are five arguments in favor of expository preaching?

1. It is the most natural way of enforcing Divine Truth.

2. More Scriptural precedents than any other mode.

3. Historical usage commends it.

4. Greatly benefits congregation.

5. It is a great incalculable service to the preacher.

 

6. How does expository preaching benefit the congregation?

  • Incites them to study Scripture.
  • Does justice to the mind of the Spirit.
  • Builds up a congregation in Divine Truth.

 

7. What are the qualifications for success in expository preaching?

    • Intelligent faith in the inspiration of the Bible
    • Power of selection
    • Logical Mind
    • Power to Preach
    • Good Study Habits

 

8. What is the supreme test of a sermon?

It is whether or not it communicates.

 

9. What is meant by showing the results rather than the process of the sermon?

Certain details are helpful & important to preparation, but may not be essential or even helpful for the pulpit.

 

10. How does asking questions help the congregation?

Questions provoke thought & often can regain lost attention.
