Genesis 6:6,7

And it repented the LORD that he had made man on the earth, and it grieved him at his heart.

 And the LORD said, I will destroy man whom I have created from the face of the earth; both man, and beast, and the creeping thing, and the fowls of the air; for it repenteth me that I have made them.

 

Question: What is meant by God saying “It repented the Lord?”

 

STRONG’S:

05162.  Mxn  nacham,  naw-kham'

a primitive root; properly, to sigh, i.e. breathe strongly; by implication, to be sorry, i.e. (in a favorable sense) to pity, console or (reflexively) rue; or (unfavorably) to avenge (oneself):--comfort (self), ease (one's self), repent(-er,-ing, self).

 

ANSWER:

It doesn’t mean God was caught off guard and that He didn’t know what would happen when He created all things. It doesn’t mean God changed his mind. It doesn’t mean that God was guilty of a wrong and therefore had to find forgiveness and seek restitution.

It does mean that God in His absolutely unchanging nature is Benevolent and kind to the obedient, and He always judges sin. Man changed in His position with God because of sin and therefore reaped the consequences of his sin. He is all the time love and all the wrath. Which “side” we see depends on how we respond to his dealings with us.
