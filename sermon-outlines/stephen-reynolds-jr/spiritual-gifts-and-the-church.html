The Ability of the Church

1 John 2:20 — 20 But ye have an unction from the Holy One, and ye know all things.
1 John 2:27 — But the anointing which ye have received of him abideth in you, and ye need not that any man teach you: but as the same anointing teacheth you of all things, and is truth, and is no lie, and even as it hath taught you, ye shall abide in him.

Does not mean to say that every individual believer possesses the full anointing, and in virtue of this knoweth all things. For if this were so, who would not despair of salvation, nor dare say: "I have, the faith"? Moreover, how could the statement, "Ye need not that any man teach you," be reconciled with the testimony of the same apostle, that the Holy Spirit qualifies teachers appointed by Jesus Himself? Not the individual believer, but the whole Church as a body possesses the full anointing of the Holy One and knows all things. The Church as a body needs not that any come to teach it from without; for it, possesses all the treasure of wisdom and knowledge, being united with the Head, who is the reflection of the glory of God, in whom dwelleth all wisdom.

Speaking in Tongues

The 3rd Sign of the Outpouring of the Holy Ghost

Acts 2:1-4 — And when the day of Pentecost was fully come, they were all with one accord in one place. 2 And suddenly there came a (1) sound from heaven as of a rushing mighty wind, and it filled all the house where they were sitting. 3 And there appeared unto them cloven tongues like as of (2) fire, and it sat upon each of them. 4 And they were all filled with the Holy Ghost, and began to speak with other (3) tongues, as the Spirit gave them utterance.

At Pentecost, they spoke a language above the influence of sin. At Jerusalem, only they understood it who were specially wrought upon by the Holy Spirit. The others understood it not. (Some heard in their native tongue and others mocked what they heard as the babblings of drunkards.
