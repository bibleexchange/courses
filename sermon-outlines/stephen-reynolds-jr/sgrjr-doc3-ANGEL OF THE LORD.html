WHO IS THE ‘ANGEL OF THE LORD’ IN THE OLD TESTAMENT?

 

OCCURENCES:

Gen 16:7 The angel of the LORD found Hagar in the wilderness.

Gen 22:15 The angel of the LORD called unto Abraham out of heaven.

Ex 14:19 The angel of God moved between Israel and Egyptians at the Red Sea.

Num 22:23 Balaam’s donkey saw the angel of the LORD standing in the way.

Judg 2:1 An angel of the LORD, said, I made you to go up out of Egypt.

Judg 13:3 The angel of the LORD told Manoah’s wife she would bare Samson

Isa 63:9 In all their affliction he was afflicted, and the angel of his presence saved them:

 

ANSWER:

In each of the passages above can be found evidence to identify the Angel of the Lord, but for brevity let’s look at Judges 13 with Manoah and his wife. The Angel of the LORD appears to them and tells them she will conceive a son and gives instruction concerning his upbringing. After the Angel of the Lord departs, Manoah requests that He return again and give further instructions. He appears to his wife in a field and she runs and gets Manoah. Manoah asks the angel of the LORD for His name. The answer Manoah gets is, “Why askest thou thus after my name, seeing it is secret?” The angel of the Lord disappears in the flames of the sacrifice they offered and appeared to them no more. Manoah declares, “We shall surely die, because we have seen God.” The Angel of the LORD is an Old Testament Theophany or appearance of Christ.
