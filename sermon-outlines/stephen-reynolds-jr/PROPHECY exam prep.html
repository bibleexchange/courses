PROPHECY

 

QUIZ #1

 

  1. What type of book is the Bible?

The Bible is prophetic.

  1. How much of the Bible is prophetic?

2/3 of Scripture is prophetic.

  1. In what way is the Bible prophetic?

It is prophetic in types, symbols, and direct statements.

  1. What does the Bible base its authenticity, authority, and inspiration on?

It bases these on prophecy.

  1. What is prophecy?

The telling of history in advance—before it happens.

  1. What is meant by prophecy being two-fold?
    1. It is Predictive – foresight – foretelling
    2. It is Descriptive – insight - forthtelling
  2. Is prophecy reasoned discourse?

No, it is the Divine utterance of the Holy Ghost.

  1. Why do we study prophecy?
    1. That we may know God’s plan.
    2. That we may understand the future.
    3. That we be not lead into error.
  2. What is the proof of prophecy?
    1. It is beyond human reasoning or foresight.
    2. It is given prior to fulfillment.
    3. It has a clear and detailed fulfillment.
  3. Give verbatim I Corinthians 10:32.

Give none offence, neither to the Jews, nor to the Gentiles, nor to the Church of God.

The future is known as God’s secret.

Yes, but only through the Bible and inspiration of the Holy Ghost and not apart from these.

  1. Is prophecy confined to certain books of the Bible?

No, prophecy is not confined to certain books of the Bible.

  1. What is the first recorded prophecy in the Bible?

Genesis 3:15 “And I will put enmity between thee and the woman, and between thy seed and her seed; it shall bruise thy head, and thou shalt bruise his heel.”

QUIZ #5

  1. Tell what you know about the truth of the book of Daniel.

Jesus refers to the book of Daniel.

  1. Who was a contemporary of Daniel?

Ezekiel.

  1. What is the book of Daniel all about?

Universal sovereignty of God.

  1. What was the time of the book of Daniel?

At the beginning of the Babylonian conquest.

  1. How do we account for the fact that Jeremiah places this captivity in the fourth year of Jehoiakim, and Daniel in the third?

Jeremiah wrote to the Jews who count partial years as a whole, and Daniel wrote to Gentiles who as rule counted the partial years precisely.

  1. Tell something of the form of the book.

The book is divided into two equal parts. The first six chapters deal with history and the last six deals with prophecy.

  1. Why is the book of Daniel different from the other prophetic books?

God is not speaking to his own people.

  1. Why is the book written in two different languages?

What concerns the Gentiles is written in Aramaic, and what concerns the Jews is written in Hebrew.

  1. What does Daniel mean, what was his name changed to and what was the meaning of this name?

Daniel: God is my Judge

Beltshazzar: Bell the protector of his life

  1. What does Hananiah mean, what was his name changed to and what was the meaning of this name?

Hananiah: Jehovah is Gracious

Shadrach: command of Aku

  1. What does Mishael mean, what was his name changed to and what was the meaning of this name?

Mishael: Who is like God?

Meshach: Who is like Aku?

  1. What does Azariah mean, what was his name changed to and what was the meaning of this name?

Azariah: Jehovah is my helper

Abednego: Servant of Nego.

  1. What was the test Daniel and his friends were put to?

For ten days ate not the king’s meat and wine, but pulse and water.

  1. What was the king’s demand?

To know the dream and interpretation

  1. What was the king’s decree?

It was angry, complete, and immediate/ all the wise men should be slain

  1. Would this have any effect on Daniel?

Yes, it included Daniel.

  1. What was Daniel’s reaction to it all?

Goes boldly to the king and requests for time. Poised and didn’t Panic.

  1. What did Daniel do about it?

He went to prayer.

  1. What was the dream Nebuchadnezzar had in detail?

There were two main features: 1) an image 2) a stone

Head of Gold, Breast and arms of silver, belly and thighs of brass, legs of iron, feet of part iron, part clay

QUIZ #6

 

  1. What was Nebuchadnezzar’s decree concerning the image that he had made?

When music played, all were to fall down and worship the image or be cast into the furnace of fire.

  1. Why did he make the image?

He feared the downfall of his kingdom that had been foretold and sought to strengthen his kingdom by it.

  1. What was the sign that was given for the people to worship?

When the band played.

  1. How many different kinds of instruments were used?

Six.

  1. Why did the three Hebrew’s defy the king’s decree?

It was God’s command not to bow down before any image. Ex. 20:4,5

  1. What is the principle of faith?

Knowledge is the principle of faith.

  1. What happened to the king when the three Hebrews gave their answer?

He lost his temper.

  1. What was done to the furnace in regards to their answer?

It was heated seven times hotter than it was want to be heated.

  1. Tell a little about the second dream that Nebuchadnezzar had?

He saw a great tree, and a watcher cried, “Hew it down, but leave the stump 7 years.”

  1. Tell what the interpretation was about.

It was a revelation of God’s judgment on Nebuchadnezzar who would be driven from among men as a beast.

  1. For how long was the judgment to last?

7 years.

  1. Describe what happened to Nebuchadnezzar’s heart and the condition of his body?

His heart was turned as a beast’s heart, body wet with dew, hair like eagle’s feathers, nails like bird’s claws, and grazed like an oxen.

  1. What was the reason for the judgment?

It was Nebuchadnezzar’s sin.

  1. How long was it before the judgment came after the dream was interpreted?

12 months later.

Repentance and Regeneration.
