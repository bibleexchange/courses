The Safari of The Ark of the Covenant

 

 

Ark Safari Site #1

 

The Lord gives commandments concerning the Ark on Mount Sinai to Moses. Numbers 19:1

 

Construction of the Ark:

  • Make an ark: 2 ½ cu. x 1 ½ cu. x 1 ½ cu. ( LxWxH) Ex 25:10; 37:1
  • Put the staves into the rings by the sides of the ark Ex 25:14,15
  • Put the mercy seat above upon the ark Ex 25:21; 26:34
  • Built by Bezaleel to whom God had given special ability. Ex 31:1-7; 37:1
  • Built out of materials given in a free-will offering. Ex 35:12,21

 

Contents of the Ark:

  • Two tables of stone upon which is the Law of God. Ex 25:16,21; 2 Chr. 5:10

 

Care and Place of the Ark:

  • To be situated behind a veil dividing the holy place and the most holy place. Ex 26:33
  • Was to be carried only by the staves put through the ark’s rings Ex 25:14; 37:5
  • To be Anointed with a holy anointing oil. Ex 30:26
  • Altar of incense was placed before the ark. Ex 40:5
  • To be placed in the charge of the Kohathites. Nu 3:31
  • When the camp moves, the High Priest should come and his sons and they shall take down the covering veil and cover the ark with it. Nu 4:5

 

Consequence  of the Ark:

  • God will meet with His people from above the mercy seat. Ex 25:22; 30:6
  • High Priest must not come “at all times” before the mercy seat or death. Lev 16:2
  • God will appear in a cloud upon the mercy seat. Lev 16:2
  • God smote Uzzah and others for touching the ark. 2 Samuel 6:7; I Samuel 6:19

 

Ark Safari Site #2

 

At the Foot of Mount Sinai

Exodus 40

 

  • Was placed in service first “on the first day of the first month.” Ex 40:1-3
  • Moses put the law of God in the ark, set the staves on ark, and put the mercy seat above the ark. Ex 40:20
  • Moses placed the ark into the tabernacle, set up the veil of the covering, and covered the ark as the Lord had commanded. Ex 40:21
  • The book of Leviticus takes place at the foot of Mount Sinai.
  • Moses goes into the completed tabernacle and God speaks as a voice from off the mercy seat which is upon the ark, from between the two cherubim. Nu 7:89
  • Israel departs from Mt. Sinai three day’s journey and the ark goes before them. Nu 10:33

Ark Safari Site #4

 

Kibrothhattaavah

Numbers 11:34

 

  • No sooner does the fire stop and the people began to lust for the food of Egypt. They spoke of flesh, fish, cucumbers, melons, leeks, onions, and garlic.
  • They complained that all they have is manna whose taste was like fresh oil.
  • When the manna fell with morning dew Moses heard the every man in the door of his weeping and he was displeased along with the Lord.
  • Moses cries unto God that the burden of the people upon him is too much.
  • Moses requests God to kill him.
  • God delegates some of the Spirit that was upon Moses to 70 elders to relieve Moses.
  • God promises to send flesh to the people for an entire month. So much that it would come out their nostrils and it would be loathsome to them. All this because they have despised the Lord and questioning his delivering them out of Egypt.
  • Eldad and Medad prophesied in the camp and not out unto the tabernacle and a young man ran and told Moses. Joshua the son of Nun requested Moses to forbid these men. Moses told Joshua not to be enviest of these men for Moses sake. “Would God that all the LORD’s people were prophets, and that the LORD would put His Spirit upon them!
  • God keeps His Word and sends flesh! Causing a wind to blew in from the sea quails and letting them fall by both sides of the camp as it were a day’ journey in both directions and 2 cubits (3 feet) high.
  • They gathered meat 2 days with those gathering the least gathering 10 homers (8 bushels). While the meat was yet in their teeth the Lord sent a plague among the people.
  • “Kibrothhattaavah” means graves of the longing.

Ark Safari Site #5

 

Hazeroth

Numbers 11:35

 

  • Miriam and Aaron speak against Moses because of his Ethiopian wife.
  • Moses is described in Numbers 12 as the meekest man on the earth.
  • The Lord personally sets Miriam and Aaron in their place. God establishes the fact that Moses has been appointed to a position above other prophets for God speaks to other prophets with dreams and visions, but to Moses He speaks mouth to mouth.
  • The Lord’s anger was kindled against them and Miriam became leprous. Moses prays for Miriam’s healing. The Lord commands her to be shut out of the camp 7 days and then to be received again.
  • “Hazeroth” means a yard (as inclosed by a fence); a hamlet (surrounded with walls); village

Ark Safari Site #26

Kirjathjearim, Gibeah

I Samuel 7:1,2

 

  • The men of Kirjathjearim came and fetched the Ark of the LORD and brought it to the house of Abinadab in the hill, and sanctified Eleazar his son to keep the Ark of the Lord.
  • Kirjathjearim means city of forests, or city of towns. Abinidab means father of generosity. Eleazar means God is helper.
  • Kirjathjearim is also known as Baale or Baalah.
  • I Samuel 14:18 mentions Saul calling for the ark to be brought to him Bethhaven, but evidently it is either returned to Kirjathjearim afterward or it is never actually brought to Bethhaven for we see David in 2 Samuel 6:2 bringing the ark out of Abinadab’s home.
  • The Ark abode here for 20 years.

 

 

Ark Safari Site #27

House of Obededom the Gittite

II Samuel 6:1-11

 

  • David gathers together a host of people to reclaim the ark at Baale of Judah. They placed the ark on a new cart and Uzzah and Ahio the sons of Abinadab drove the new cart.
  • When they came to Nachon’s threshing floor (or Chidon’s; 1 Chr. 13:9) Uzzah put forth his hand to the Aark and took hold of it when the oxen shook it. The Lord smote him and he died there by the ark. They named the place Perezuzzah which means the breach of Uzzah.
  • For fear that God was not pleased, David took the ark to the house of Obededom the Gittite where it remains for three months instead of bringing it to the city of David which is Jerusalem. Obededom was a Levite and probably from Gathrimmon a Levitical city. (Joshua 21:24,25; I Chronicles 15:24)

 

Ark Safari Site #28

Temporary Tabernacle at Jerusalem

II Samuel 6:17

 

  • When David hears that the house of Obededom is being blessed because of the ark, he decided to bring the ark to Jerusalem.
  • It was transported now on the shoulders of the priests, who had been carefully prepared for the work, and the procession was distinguished by extraordinary solemnities and demonstrations of joy. —Jamieson-Fausset-Brown
  • Here in Jerusalem David had pitched a temporary tabernacle for the ark. (The old tabernacle was probably still at Gibeon.)
  • 2 Samuel 15:24-29. David is fleeing from Absalom out of Jersusalem. The Levites begin to follow carrying the ark, but David commands them to return to the city Jerusalem.
  • In I Chronicles 14:1 a new palace is built for the king by Hiram king of Tyre, and in 17:1 David is convicted by the fact he lives in a house of cedar while the ark of God rests in under curtains. David desires to build a temple for the Lord, but God does not allow him to. David’s part will merely be in storing up supplies in preparation for the temple to be built.

 

 

Ark Safari Site #29

Solomon’s Temple at Jerusalem

I Kings 8:1-11

 

  • Solomon completed the temple which David could only dream about.
  • The ark was deposited in the oracle; that is, the most holy place, under the wings of the cherubim--not the Mosaic cherubim, which were firmly attached to the ark (Ex 37:7,8), but those made by Solomon, which were far larger and more expanded.
