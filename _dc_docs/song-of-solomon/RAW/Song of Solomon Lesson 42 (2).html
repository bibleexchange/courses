She desires to find Him now after she has been awful neglectful.

There are some things that we must be careful not to involve ourselves with.

In seeking the Lord there are some dangers we must be aware of that we might avoid them.

  1. Dangers the Bride must avoid

Don’t neglect the fact that God wants to do more (for you, in you and through you) and you end up sleeping again.

The Bride has been awakened and aroused she wants to do something about her condition.

“I opened to my beloved”   ---   We open the door by obeying the truth

 

“I opened to my beloved; but my beloved had withdrawn himself, and was gone”

 

There is a time when God speaks to us and we don’t respond it will be very costly to gain back that position that we had with Him.

 

Pr 1:24 Because I have called, and ye refused; I have stretched out my hand, and no man regarded;                                                                                                                                                         Pr 1:26 I also will laugh at your calamity; I will mock when your fear cometh;

By her own neglect she has brought herself to this condition.                                                                                                              She now realizes that she has been neglectful too long, you can put Him off too long.

While she slept He knocked, while she rested He called, He spoke to her and she did not listen, While He called She refused to respond, She didn’t do anything to respond to His calling and knocking.

When she finally awoke He had withdrawn Himself, He had waited a long time but she was too late.                         We must sense and realize that He will not always be there, calling and beckoning us to come.

There comes a time when He withdraws Himself. If we wait too long He will not be there.
