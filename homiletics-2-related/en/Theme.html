HOMILETICS II

 

THE THEME

 

  1. THE THEME IS THE SUBJECT OF THE DISCOURSE.

 

A. The subject upon which the preacher proposes to speak.

1. Chosen after prayerful consideration.

 

2. A proposal or promise made to the congregation.

 

a. Presented with plain direct application.

  1. Prove what is proposed.

 

B. The theme should be derived from the passage of Scripture

which the preacher has chosen for his text.

1. As a rule the text naturally suggests the theme.

(The text should not need to be flung on the rack and

tortured before the theme can be extracted from it.)

 

2. The theme should be evident in the text as one of

its ruling ideas.

 

3. During the delivery of the sermon frequently use the

words of the text.

 

a. Remember you are preaching the Word.

- II Tim. 4:2.

b. Your authority comes from the Word.

" It is foolish to be lavish in words and niggardly

in truth." - Spurgeon.

 

C. The theme should be expressed with the purpose the preacher

has in view.

1. Always have a purpose.

( If you aim at nothing, you will hit nothing.)

2. A clear purpose will insure only one theme in the sermon.

(There may be more than one in the text, but there should not be more than one in the sermon.)

  1. THE ADVANTAGES OF HAVING A THEME.

 

A. The theme insures arrangement in the discourse.

1. The theme is the sermon condensed.

 

2. The sermon is the theme unfolded.

 

3. The theme should be kept in mind when preparing

the sermon.

 

B. The theme promotes unity in the discourse.

1. Let the people know where you are going, so they can

follow you.

 

2. Let the theme be evident throughout the sermon.

(Would a late-comer be able to determine the

subject of the sermon.)

 

a. Arguments - Are you PROVING the theme?

b. Illustrations - Are you PAINTING the theme?

  1. Applications - Are you PERSUADING the theme?

 

3. The theme should remain the final and ruling impression

on the hearer's mind.

 

C. The theme helps to give clearness of thought to the discourse.

1. Clearly define the theme.

 

a. Don't aim at more than can be handled.

" He grasps at the stars and sticks in the mud."

- John Wesley.

b. Save something for next time.

 

2. Keep the theme within limitations.

a. Deal with one aspect of truth at a time.

b. Narrowly define the subject to be preached.
