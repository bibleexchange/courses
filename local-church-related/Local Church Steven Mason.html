I. Qualifications for an Elder

    1. Positive moral characteristics
      1. Blameless (I Tim. 3:2, Titus 1:6)
      2. Temperate (Titus 1:8, I Cor. 9:25)
      3. Sober (I Tim. 3:2, Titus 1:8)
      4. Good Behavior (I Tim. 3:2)
      5. Patient (I Tim. 3:3)
    2. Negative moral characteristics (Must not be)
      1. Not to be given to wine (I Tim. 3:3, Titus 1:7)
      2. Not to be a striker (I Tim. 3:3, Titus 1:7)
      3. Not to be greedy of money (I Tim. 3:3, Titus 1:7)
      4. Not to be a brawler (I Tim. 3:3)
      5. Not to be covetous (I Tim. 3:3)
      6. Not to be self-willed (Titus 1:7)

 

 

    1. Domestic qualifications
      1. The husband of one wife (I Tim. 3:2, Titus 1:6)
      2. Hospitable (I Tim. 3:2, Titus 1:8)
      3. Rule his house well (I Tim. 3:4-5, Titus 1:6)
      4. Lover of good men (Titus 1:8)
    2. Spiritual qualifications
      1. Able to teach (I Tim. 3:2)
      2. Ruler (I Tim. 3:5)
      3. Not a novice (I Tim. 3:6)
      4. Just (Titus 1:8)
      5. Holiness (Titus 1:8)
      6. Hold fast the faithful word (Titus 1:9)
      7. Exhort and Convince (Titus 1:9)
