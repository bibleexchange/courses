I was saved off the streets of Portland approximately ten years ago in September.  The Lord brought me out of deep sin and placed me in a Christian home on Munjoy Hill for about two years.  For about seven years, I was involved in a ministry that helped street people in Portland.  I left that ministry and moved to Windham.  While in Windham, I attended various churches while working in Portland.  I never felt totally comfortable in any of the churches and I began to search for God’s will in my life.  One day, while in a Christian bookstore in Scarborough, I saw a poster advertising the Deliverance Center’s spring convention.  I went to the meetings and when Pastor Reynolds asked if anyone had a desire to attend the school, I felt God call me to Bible school.  I started Bible school in Oct. 2003 and in about two weeks, God willing, I will graduate.  God has blessed me mightily in the years of school.  I know it was the hand of God that has kept me and helped during the three years.  I want to make myself available and obedient to do God’s will the rest of my life.

Steven Mason

 

 

In the middle of the spring of the year 2006, I was working on a metal building.  Two other workers and I were working on a metal staging approximately 20’ high.  I saw a wire sticking out of the wall and asked one of my fellow workers if it was a live wire.  He said it was and to be very careful.  About a couple of hours went by and by that time I had forgotten about the wire.  I was working just below it, putting a metal stud in place, when the top of the stud touched the wire producing a shower of sparks.  I asked one of the other workers why I wasn’t electrocuted and they pointed out that the bottom end of the stud had been resting on the metal beam, grounding it.  The electricity had gone through the stud and into the beam instead of into me.  I said, “Wow, Thank you Jesus for we could have been electrocuted, fallen off the staging and died.

Victor Rios
