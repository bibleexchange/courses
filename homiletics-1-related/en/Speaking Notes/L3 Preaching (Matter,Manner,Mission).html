CHRISTIAN PREACHING

 

Homiletics is the science or art of religious discourse, or preaching.  The science is the theoretical knowledge of how it should be done.  The art is the ability/skill to do it, which develops with doing/practice/application of the knowledge, or know-how.

  1. The Matter of Preaching

 

Webster: I went not to learn history, but to learn duty.  To hear the way to Heaven, not to Palestine.

This reveals three things: —

  1. The Limits of Christian preaching.
  1. It is the delivery of a message from God to man.
  2. It concerns itself with religion/Christian duty.
  3. It must be founded on the Scriptures/The Word of God.

 

  1. The Extent of Christian preaching.
  1. Be careful—Don’t attempt too much.  Don’t give them the whole load.  [Illustration of farmer and young preacher]
  2. Be clear—Have one compact thought.  Not a message from Genesis 1:1 to Revelation 22:21, with every possible application and illustration that can be appended to it.  
  3. Be conservative—Leave something for the next time.  You may not run out of sermon material, but you may very well run out listeners and good will.

  1. The Authority of Christian preaching.
  1. Conviction—you are delivering God’s Word.  (Firm/strong belief; the state of being convinced)
  2. Knowledge—you must know God’s Word.  (Know it from reading it; know it so you can quote it; know it from diligent, daily application.)
  3. Positiveness—God’s Word is positive; don’t be negative.
