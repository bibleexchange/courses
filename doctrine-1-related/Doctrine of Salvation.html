  1. The Nature of Salvation

 

We will be dealing with the subject: What Constitutes Salvation?; or, The “State of Grace.”

 

 

  1. Three Aspects of Salvation

Justification – Regeneration – Sanctification

 

  1. Justification –

A judicial term bringing into our minds a courtroom scene

 

 

  1. Regeneration –

Born into the family, bringing to our minds a household scene

 

 

  1. Sanctification

Connected with worship, bringing to our minds a temple scene

 

 

  • Justification – A saved man is one who has been set right with God;

 

  • Regeneration – And born into the divine family of God;

 

  • Sanctification – And is now dedicated to the service of God.

 

Being justified, he is made righteous.

Being regenerated, he is a child of God.

Being sanctified, he is a “saint.”

 

 

Do these blessings follow one another or are they simultaneous as to time?

 

  • Set right in relation to God’s law. – Romans 3:24
  • He has a new nature imparted. – John 3:6
  • Separated unto a new life and service. – Ephesians 2:10

 

Yet, the three experiences are simultaneous – separated for the purpose of understanding and study.

 

All three blessings were procured by the atoning death of Christ.  Also, all three blessings flow from our union with Christ.

 

2 Corinthians 5:21          Ephesians 1:7          2 Corinthians 5:17
