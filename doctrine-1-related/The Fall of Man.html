The Fall of Man

 

 

I. The Fact of the Fall

 

Genesis 3:1-6 reveals that the first man fell.  The steps in the fall were:

 

1. Listening to slanders about God.

“Hath God said,” Genesis 3:1.

 

2. Doubting God’s Word and His love.

Vv. 4,5

 

3. Looking at what God has forbidden.

“And when the woman saw…the tree,” v. 6

Compare with Joshua 7:21

 

Note the steps of sin:

“I saw”

“I coveted”

“I took”

“I hid”

 

4. Lusting for what God has prohibited.

v.6

 

Here we have the

“Lust of the flesh”

“Lust of the eyes”

and “Pride of life”

spoken of in 1 John 2:16

 

Compare Luke 4:1-15

 

II. The Results of the Fall

 

A. Upon man’s physical nature

 

1. The ground was cursed, so that henceforth it would not yield good alone.

 

Genesis 3:17

 

2. Sorrow and pain to the woman in childbearing, and subjection of the woman to the man.

 

Genesis 3:16

3. Exhausting physical labor in order to subsist.

Genesis 3:19

 

4. Physical and spiritual death.

 

Genesis 3:19; 3:2; 5:5; Romans 5:12

 

Of course, with all this came also a fear of God, a shame of sin, a hiding from God’s presence, and finally, an expulsion from the garden.

 

Genesis 3:8-11,22-24

 

B. Upon man’s spiritual nature

 

1. All men, without respect of condition or class, are sinners before God.

 

Romans 3:9,22,23; Psalm 14; Isaiah 53:6

 

There may be a difference in the degree but not in the fact of sin.

 

2. The whole world rests under condemnation, wrath and a curse.

 

Romans 3:19 “That every mouth may be stopped and all the world may become guilty before God.”

 

Galatians 3:10; Ephesians 2:3

 

The law of God demands perfect obedience, but no son of man can yield such obedience; hence, the curse of a broken law rests upon those breaking it.  John 3:36

 

3. Unregenerate men are regarded as children of the devil and not Sons of God.

 

1 John 3:8-10; John 8:44

 

“Ye are of your father the devil.”

 

1 John 3:19

 

“And we know that we are of God, and the whole world lieth in wickedness.” (RV: in the wicked one)

 

 

4. The whole race of men is in hopeless captivity to sin and Satan.

Romans 7:14; John 8:31-36; Ephesians 2:3

 

5. The entire nature of man, mentally, spiritually, morally, physically, is sadly affected by sin.

 

a. The understanding is darkened.

  Ephesians 4:18; 1 Corinthians 2:14

 

b. The heart is deceitful and wicked.

  Jeremiah 17:9,10

 

c. The mind and conscience are defiled.

  Genesis 6:5; Titus 1:15

 

d. The will is enfeebled.

  Romans 7:18

 

e. The flesh and spirit are defiled.

  2 Corinthians 7:1
