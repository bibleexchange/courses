HUMILITY

I Peter 5:1-11

TEXT: I Peter 5:6

 

I.   Humility is Defined as Submission in Action.

Letting go and letting God

Humility is…

  • a genuine submission to the Word & Will of God.
  • obedience with conviction.
  • True substance in opposition to pride, anxiety and carnal desires, thoughts, and emotions which all leave you with a hollow experience.

 

Humility shows…

  • strength of faith.
  • measure of courage.
  • level of conviction.

 

Humility is not style and appearance as well as commitment and obedience.

 

hu·mil·i·ty n. modesty or respectfulness: the quality of being modest or respectful

Humility is a call to arms in God’s redemptive purposes.

  • We must be receiving God’s grace.
  • We must be asserting our convictions.
  • We must appropriate every promise.

3

Imagine the amount of courage it took for Jesus to commit Himself to such a course of action as the cross.

  • He was born in a dirty, smelly, manger (a feeding trough for barnyard animals.)
  • He was treated lower than a slave throughout His life.
  • He died like a criminal on the cross.

Luke 2:7 Phil. 2:5-8

John 13:1-17 I Peter 2:20-25

 

This humble invasion has changed the course of human history forever.

Though unclear at the time of His suffering, we clearly see now the benefits of Christ’s humiliation.
