Completely Overcoming the

Serious Nature of Sin

 

Text: James 1:12-15, 22

 

INTRODUCTION

 

  1. Many are blinded to the promise of completely overcoming the nature of sin.

 

  1. Satan has blinded sinners and professing Believers from knowing the will of God.

II Cor. 4:4; Rom. 3:23; Heb. 4:1,2;

Acts 9:5,6

 

Many have the wording and profession, but not the understanding nor the experience. (James 1:22)

 

  1. The Natural Mind has been Blinded through Carnal Thinking. v.16

 

  1. Carnal Thinking Causes Indifference.

 

— Indifference is simply a lack of difference—

 

  1. Indifference is when someone cannot see the difference between two things.

 

 

  1. Indifference causes double-mindedness. James 1:6-8

 

  1. “scattered abroad” James 1:1

 

  1. Having “diverse temptations” and never learning the joy of Faith.

 

  1. “Diverse temptations” trying your faith getting your attention off from the Lord.

 

Stay a servant to the Lord Jesus Christ and never yield yourself to sin in any measure. (James 1:1)

 

You cannot serve sin and serve the Lord at the same time.

 

  1. The Natural Mind Cannot Submit to God through Carnal Thinking.

 

  1.  Carnal thinking ‘cannot please God’ Rom. 8:6-10; Heb. 11:6

 

  1. Carnal thinking is enmity against God.

Rom. 8:7

 

  1. Carnal thinking is trying to be religious without truly seeking God.

 

  1. Carnal thinking always ends in death.

Rom. 8:6; James 1:15

 

 

  1. The Natural Mind is Blind and Dead. v.15

Rom. 8:6, 27; II Cor. 4:4; Rom. 12:2

 

  1. Blind and dead to responsibility.

 

  1.  ‘we are debtors’ Rom. 8:12

 

  1. ‘then do we’ Rom. 8:25; Phil. 4:9

 

  1. Every Believer has the responsibility to receive all from the Lord with a Spiritual mind. James 1:5-7

 

  1. The Spiritual mind is a transformed mind.

The OLD is NOT:

  • Thought Upon
  • Used
  • There

  1. A Renewed Mind

Rom. 12:2; II Cor. 5:17

 

  1. A Quickened Mind

Rom. 8:11

 

  1. An Awakened Mind

Eph. 5:14; 2:8,10

 

  1. A Disciplined Mind

Rom. 12:2; James 1:26-27

 

  1. The Spiritual mind is the mind of Christ. Phil. 2:5-11; I Cor. 2:16; II. Cor. 5:17

 

 

 

 

CONCLUSION

 

  1. We must completely overcome the carnal mind and sin.

 

  1. We will never be Christ for that is a distortion of Truth.

Matt. 24:5; John 1:20

 

  1. BUT we are to have Christ in full measure in our body, heart, soul, mind, and spirit.

Col. 1:27; 3:16; Eph. 4:13; Rom. 8:1-23, 29-30
