# The Psalms of lament
#Zaburi za maombolezo

## Introduction
## Utangulizi

### A definition of a psalm of lament
### Maana ya zaburi za maombolezo

What is a "psalm of lament?" These psalms contain a cry for deliverance or defense and are addressed directly to God. Nini maana ya zaburi ya maombolezo?” zaburi hizi zina kilio cha ukombozi au ulinzi na zinashungulikiwa moja kwa moja na Mungu.They are more than just emotional complaints to God, but they has many complex and varied characteristicsNi zidi ya malalamiko ya kihisia kwa Mungu,bali zina sifa mbalimbali na tata. "A lament psalm relates an experience, so it conveys a narrative.”Zaburi ya ombolezo inafanana na uzoefu,hivyo inafikisha simulizi.” It presents this within a metrical structure, so it is poetry.Inawasilisha hii ndani ya muundo wa vipimo,hivyo ni mashairi. It asks for something from God, so it is prayer.Inauliza kitu kwa Mungu, It pleads a case, so it is argument. It expresses a faith, so it is theology."[1] Inaongoza kesi,hivyo ni majibizano.inaonyesha imani,hivyo ni theolojia.”[1]

### The structure of a psalm of lament
###Muundo wa zaburi ya maombolezo

Most of the lament psalms contain four parts: invocation, complaint, petition, and conclusion.Maombolezo mengi yana sehemu tatu:sala,malalamiko,dua na hitimisho

#### Invocation
#### Sala

The lament psalms begin with and initial request to God. Many of them begin with the words "have mercy on me, O God."Zaburi ya maombolezo huanza na ombi la mwanzo kwa Mungu.Nyingi huanza na neno nihurumie,Ee Mungu.”

#### Complaint
#### Malalamiko

This section gives the reason why the psalmist is praying. It talks about the problems that cause the complaint. Some reasons for lament are:Sehemu hii inatoa sababu kwanini mwimba zaburi anaomba.Inazungumzia kuhusu matatizo yanayosababisha malalamiko.Baadhi ya sababu za malalamiko ni:

* illness (6; 13; 38; 39; 69; 88)
* Magonjwa(6;13;38;39;69;88)
* slander (64:3; 57:4; 140:3, 9)
* Kashfa (64:3;57:4140:39)
* reproach by friends and family (55:12–14, 20; 31:11; 35:11; 69:8, 20; 88:8, 18)
* Aibu ya marafiki na familia(55:12-14,20;31:11;35:11;69:8,20;88:8,18)

Sometimes the psalmist also complains about God Himself.Wakati mwingine mwimba zaburi pia anamlalamkia Mungu mwenyewe. "They say not only ‘I am afflicted’ but often ‘You have afflicted me.Wanasema siyo tu `nimetaabishwa bali mara nyingi umenitaabisha.’"[2] Sometimes the psalmist is experiencing the judgment of God, and he is repenting because of it.`”[2]Wakati mwingine mwimba zaburi anapata hukumu za Mungu,na anatubu kwasababu ya hukumu hiyo. One example is Psalm 51, which is thought to be David's repentance after his sin with Bathsheba (2 Samuel 11–12). Other times, he feels he has been righteous and that God has been unjust to punish him.[3]Mfano mmoja ni zaburi ya 51, ambayo ilifikiriwa kuwa toba za Daudi baada ya dhambi yake na Bathsheba (2samuel 11-12).Wakati mwingine,alijiskia amekuwa mwenye haki na kwamba Mungu alikumuonea kwa kumwadhibu.[3]

In these psalms there is talk about the _enemies_.Katika zaburi hizi kuna mazungumzo kuhusu maadui-. It is possible that these were real armies that were attacking Israel, but they may also be symbolic of people who stand against the principles of Jehovah.[4]Inaweza kuwa haya yalikuwa majeshi halisi ambayo yaliwakabili wana wa Israel,lakini pia zinaweza kuwamifano ya watu waliosimama kinyume na kanuni za Yehova.[4]

#### Petition
#### Dua

This section contains request for help and motivation or reasons why God should help. "Sometimes the psalm may include a protestation of innocence, e.g. ‘I wash my hands in innocence’ (26:6) or a confession of sin, e.g. ‘For I know my transgressions, and my sin is ever before me’ (51:3), though often neither is present."[5] Many of these psalms contain questions addressed to God.Sehemu hii inaomba msaada na motisha au sababu za kwanini Mungu lazima asaidie.”Wakati mwingine zaburi inaweza kujumuisha mashtaka ya hatia,mfano .`nanawa mikono yangu katika hatia` (26:6) au ukiri wa dhambi,mfano.`Kwa kuwa najua makosa yangu,na dhambi zangu zitakuwa mbele yangu daima (51:3),Japokuwa mara nyingi wala ipo.”[5]Zaburi hizi nyingi zina maswali yaliyojibiwa na 	Mungu.

#### Conclusion
Hitimisho

The conclusion of the lament psalm is almost always praise to God.
Hitimisho la zaburi za maombolezo kwa kawaida huwa na sifa kwa Mungu.

There are 56 psalms of lament, which are divided into individual lament and national lament.Kuna zaburi 56 za maombolezo,ambazo zimegawanyika katika maombolezo binafsi na maombolezo ya kitaifa.

### Voicing frustration to God
### Kueleza kuchanganyikiwa kwetu kwa Mungu

Many of these psalms contain complaints and strong accusations against God.Zaburi hizi nyingi zina malalamiko na mashtaka baina ya Mungu. For example, Psalm 13:1 says, "How long wilt thou forget me, O Lord? for ever? how long wilt thou hide thy face from me?" When the Israelites were wandering in the wilderness, they complained, and God was angry with them because they complained (Numbers 14:22). How is the complaints in Psalms different from the complaints of the Israelites?Kwa mfano,zaburi ya 13:1 inasema, “Ee  Bwana hata lini utanisahau,hata milele?hata lini utanificha uso wako?Waisrael  walipokuwa jangwani,walilalamika,na Mungu aliwakasirikia kwasababu ya malalamiko yao(Hesabu 14:22).Je malalamiko katika zaburi yanatofautianaje na malalamiko ya wana wa Israel?

* The Israelites spoke about God (as it were) behind his back. They spoke as if they had given up on God.
* Waisrael waliongea kuhusu Mungu(kaka ilivyokuwa) nyuma yake.Walizungumza kama vile wamekata tamaa kwa Mungu.
* The complaints of the psalmists are spoken directly to God. The psalmists have not given up on God but are expressing the opposite of that.Malalamiko ya mwimba zaburi yamezungumzwa moja kwa moja kwa Mungu.Mwimba zaburi  hakukata tama kwa Mungu lakini anaelezea kinyume chake. 
* Whatever real emotions they had they present to God, but in the context of asking for help and hoping that he would answer them in their distress.Bila kujali hisia walizokuwa nazowalizikabidhi kwa Mungu,lakini katika mazingira ya kuomba msaada na kuwa na tumaini kwamba atawajibu dhiki zao.

Lament is very prominent in the prayers of Israel.Maombolezo ni maarufu sanakatika maombi ya waisrael. This shows us that prayer of of "anguish and puzzlement in the life of faith is not a sign of deficient faith, something to be outgrown or put behind one, but rather is intrinsic to the very nature of faith.Hii inatuonyesha sisi  kwamba maombi ya dhiki na mshangao katika maisha ya imani siyo alama ya upungufu wa imani,kitu cha kuiondoa ile tabia au kuweka nyuma ya mtu,lakini badala yake ni asili kwa asili ya imani."[6] Sometimes we sing songs of only joy or praise, but when we remove the songs of lament, we conclude that "the hard issues of justice are improper questions to pose at the throne, because the throne seems to be only a place of praise. “ [6]Wakati mwingine tunaimba nyimbo za furaha tu au sifa,lakini tukiondoa nyimbo za maombolezo ,Tunahitimisha kwamba “ mambo magumu ya haki  ni maswali yasiyofaa  kupeleka katika kiti cha enzi,kwasababu kiti cha enzi kinaonekana kuwa eneo la sifa tu."[7] The lament psalms teach us that "God matters in every dimension of life. “[7] zaburi za maombolezo zinatufundisha sisi kwamba  “ Mungu anahusika katika kila kipimo cha maisha." If we do not bring our problems before God in lament, then all we have is anxiety and despair because of our troubles.[8] “ Kama hatutaleta shida zetu kwa Mungu kwa njia ya maombolezo,basi kils tulichonacho ni wasiwasi na kukata tamaa kwasababu ya shida zetu.[8]

But what about us today?Lakini vipi kuhusu sisi leo? Should Christians express complaints against God?Je wakristo waeleze malalamiko yao dhidi ya Mungu? Protest towards God is understandable in the Old Testament, because in that dispensation two major principles were the blessing of those that kept the covenant and the cursing of those who did not keep the covenant. Maandamano kuelekea kwa Mungu yanaeleweka katikaagano la kale,kwasababu katika mpango  kanuni kuu zilikuwa Baraka za wale waliotunza agano na laana za wale ambao hawakutunza agano.If someone who was righteous in keeping the covenant suffered, then it seemed like injustice.Kama mtu mmoja aliyekuwa mwenye haki katika kutunza agano waliteseka,halafu inainesha kama siyo haki. But in the new dispensation, Jesus and the apostles make it clear that the righteous will suffer persecution. Lkini katika mpango mpya,Yesu na maaskofu wanaeleza k]vizuri kuwa mwenye haki atapata mateso.Christ suffered, and He said that we would also suffer.[9] We should still take our burdens to God, but we should not be surprised by suffering.Yesu aliteseka,na akasema kwamba ata sisi tutateseka.[9]Tutatakiwa kuendelea kupeleka mizigo yetu kwa Mungu,Lakini hatutatakiwa kushangaa tunapopitia mateso.

## Psalms of national lament
## Zaburi za maombolezo ya kitaifa

The psalms of national lament are 12, 44, 58, 60, 74, 77, 79, 80, 82, 83, 85, 90, 94, 106, 108, 123, 126, 137.Zaburi za maombolezo ya kitaifa ni 12, 44, 58, 60, 74, 77, 79, 80, 82, 83, 85, 90, 94, 106, 108, 123, 126, 137.


"In these psalms, Israel moves from articulation of hurt and anger, to submission of them to God, and finally to relinquishment."[10] Katika zaburi hizi,Waisrael walitoka katika tamko la kuumiza na hasira,kuwasilisha kwa Mungu, na mwisho kusimamisha. “[10]

In these psalms, there is a belief in the deliverance of God, but they mix "lament with praise, because they know beyond doubting that in God’s unchanging, unfailing love they will be saved in the end."[11]Katika zaburi hizi,kuna imani katika ukombozi wa Mungu,lakini zinachanganya “maombolezo ya sifa,kwasababu wanafahamu zaidi kwamba Mungu habadiliki,upendo usioshindwa wataokolewa mwisho.”[11]
