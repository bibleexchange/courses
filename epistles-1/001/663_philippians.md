<h1>Philippians</h1>
<h2>The Writer</h2>
<h3>Phil. 1:1 - Paul</h3>
<h3>"Paul and Timotheus, the servants of Jesus Christ."</h3>
<h2>The Purpose</h2>
<h3>To Thank</h3>
<p>This letter was written in part to thank the Philippians for their gifts. 4:10,18</p>
<h3>To Warn</h3>
<p>Paul also warns them concerning false teachers. 3:1-3 He describes these as:</p>
<h4>Dogs - false prophets Isa. 56:10-11</h4>
<h4>Evil Workers - Works without faith 3:3; Try to worship God in the flesh.</h4>
<h4>Concision - cutting down instead of believing God for deliverance.</h4>
<h3>To Advise</h3>
<p>Paul's advice is concerning the dangers of dissension or contentious quarreling.</p>
<h4>1:28 &quot;And in nothing terrified by your enemies.&quot;</h4>
<h4>2:3 &quot;Let nothing be done through strife.&quot;</h4>
<h4>2:5 &quot;Let this mind be in you which was also in Christ Jesus.&quot;</h4>
<h4>2:14 &quot;Do all things without murmurings and disputings.&quot;</h4>
<h4>3:2 &quot;Beware&quot; &quot;Beware&quot; &quot;Beware&quot;</h4>
<h4>3:3 &quot;Have no confidence in the flesh.&quot;</h4>
<h4>2:2; 3:16; 4:2 &quot;be of the same mind&quot;</h4>
<h4>3:18 &quot;enemies of the cross of Christ&quot;</h4>
<h4>4:4 &quot;rejoice in the Lord&quot;</h4>
<h4>4:5 &quot;let your moderation be known unto all men&quot;</h4>
<h4>4:6 &quot;be careful for nothing&quot;</h4>
<h4>4:8-9 &quot;think&quot; and &quot;do these things&quot;</h4>
<h3>To Encourage</h3>
<p>In this epistle Paul encourages them all to go on.</p>
<h4>&quot;All&quot; is mentioned 26 times.</h4>
<h4>Sin is not mentioned once.</h4>
<p>Salvation is progressive in that at the new birth you are free from the penalty of sin but as your faith grows you can be totally free from the power of sin. 1:6, 9, 11, 27-29; 2:12; 3:8-14, 21; 4:23</p>
<h4>The word joy or rejoice is found in every chapter. 1:4,18,25-26; 2:2,16,18; 3:1,3; 4:1,4,10</h4>
<h3>To Inform</h3>
<p>To tell them of his intended visit. 1:25, 26; 2:24</p>

<h3>1:6&quot;Being confident&quot;</h3>
<h3>3:21&quot;Fashioned like unto His&quot;</h3>

<h3>4:7&quot;Keep your hearts and minds through Christ&quot;</h3>
<h3>4:13&quot;I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me&quot;</h3>
<h2>Outline of Philippians</h2>
<h3>Introduction to all the saints. 1:1-2</h3>
<p>&quot;Grace be unto you and peace&quot;</p>
<h4>&quot;To all the saints in Christ Jesus&quot;</h4>
<h5>Holiness in character: religious, sacred, consecrated.</h5>
<h5>Morally blameless</h5>
<h5>Physically and spiritually pure</h5>
<h5>One set apart for Holy fellowship and service.</h5>
<h4>&quot;Which are at Philippi&quot;</h4>
<p>Acts 16:9-40 A chief city of Macedonia</p>
<h4>Bishops I Tim. 3:1-7</h4>
<h5>Overseer</h5>
<h5>Pastor</h5>
<h5>Under-Shepherd</h5>
<h5>Elder</h5>
<h5>Christ is the Shepherd and Bishop of our Souls. I Peter 2:25</h5>
<h4>Deacons I Tim. 3:8-13</h4>
<h5>Attendant</h5>
<h5>To run errands</h5>
<h5>Minister/voluntary servant</h5>
<h5>Church officer</h5>
<h4>Givers</h4>
<p>The Philippians had heard of Paul&apos;s imprisonment and had sent a love gift by Epaphroditus. 4:10,18</p>
<p>They also heard of Epaphroditus getting deathly sick and Paul wanted them to know that &quot;God had mercy on him&quot; 2:27</p>
<h3>Always when in prayer requesting fellowship. 1:3-5</h3>
<h3>Confidence in God&apos;s ultimate purpose. 1:6</h3>
<h4>The grounds of his confidence. &quot;He which hath began&quot;</h4>
<h4>The subject of His confidence.&quot;A good work in you&quot;</h4>
<h4>The scope of His confidence. &quot;Will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ&quot;</h4>
<h5>Perfection, full stature</h5>
<h5>Till Christ is fully formed in you</h5>
<h5>&quot;Perform&quot; fulfill, terminate, accomplish, finish and perfect</h5>
<h3>Paul&apos;s prayer for the Philippians 1:7-11</h3>
<h4>Perfection of love is produced by the increase of the knowledge of the Son of God, &quot;yet more and more in knowledge and in all judgment&quot; Phil. 3:10 &quot;That I may know Him&quot;</h4>
<h4>Perfection of love produces the ability to discern. 1:10 &quot;Approve things that are excellent&quot;</h4>
<h4>The perfection of love produces purity. &quot;Sincere&quot; &mdash; judged by sunlight. Tested as genuine, Pure, &quot;Without offence&quot;</h4>
<h4>The perfection of love produces a fruitful life. 1:11 &quot;Being filled With the fruits of righteousness&quot;The nature of man must be changed by grace before he can bear fruit. &quot;Which are by Christ Jesus Unto the Glory and Praise of God&quot; Fruit is the Indication of the general character.</h4>
<h4>Knowledge is increased by fellowship, love and truth. 1:7 &quot;Partakers of my grace&quot; Luke 19:15 &quot;gained by trading&quot; To partake of this grace is to communicate the same you receive to others.</h4>
<h4>&quot;In all judgment&quot; shows our perception advancing</h4>
<h4>&quot;Approving things that are excellent&quot; Knowledge produces love but love produces greater knowledge.</h4>
<h3>The Gospel progressing in spite of persecution and false motives. 1:12-18</h3>
<h3>Christ magnified by life or by death. 1:19-26</h3>
<h3>Unity and humility enforced. 1:27-30</h3>
<h3>Christ&apos;s lowliness and humility. 2:1-30</h3>
<h3>Warnings for all. 3:1-21</h3>
<h3>Rejoicing in the Peace of God. 4:1-7</h3>
<h3>Mediate with a pure mind and do these things &quot;and the God of Peace shall be with you&quot; 4:8-9</h3>
<h3>The peace of contentment in any state. 4:10-12</h3>
<h3>Trusting in the strength and riches of Christ. 4:13-20</h3>
<h3>Greetings from all to all. 4:21-23</h3>
