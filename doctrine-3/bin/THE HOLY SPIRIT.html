THE HOLY SPIRIT

 

The study of the Holy Spirit is referred to as pneumatology. Pneuma is the Greek word for spirit and ology is the study of a particular subject. The English words spirit and ghost are renderings of the Greek word pneuma. The King James translators used both of these words to translate the one Greek word pneuma which literally means wind or breath. The gifts of the Spirit have already been covered in Doctrine 2 so they will not be discussed again in this course.

 

I. THE PERSON OF THE HOLY SPIRIT

 

A. The Holy Spirit is referred to as third person of the Trinity.

 

1. The Holy Spirit is not third in power or position.

 

2. The Holy Spirit is equal with The Father and The Son.

 

B. The Holy Spirit possesses the attributes of personality.

 

1. The Holy Spirit is described as having a mind.

 

Romans 8:27 – And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to the will of God.

 

2. The Holy Spirit has a will.

 

I Corinthians 12:11 – But all these worketh that one and the selfsame Spirit, dividing to every man severally as he will.

 

This does not mean that the will of the Holy Spirit is not in perfect harmony with the wills of the Father and the Son.

 

3. The Holy Spirit can be grieved.

 

Ephesians 4:30 – And grieve not the Holy Spirit of God, whereby ye are sealed unto the day of redemption.

 

C. The Holy Spirit has personal activities ascribed to him.

 

1. The Holy Spirit reveals truth.

 

II Peter 1:21 – For the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man:  but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost.

 

2. The Holy Spirit is a teacher.

 

John 14:26 – But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you.

3. The Holy Spirit bears witness.

 

Romans 8:16 – The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the children of God:

 

4. The Holy Spirit intercedes for the saints of God.

 

Romans 8:26 – Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.

 

The King James translators rendered the Greek word autos as the neutral pronoun itself possibly to convey the thought of the gender neutrality of a spirit.  We must understand the Holy Spirit is a person and not an it.

 

5. The Holy Spirit speaks.

 

Revelation 2:7 – He that hath an ear, let him hear what the Spirit saith unto the churches; To him that overcometh will I give to eat of the tree of life, which is in the midst of the paradise of God.

 

6. The Holy Spirit commands.

 

Acts 6:16 – Now when they had gone throughout Phrygia and the region of Galatia, and were forbidden of the Holy Ghost to preach the word in Asia,

 

7. The Holy Spirit testifies.

 

John 15:26 – But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:

 

II. THE MINISTRY OF THE HOLY SPIRIT

 

A. The Holy Spirit will always exalt Jesus.

 

John 16:13-14 – Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come.  He shall glorify me: for he shall receive of mine, and shall shew it unto you.

 

1. He shall not speak of Himself.

 

The Holy Spirit does not exalt Himself. He will always exalt Jesus. The error of the false charismatic churches is their exaltation of the Spirit. The spirit that they operate in is not the true Holy Spirit.

 

 John 15:26 – But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:

 

2. He will not glorify man.

 

The Holy Spirit does not exalt man. The Holy Spirit will use a man to glorify Jesus.

 

B. The Holy Spirit will show you things to come.

 

John 16:13 – Howbeit when he, the Spirit of truth, is come, he will guide you into all truth: for he shall not speak of himself; but whatsoever he shall hear, that shall he speak: and he will shew you things to come.

 

1. The Holy Spirit speaks through prophecy.

 

2. The Holy Spirit will reveal future events.

 

3. The Holy Spirit will show what is to come through an understanding of the Word.

 

C. The Holy Spirit will give power to minister.

 

 Acts 1:8 – But ye shall receive power, after that the Holy Ghost is come upon you: and ye shall be witnesses unto me both in Jerusalem, and in all Judaea, and in Samaria, and unto the uttermost part of the earth.

 

1. He will anoint a man to preach the Gospel.

 

I Thessalonians 1:5 – For our gospel came not unto you in word only, but also in power, and in the Holy Ghost, and in much assurance; as ye know what manner of men we were among you for your sake.

 

I Corinthians 2:4 – And my speech and my preaching was not with enticing words of man's wisdom, but in demonstration of the Spirit and of power:

 

2. He will minister to the congregation during the preaching.

 

 D. The Holy Spirit will lead men to the truth.

 

1. He will help you to understand the Word.

 

2. He will draw men to the truth.

 

E. The Holy Spirit is the comforter.

 

John 14:26 – But the Comforter, which is the Holy Ghost, whom the Father will send in my name, he shall teach you all things, and bring all things to your remembrance, whatsoever I have said unto you.

 

F. The Holy Spirit will help you to pray.

 

1. The Holy Ghost will empower and help you to pray. There is praying in tongues in the Holy Ghost, but there is also praying in one’s own language in the power of the Holy Ghost.

Jude 20 – But ye, beloved, building up yourselves on your most holy faith, praying in the Holy Ghost,

 

2. Praying through the saints.

 

 Romans 8:26-27 – Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.

And he that searcheth the hearts knoweth what is the mind of the Spirit, because he maketh intercession for the saints according to the will of God.

 

G. The Holy Spirit will lead you into the will of God.

 

Rom. 8:14 – For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the sons of God.

 

 Matthew 4:1 – Then was Jesus led up of the Spirit into the wilderness to be tempted of the devil.

 

1. He will give you direction for ministry. – Acts 8:29, Acts 10:19-20

 

2. He will keep you from doing your own will. – Acts 16:6-7

 

H. The conviction of the Holy Spirit.

 

1. The Holy Spirit will convict men of their sin.

 

John 16: 7-8 – Nevertheless I tell you the truth; It is expedient for you that I go away: for if I go not away, the Comforter will not come unto you; but if I depart, I will send him unto you.

And when he is come, he will reprove the world of sin, and of righteousness, and of judgment:

 

a. The word for reprove here has the meaning of convincing and convicting.

 

b. The Word alone without the power of the Holy Ghost will not produce conviction of sin.

 

2. Man cannot produce conviction of sin.

 

III. THERE IS A WORK OF THE HOLY SPIRIT AT SALVATION

 

A. Born of the Spirit.

 

John 3:5-7 – Jesus answered, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born of water and of the Spirit, he cannot enter into the kingdom of God.

 

 That which is born of the flesh is flesh; and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit.

 

 Marvel not that I said unto thee, Ye must be born again.

 

1. Salvation is a work of the Spirit of God.

 

2. There is a difference between being born of the Spirit and being filled with the Spirit.

 

B. The Holy Spirit is at work in Sanctification.

 

Romans 15:16 – That I should be the minister of Jesus Christ to the Gentiles, ministering the gospel of God, that the offering up of the Gentiles might be acceptable, being sanctified by the Holy Ghost.

 

1. Sanctification is a part of Salvation.

 

2. There is not true salvation without a work of sanctification.

 

C. The power of the Holy Spirit helps the saint to stay saved.

 

1. The Holy Spirit gives the saint power over sin.

 

2. The Holy Spirit will keep us from the world.

 

1Peter1:5 – Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be revealed in the last time.
