Study Questions #1

The Nature of God: Study Questions

 

  1. Give Strong’s definition of God.

“God is infinite and perfect Spirit in whom all things have their source, support and end.” – Strong

 

  1. Give four Scriptural descriptions of God.
  1. God is spirit (John 4:24)
  2. God is light (I John 1:5)
  3. God is love (I John 4:16)
  4. God is a consuming fire (Deuteronomy 4:24; Hebrews 12:29)

 

  1. List three primary names of God.
  1. Elohim (God)
  2. Jehovah (Lord)
  3. Adonai (Lord)

 

  1. Give the creation name of God, and the meaning of the name.

The creation name of God is Elohim; Meaning the Trinity of God.

 

  1. What name of God does LORD signify, and what is the Hebrew root with its meaning of this name.

Lord signifies Jehovah; and the Hebrew root is havah meaning the I AM, the Self Existent one.

 

  1. List the seven covenant names of God with the meaning of each name.
  1. Jehovah-jireh – the Lord will provide (Genesis 22:15)
  2. Jehovah-rapha – the Lord that healeth (Exodus 15:26)
  3. Jehovah-nissi – the Lord our banner (Exodus 17:8-15)
  4. Jehovah-shalom – the Lord our peace (Judges 6:24)
  5. Jehovah-ra’ah – the Lord my shepherd (Psalm 23:1)
  6. Jehovah-tsidkenu – the Lord our righteousness (Jeremiah 23:6)
  7. Jehovah-shammah – the Lord is there (Ezekiel 48:35)

 

  1. What is the meaning of El Shaddai?

The meaning of El Shaddai is the Almighty God; The God who is sufficient.

 

  1. Define agnosticism.

Agnosticism is the belief that it is impossible to know whether or not God exists, that God’s existence is unprovable.

 

  1. Define polytheism.

Polytheism is the worship or belief of many gods.

 

  1. Define pantheism.

Pantheism is the belief that God is everything; that God and the material world are one and the same thing and that God is present in everything.

 

  1. Define deism.

Deism is the belief that there is a personal God who created the world and the set the universe in motion, then left it to be governed by natural law, never to interfere in how it runs.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  1. Give Scripture verbatim with reference to show the essence of God.

“God is a Spirit: and they that worship Him must worship Him in spirit and in truth.” – John 4:24

 

  1. What is the only way man is capable of expressing God?

The only way man is capable of expressing God is in human terms.

 

  1. If the earth is God’s footstool does this mean that God has feet?

No, God does not have feet; He cannot be limited to human form, to human dimensions.

 

  1. Describe God as being infinite.

God is infinite in that He is without boundaries, limitations, or measurements.

 

  1. What is meant by the non-moral attributes of God?

The necessary truths of the Divine essence that do not involve moral qualities (a choice of right or wrong).

 

  1. List four non-moral attributes of God with an explanation of each one.
  1. Omnipresence – God is everywhere present
  2. Omniscience – God has perfect and eternal knowledge of all things
  3. Omnipotence – God is all powerful
  4. Immutability – God is devoid of all change in essence, attributes, consciousness, and will

 

  1. What are all moral attributes phenomena of?

All moral attributes are phenomena of the will.

 

  1. What is meant by the Holiness of God?

“The holiness of God means His absolute moral purity; He can neither sin nor tolerate sin.”

– Knowing The Doctrines Of The Bible, Myer Pearlman

  1. List six moral attributes of God.
  1. God is Holy
  2. God is Righteous
  3. God is Faithful
  4. God is Merciful
  5. God is Love
  6. God is Good
