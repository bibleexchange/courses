The Definition of TheologySWAHILI

Three popular Views of the TermSWAHILI

Christian (Bible Theology):SWAHILI

The science of God (His existence, nature, attributes, laws, government, works, whatever may be known of Him), of the relations that exist between God and the universe, and of our relations, duties, responsibilities to Him and the universe. It is the science of things divine. This view, in its broadest sense, includes all knowledge/truth.SWAHILI

Scientific:SWAHILI

The critical, historical, philosophical and psychological study of religion and religious ideas. SWAHILI

Common:SWAHILI

The study of man's universal concepts of a Higher Power or Person. SWAHILI

Etymology of the TermSWAHILI

Two Greek words supply the basic meaning. a. Theos: God b. Logos: word, speech, reason. The etymology suggests a reasoned treatise or discourse about God.SWAHILI

Use of the TermSWAHILI

Broad, general, inclusive:SWAHILI

The sum of all Christian doctrines, or religious truth; rational inquiry into religious questions.SWAHILI

Narrow, particular, exclusive:SWAHILI

The doctrine of God, His Being and His works.SWAHILI

Synonymous treatment of doctrine and theologySWAHILI

Both are sciences that treat of religious beliefs, creeds and principles.SWAHILI

Both are divine revelations of truth. Doctrine is the specific revelation of truth. Dogma is the human statement of the truth incorporated in a creed.SWAHILI

The Ground of TheologySWAHILI

The unmistakable evidences of the existence of a God who is related to our universe.SWAHILI

The capacity of the human mind to know God and certain of those relations.SWAHILI

The provision of a revelation that brings God and the human being into contact and communion.SWAHILI

The absolute dependence of the human being on a Higher Power to live at peace with himself and his fellow creatures.SWAHILI

The ever recurrent questions concerning human origin, human destiny and the riddle of life demand intelligible answers.SWAHILI

The Sources of TheologySWAHILI

There are two authentic sources.SWAHILI

Nature (Natural Theology)SWAHILI

The universe and the world in which we live. (Psalm 19:1-4)SWAHILI
The intuitive response to nature's light. (Romans 1:8-20; 2:14,15)SWAHILI
Natural knowledge is elemental and limited(Acts 17:23)SWAHILI

The Holy Scriptures (Revealed Theology)SWAHILI

They are absolute, final and complete.SWAHILI
They give saving knowledge.SWAHILI

There are three undependable sources.SWAHILI

TraditionalismSWAHILI

This theory asserts the claim that the ecclesiastical interpretations of the Scriptures are equal to, and often superior to, the Holy Scriptures themselves. This is a Roman fallacy. SWAHILI

RationalismSWAHILI

This theory places reason above revelation. The rationalists reject all Scriptures that do not pass the test of human understanding and reason. The miracles of the Bible are subjected to these tests. The rationalists give three interpretations to the miracles: Natural, Mystical, and Mythical. SWAHILI

MysticismSWAHILI

This theory supports the practice of getting new light about God through personal revelation. Subjective experience is claimed to be equal to written revelation. Personal notions and prejudices may lead to error. SWAHILI

Theology is a science.SWAHILI

It treats of certified facts and data that are logically arranged and classified.SWAHILI

Religion is a life/lifestyle.SWAHILI

The Latin meaning is to bind or tie fast, to restrain. Religion is a life in communion with and controlled by God.SWAHILI

Morality is a law.SWAHILI

i.e., a code of behavior by which people govern themselves; a system of ideas of right and wrong conduct. SWAHILI

Worship is an art.SWAHILI

It is the outward expression of religion. SWAHILI

Salvation is an experience.SWAHILI

It involves a personal awareness of God and divine forgiveness. SWAHILI

A theologian may be only a scientist.SWAHILI

A Christian student of theology fits all the relationships.SWAHILI

The Value of TheologySWAHILI

It is the foundation of solid Christian experience.SWAHILI

Right believing is the first requisite of saving grace. SWAHILI

It builds a pattern for living the Christian way.SWAHILI

It builds a moral pattern.SWAHILI

It guards against a pattern of immorality.SWAHILI

Immoral - Inconsistent with moral rectitude; contrary to the moral or divine law; wicked; unjust; dishonest; vicious. - Webster's 1828 edition. SWAHILI

It protects the mind against error.SWAHILI

Truth garrisons the heart and mind. SWAHILI

It is a necessary qualification for the ministry.SWAHILI

To preach about God, one must know Him and know about Him. With open minds, humble hearts and absolute dependence upon the Holy Spirit, we begin our quest of God and divine things. SWAHILI
