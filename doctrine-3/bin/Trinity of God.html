THE TRINITY OF GOD

 

I. The doctrine defined

 

A. Trinity is from the Latin trinus and means threefold.

 

1. In reference to God it describes the three persons within the one God.

2. The phrase Triune God is used to describe the Trinity and refers to the Three persons within One God.

 

3. The Scriptural word Godhead is also used to refer to The Three Persons within One God.

Romans 1:20 - For the invisible things of him from the creation of the world are clearly seen, being understood by the things that are made, even his eternal power and Godhead; so that they are without excuse:

B. Within the One God are Three distinct Persons: The Father, The Son, and The Holy Ghost.

 

1. They are all eternal.

 

2. They are all equal in power.

 

3. They are one in unity.

 

C. The doctrine of the Trinity is revealed in Scripture.

 

I John 5:7 – For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one.”

 

II. Erroneous views of God.  (THESE ARE FALSE VIEWS!)

These false views will be refuted as we look into the truth of the Word of God.

A. Sabellianism

 

1. Bishop Sabellius taught that Father, Son, and Holy Ghost are simply three aspects or manifestations of God.

 

2. This false doctrine promotes there are three manifestations of one God.

 

a. Sometimes this is referred to as three personalities of one God.

 

b. Oneness teaching falls under this category.

 

Oneness sometimes called Jesus only is not an alternative to true Biblical doctrine. Oneness teaching is a dangerous false doctrine.

 

1John 2:22 – Who is a liar but he that denieth that Jesus is the Christ?  He is antichrist, that denieth the Father and the Son.

 

B. Arianism

 

1. Arius was the Presbyter of Alexandria about 318 AD.

 

2. Taught the Son is subordinate to the Father and that Jesus is a lesser God than the Father.

 

3. Christ is not eternal in existence and Jesus had a beginning.

               

(REMEMBER THIS IS A FALSE VIEW!)

 

C. Tritheism

 

1. The belief in three Gods.

 

2. Polytheism is an erroneous view of God.

 

III. Each distinct person of the Godhead is God in essence and attributes.

A. The Father is God.

 

Ephesians 4:6 – One God and Father of all, who is above all, and through all, and in you all.

 

1. Jesus referred to The Father as God. – John 20:17

2. The Father has life in Himself – John 5:2

3. Scripture refers to The Father as God.

John 20:17 – Jesus saith unto her, Touch me not; for I am not yet ascended to my Father: but go to my brethren, and say unto them, I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God.

 

 Phillipians 2:11 – And that every tongue should confess that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father.

 

I Peter 1:2 – Elect according to the foreknowledge of God the Father, ...

 

B. The Son is God

 

1. The Word that became flesh is God .

 

 John 1:1 – In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.  

 

2. Jesus is The I Am – John 8:58

 

3. Jesus is the Mighty God – Isaiah 9:6-7

 

4. Jesus is the Great God – Titus 2:13

 

5. In Jesus dwelt the fullness of The Godhead bodily – Colossians 2:9

 

6. Christ is eternal.

 

Revelation 1:8 – I am Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the ending, saith the Lord, which is, and which was, and which is to come, the Almighty.

 

Micah 5:2 – But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of Judah, yet out of thee shall he come forth unto me that is to be ruler in Israel; whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting.

This is a messianic prophecy of the Christ who is an eternal being.

 

7. Jesus is equal with God.

 

Philippians 2:5-6 – Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus:  Who, being in the form of God, thought it not robbery to be equal with God:

                              

Colossians 2:9 – For in him dwelleth all the fulness of the Godhead bodily.

 

C. The Holy Spirit is God

 

1. Scripture refers to The Holy Spirit as God – Acts 5:3-4

 

2. The Holy Spirit is ascribed the attributes of Deity – Acts 1:8, Romans 15:19, I Corinthians 2:11, I John 5:6

 

IV. Scripture reveals the plurality of the Godhead.

 

1. Elohim the Hebrew word used for God is a plural noun.  The Divine Author knows the difference between plural and singular nouns. The plural noun Elohim was used to declare that there are three persons within one God.

 

Genesis 1:1In the beginning God created the heaven and the earth.  

 

2. Plural pronouns are used in Scripture to refer to God.  Plural pronouns are not used to refer to one person. Again the Divine Author knows the correct usage of plural pronouns that are used to refer to more than one person.

 

Genesis 1:18 – And God said, Let us make man in our image, after our likeness…

 

3. The Bible declares there are three persons within One God.

 

I John 5:7 For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one.

 

4. Examples in Scripture of more than one Person of the Godhead present at the same time.

 

a. Stephen saw Jesus standing at the right hand of the Father.

Acts 7:55-56 – But he, being full of the Holy Ghost, looked up stedfastly into heaven, and saw the glory of God, and Jesus standing on the right hand of God,

And said, Behold, I see the heavens opened, and the Son of man standing on the right hand of God.

 

b. Jesus the Lion of Judah took the book out of the hand of the Father who sat upon the throne.

 

Revelation 4:2 – And immediately I was in the spirit: and, behold, a throne was set in heaven, and one sat on the throne.

 

Revelation 5:1 –  And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals.

 

Revelation 5:5-10  – And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book, and to loose the seven seals thereof.

And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.

And he came and took the book out of the right hand of him that sat upon the throne.

And when he had taken the book, the four beasts and four and twenty elders fell down before the Lamb, having every one of them harps, and golden vials full of odours, which are the prayers of saints.

And they sung a new song, saying, Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation;

And hast made us unto our God kings and priests: and we shall reign on the earth.

 

This one on the throne is described as Deity and receives worship.

Jesus is the one who redeemed us to God by His blood. This passage clearly shows Jesus taking the book out of the Father’s hand who is sitting upon the throne. This passage clearly shows Jesus and the Father present at the same time as two distinct persons.

 

c. The Son of man came to the Ancient of days.

 

Daniel 7:13 – I saw in the night visions, and, behold, one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven, and came to the Ancient of days, and they brought him near before him.

 

Jesus the Son of man, who is described in verse 14 as having an everlasting kingdom, came to the Ancient of days which refers to the Father in this passage. Again there are two persons of the Godhead present in the same Scripture.

 

V. There is one God

 

A. Scripture declares there is One God.

 

Deuteronomy 6:4 – Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD:

Singular                 Plural                       Singular

1. Jehovah our Elohim is one Jehovah.

2. These three are one. – I John 5:7

B. One God revealed in three Persons

 

1. There are not three Gods.

 

2. More than one God would be contrary to Scripture.

 

C. The Bible defines the oneness of God.

 

1. God is one in unity in the same way the body is one in unity.

 

John 17:22 – And the glory which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be one, even as we are one:

 

2. The body is one in unity, yet many people compose the body of Christ.

 

3. Husband and wife become one, yet there are still two persons.

 

Matthew 19:6 – Wherefore they are no more twain, but one flesh. What therefore God hath joined together, let not man put asunder.  

VI. The one God is composed of three distinct persons.

A. There are sharp distinctions revealed in Scripture between The Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

 

1. The Father sent The Son.

 

John 3:16 – For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.

 

John 16:28 – I came forth from the Father, and am come into the world: again, I leave the world, and go to the Father.  

 

2. The Son returns to The Father.

 

John 20:17 – Jesus saith unto her, Touch me not; for I am not yet ascended to my Father: but go to my brethren, and say unto them, I ascend unto my Father, and your Father; and to my God, and your God.

 

John 16:16 – A little while, and ye shall not see me: and again, a little while, and ye shall see me, because I go to the Father.

 

3. The Father and Son send The Spirit

 

John 15:26 – But when the Comforter is come, whom I will send unto you from the Father, even the Spirit of truth, which proceedeth from the Father, he shall testify of me:

 

John 16:7 – Nevertheless I tell you the truth; It is expedient for you that I go away: for if I go not away, the Comforter will not come unto you; but if I depart, I will send him unto you.

 

B. The Spirit intercedes with The Father.

 

Romans 8:29 – Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for we know not what we should pray for as we ought: but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with groanings which cannot be uttered.

 

C. The Son intercedes with the Father.

 

Hebrews 7:25 – Wherefore he is able also to save them to the uttermost that come unto God by him, seeing he ever liveth to make intercession for them.

 

D. Each Person of The Godhead is active in creation.

 

1. Father – Gen. 1:1

 

2. Son – John 1:1-2

 

3. Holy Spirit – Gen. 1:2

 

VII. The Trinity in Type

 

A. The Ark of the Covenant – Heb. 9:4

 

1. The tables of stone – The Father is the giver of the law.

 

2. The Golden pot of manna – The Son is The Divine Word that became flesh.

a. Gold a type of the Divine nature.

 

b. Manna a type of the Word.

 

3. Aaron’s rod that budded – The Holy Spirit produces life.

 

B. The bringing of Rebecca to Isaac.

 

1. Abraham a type of The Father

 

2. Isaac a type of The Son

 

3. Eliezer  a type of The Holy Spirit

 

VIII. Examples of all three revealed distinct from each other in one Scripture passage.

 

A. The baptism of Jesus – Mt. 3:16-17

 

1. Jesus is in the water.

 

2. The Father speaks from heaven.

 

3. The Holy Spirit descends as a dove.

 

B. The Great commission – Mt. 28: 19-20

 

1. Name of The Father

 

2. And of The Son

 

3. And of The Holy Spirit

 

4. And is a conjunction that grammatically joins two or more persons or things together.

 

C. Benediction to the Corinthian Church – II Cor. 13:14

 

D. Promise of The Comforter – John 14:26, John 15:26

 

E. God’s provision for man – I Peter 1:2

 

1. Elect according to the foreknowledge of God the Father.

 

2. Sanctification of The Holy Spirit.

 

3. Cleansed by the Blood of Jesus.

F. The Triune God revealed – I John 5:7

 

G. The means of the saints building themselves in the holy faith – Jude 20,21

 

1. Pray in The Holy Ghost.

 

2. Keep in the love of God (In the context of the passage this is referring to God the Father)

 

3. Look for eternal life through the mercy of Jesus Christ.

1. One man composed of body, soul, and spirit

 

2. Three’s in one throughout the body.
